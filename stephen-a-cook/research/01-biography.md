# Stephen A. Cook - Biography

## Early Life (1939-1957)

**Birth and Family**
- Born December 14, 1939, in Buffalo, New York
- Father: Gordon Cook, industrial chemist at Union Carbide
- Mother: Hilda Cook, homemaker
- Two younger brothers: Robert and Donald

**Childhood in Buffalo**
- Grew up in suburban Amherst, New York
- Attended public schools
- Early interest in mathematics and chess
- Built crystal radio sets as hobby

**Amherst Central High School**
- Excelled in mathematics and science
- Member of mathematics club
- State mathematics competition participant
- Graduated 1957

## Education

**University of Michigan (1957-1961)**
- Bachelor of Science in Mathematics (1961)
- Honor society membership
- Strong algebra and logic courses
- Interest in mathematical structures

**Harvard University Graduate Studies (1961-1966)**
- PhD in Mathematics (1966)
- Thesis advisor: Hao Wang (logic and automated theorem proving)
- Dissertation: "On the Minimum Computation Time of Functions"
- Focus: Computational complexity and recursion theory

**Doctoral Research Focus**
- Studied bounded arithmetic
- Investigated proof complexity
- Early computational complexity insights
- Connections between logic and computation

## Academic Career

**University of California, Berkeley (1966-1970)**
- Assistant Professor of Mathematics
- Taught logic and complexity courses
- Collaborated with complexity theory researchers
- Left due to denial of tenure

**University of Toronto (1970-present)**
- Joined as Associate Professor (1970)
- Promoted to Full Professor (1975)
- University Professor (1985)
- Retired as Professor Emeritus (present)

**Department Leadership**
- Computer Science department founder
- Chair (1980-1981)
- Graduate program development
- Recruitment of theoretical computer scientists

## The P vs NP Paper (1971)

**The Conference Presentation**
- STOC (Symposium on Theory of Computing) 1971
- Presentation: "The Complexity of Theorem-Proving Procedures"
- Audience: ~50 theoretical computer scientists
- Initial reaction: Interest, not immediate recognition

**Key Contributions**
- Formal definition of NP-completeness
- Proof that SAT is NP-complete
- Concept of polynomial-time reduction
- Framework for complexity classification

**Initial Reception**
- Published in conference proceedings
- Gradual recognition over 1970s
- Karp's 21 problems (1972) popularized the concept
- By 1980s: Central to theoretical computer science

## Personal Life

**Family**
- Married to Linda Cook
- Children: Two sons
- Maintained work-life balance
- Private family life

**Character and Habits**

**Intellectual Interests**

## Later Career (1990s-Present)

**Research Continuation**
- Proof complexity research
- Bounded arithmetic connections
- Propositional logic investigations
- Collaborations with younger researchers

**Mentorship**
- Supervised 30+ PhD students
- Influential in Canadian theory community
- International student recruitment
- Generous with time and ideas

**Philosophical Position**
- Believes P ≠ NP (widely shared view)
- Skeptical of quick resolution
- Values mathematical rigor
- Long-term perspective on problems

- Recognition of NP-completeness contribution
- Elevated complexity theory to prominence

**Other Honors**
- Fellow of the Royal Society of Canada (1979)
- Killam Research Fellow (1982)
- ACM Fellow (1994)
- Officer of the Order of Canada (2013)

**Named Lectures**
- Knuth Prize (shared with Leonid Levin, 2012)
- Multiple honorary doctorates
- Keynote addresses at major conferences
