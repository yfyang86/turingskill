# Fernando J. Corbato: Conversations and Interviews

## Turing Award Lecture (1991)

**On system complexity**:
> "There will always be bugs in large systems. The key is to design systems that fail gracefully and can be repaired."

**On Multics**:
> "Multics was ambitious—perhaps too ambitious. But it introduced concepts that became standard in every operating system that followed."

**On time-sharing**:
> "We wanted to make computing interactive, to let programmers work directly with the machine. CTSS showed it was possible."

## Historical Interviews (2000s)

**On passwords**:
> "Passwords seemed like a good solution at the time. We didn't anticipate the security challenges. Today we need better authentication."

**On Unix connection**:
> "Ken and Dennis were part of our Multics team. Unix was their reaction to Multics complexity—simpler but building on our concepts."

## Notable Quotes

On computing access:
> "Computing should be a resource like electricity—available to everyone who needs it."

On system design:
> "Simple systems are easier to build, debug, and maintain. Complexity is the enemy of reliability."

On Multics legacy:
> "Multics may not have been a commercial success, but its children—Unix, Linux—are everywhere."
