# Alfred V. Aho: Key Collaborators

## Jeffrey D. Ullman (1942-)

### Relationship
Co-recipient of the 2020 Turing Award. Aho and Ullman's collaboration began in 1963 at Princeton University and continued for over five decades.

### How They Met
> "Aho and Ullman met while waiting in the registration line on their first day of graduate school at Princeton University in 1963."

### Joint Contributions
- Nine textbooks (including various editions)
- Compiler algorithms and theory
- *The Design and Analysis of Computer Algorithms*
- *Principles of Compiler Design* (the "Dragon Book")
- *Foundations of Computer Science*

### Collaboration Pattern
Despite working at different institutions from 1969 onward (Ullman at Princeton/Stanford, Aho at Bell Labs/Columbia), they:
- Co-authored books and papers
- Met regularly to discuss research
- Maintained intellectual partnership across decades

## Brian Kernighan (1942-)

### AWK Language
Co-creator of the AWK programming language with Aho and Peter Weinberger.

### Bell Labs Connection
Both were part of the Unix research group at Bell Labs, contributing to the Unix philosophy of small, composable tools.

## Peter Weinberger

### AWK Language
The "W" in AWK, Weinberger collaborated with Aho and Kernighan on this influential text processing language.

## Margaret Corasick

### Aho-Corasick Algorithm
Co-inventor of the Aho-Corasick string matching algorithm, which efficiently finds multiple patterns in text simultaneously.

## John Hopcroft (1939-)

### *The Design and Analysis of Computer Algorithms*
Co-author with Aho and Ullman on this seminal algorithms textbook. Hopcroft won the Turing Award in 1986 (independently).

## Ravi Sethi

### Dragon Book
Co-author of the second edition of *Compilers: Principles, Techniques, and Tools* with Aho and Ullman.

## Monica Lam

### Dragon Book
Co-author of the third edition of the Dragon Book, updating it for modern compiler construction.

## Richard Hamming (1915-1998)

### Mentor at Bell Labs
Hamming, himself a Turing Award winner (1968), gave Aho crucial advice:
> "If you want to be a great scientist, you have to do more than great work; you have to teach others how to use your work."

This shaped Aho's approach of combining research with textbook writing.

## Bell Labs Colleagues

Aho was part of a legendary research environment that included:
- **Ken Thompson** and **Dennis Ritchie** (Unix, C)
- **Doug McIlroy** (pipes, software components)
- Numerous other Turing Award winners

## Columbia Students

As a professor at Columbia, Aho mentored many students who went on to successful careers in academia and industry.

## Collaboration Philosophy

Aho's collaborative style:
- Values long-term intellectual partnerships
- Prioritizes teaching and knowledge dissemination
- Bridges academia and industry
- Open to working across institutional boundaries

## Key Insight

Aho's most significant collaborator has been Jeffrey Ullman, with whom he shared the 2020 Turing Award. Their 50+ year partnership, producing nine textbooks and foundational research, exemplifies the power of sustained scientific collaboration.
