# Ken Thompson - Timeline

## Early Life and Education

**1943, February 4** - Kenneth Lane Thompson born in New Orleans, Louisiana

**1960-1963** - Undergraduate studies at University of California, Berkeley, Electrical Engineering and Computer Science

**1963-1965** - Master's degree studies at UC Berkeley under Butler Lampson; thesis work on Berkeley Timesharing System (BTS)

**1965** - Earns Master's degree from UC Berkeley

## Bell Labs Era: Unix Creation

**1966** - Joins Bell Telephone Laboratories, Murray Hill, New Jersey; assigned to Computing Techniques Research Department

**1968** - Publishes "Regular Expression Search Algorithm" in Communications of the ACM, introducing NFA-based regex matching

**1969** - Bell Labs withdraws from Multics project; Thompson begins work on Unix on a PDP-7 minicomputer

**1969** - Creates the B programming language, a simplified BCPL, to support Unix development

**1970** - Unix ported to PDP-11; system begins internal use at Bell Labs

**1971** - First Edition Unix released; Thompson creates the first Unix Programmer's Manual

**1972** - Implements the pipe mechanism in Unix, enabling the compositional philosophy

**1973** - Unix rewritten in C; Fourth Edition Unix released; Thompson and Ritchie present Unix at ACM Symposium on Operating Systems Principles

**1974** - "The UNIX Time-Sharing System" published in CACM with Dennis Ritchie, introducing Unix to academic community

**1974** - UC Berkeley receives Unix copy, leading to BSD development

## Bell Labs: Expansion and Chess

**1976** - Begins work on Belle chess machine hardware

**1978** - Seventh Edition Unix released, the ancestor of most commercial Unix systems

**1979** - Belle achieves master-level chess play; Thompson awarded US Master title

**1980** - Unix adoption accelerates in universities and research institutions

**1983** - Awarded ACM Turing Award with Dennis Ritchie "for their development of generic operating systems theory and specifically for the implementation of the UNIX operating system"

**1984** - Delivers Turing Award lecture "Reflections on Trusting Trust" at ACM conference; published in CACM

## Bell Labs: Plan 9 and UTF-8

**1989** - Begins leading development of Plan 9 from Bell Labs, a reimagined distributed operating system

**1990** - Plan 9 first release to universities

**1992** - Designs UTF-8 Unicode encoding with Rob Pike and Rob Weinberger over dinner; implemented in Plan 9

**1993** - Plan 9 Second Edition released

**1994** - Receives IEEE Computer Pioneer Award

**1995** - Plan 9 Third Edition released; "Plan 9 from Bell Labs" paper published

**1996** - Inferno operating system development begins, targeting embedded and network devices

## Late Bell Labs and Transition

**1998** - Receives National Medal of Technology from President Bill Clinton, shared with Dennis Ritchie

**2000** - Leaves Bell Labs following Lucent restructuring; joins Entrisphere, Inc.

**2000** - Plan 9 released as free software under open source license

## Google Era

**2006** - Joins Google as Distinguished Engineer

**2007** - Begins work with Rob Pike and Robert Griesemer on new programming language (later Go)

**2009** - Go programming language publicly announced (November 10)

**2009** - Go released as open source project

**2010** - Go 1.0 specification finalized

**2012** - Go 1.0 released (March); "Go at Google" paper published

**2011** - Receives Japan Prize with Dennis Ritchie for Unix development

**2012** - Dennis Ritchie dies (October); Thompson continues Go development

**2015** - Go 1.5 released with significant runtime improvements

**2018** - Go 1.11 released with modules support, addressing dependency management

**2021** - Retires from Google after 15 years

## Post-Retirement and Legacy

**2021** - Concludes active career; legacy includes Unix, C (indirectly), UTF-8, Go, and foundational security insights

**Ongoing Influence**:
- Unix and Linux run majority of servers, mobile devices, and embedded systems
- UTF-8 used by over 98% of web pages
- Go powers major cloud infrastructure (Docker, Kubernetes)
- "Reflections on Trusting Trust" remains required security reading
- Regular expression algorithms still used in modern implementations

## Decade-by-Decade Summary

**1960s**: Education at Berkeley, joining Bell Labs, early operating systems research

**1970s**: Creation of Unix, B language, pipes, regex implementation; chess programming begins

**1980s**: Unix standardization, Belle chess machine completion, Turing Award, "Trusting Trust" lecture

**1990s**: Plan 9 development, UTF-8 design, Inferno project, awards recognition

**2000s**: Transition from Bell Labs, joining Google, Go language creation

**2010s**: Go language maturation and adoption, continued systems research

**2020s**: Retirement, continued influence through established technologies

## Contemporaneous Context

**1969**: Unix creation same year as ARPANET's first message, moon landing

**1983**: Turing Award same year as Apple Lisa release, C++ first appearance

**1992**: UTF-8 design during early web standardization period

**2009**: Go release during cloud computing emergence, concurrent with Node.js and Redis

**2021**: Retirement as Go achieves major cloud infrastructure adoption

## Notable Durations

- **34 years** at Bell Labs (1966-2000)
- **15 years** at Google (2006-2021)
- **55 years** active career in computing (1966-2021)
- **52 years** since Unix creation (1969-2021)
- **29 years** since UTF-8 design (1992-2021)
- **12 years** since Go public release (2009-2021)
