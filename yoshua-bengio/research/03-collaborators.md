# Yoshua Bengio: Key Collaborators

## Geoffrey Hinton (University of Toronto, 1990s-Present)
Hinton and Bengio are often called the "godfathers of deep learning" along with Yann LeCun.

### Collaborative Relationship
- Co-authors on influential papers
- Co-recipients of the 2018 Turing Award
- Shared vision during the neural network winter
- Mutual citation and influence

### Joint Work
- Deep learning surveys and position papers
- Advocacy for neural networks when unfashionable
- Co-authorship on breakthrough deep learning papers
- Joint leadership of the field

## Yann LeCun (NYU/Facebook, 1990s-Present)
LeCun and Bengio have collaborated for decades.

### Long-term Collaboration
- Met at Bell Labs in early 1990s
- Co-recipients of 2018 Turing Award
- Shared commitment to neural networks
- Co-authors on survey papers

### Joint Contributions
- "Deep Learning" Nature paper (2015)
- Advocacy for the field
- Training generations of researchers
- Shaping public understanding

## Competitors and Critics
Healthy scientific debate has shaped Bengio's work:

### Symbolic AI Researchers
During the neural network winter, Bengio engaged with critics of the connectionist approach, ultimately prevailing as the data and results supported deep learning.

### Alternative Machine Learning Approaches
Engagement with researchers pursuing:
- Kernel methods
- Bayesian approaches
- Probabilistic graphical models
- Other machine learning paradigms

These interactions strengthened deep learning by forcing clarity about its strengths and limitations.
