# Judea Pearl: Key Publications

## Foundational Papers

### "Probabilistic Reasoning in Intelligence Systems" (1988)
**Author**: Judea Pearl
**Publisher**: Morgan Kaufmann
**Significance**: Definitive work on Bayesian networks. Established probabilistic methods in AI.

### "Causality: Models, Reasoning, and Inference" (2000, 2009)
**Author**: Judea Pearl
**Publisher**: Cambridge University Press
**Significance**: Created field of causal inference. Second edition expanded scope.

### "A New Method for Reasoning about Actions and Causes" (1990s)
**Author**: Judea Pearl
**Venue**: Various papers on do-calculus
**Significance**: Mathematical framework for causal reasoning.

## Bayesian Networks

### "Fusion, Propagation, and Structuring in Belief Networks" (1986)
**Author**: Judea Pearl
**Venue**: Artificial Intelligence
**Significance**: Belief propagation algorithms.

### "Belief Networks Revisited" (1993)
**Author**: Judea Pearl
**Venue**: AI Magazine
**Significance**: Comprehensive survey of Bayesian network methods.

## Causal Inference

### "Causal Diagrams for Empirical Research" (1995)
**Author**: Judea Pearl
**Venue**: Biometrika
**Significance**: Introduced causal diagrams to statistics.

### "Causal Inference in the Health Sciences" (2000s)
**Author**: Judea Pearl
**Venue**: Various medical and statistical journals
**Significance**: Applied causal inference to medicine.

## Counterfactuals

### "The Logic of Counterfactuals" (2000s)
**Author**: Judea Pearl
**Venue**: Multiple papers and book chapters
**Significance**: Formal semantics for counterfactual reasoning.

## Popular Science

### "The Book of Why: The New Science of Cause and Effect" (2018)
**Authors**: Judea Pearl, Dana Mackenzie
**Publisher**: Basic Books
**Significance**: Accessible introduction to causal inference for general readers.
