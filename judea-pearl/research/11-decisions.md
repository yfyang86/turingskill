# Judea Pearl: Key Decisions

## Career Decisions

### Move to UCLA (1970)
Chose UCLA for academic career. Result: Built world-class program; remained for 50+ years.

### Stay in Academia
Maintained academic career rather than industry. Result: Freedom to pursue foundational research.

## Research Direction Decisions

### Develop Bayesian Networks (1980s)
Created probabilistic framework for AI. Risk: Probability was unpopular in AI. Result: Transformed the field.

### Pursue Causal Inference (1990s)
Developed mathematical causation theory. Risk: Challenged statistical orthodoxy. Result: Created new field.

### Write "Causality" Book (2000)
Comprehensive treatise on causal inference. Result: Became definitive reference.

### Write for General Audience ("Book of Why")
Made causation accessible. Result: Broadened impact beyond academia.

## Personal Decisions

### Daniel Pearl Foundation
After son's murder in 2002, created foundation promoting tolerance. Result: Humanitarian legacy alongside scientific.

## Decision Style
- **Intellectual courage**: Tackling controversial topics
- **Persistence**: Decades developing causal theory
- **Interdisciplinary openness**: Reaching across fields
- **Personal resilience**: Transforming tragedy into positive action

Pearl's decisions show commitment to truth, understanding, and human values.
