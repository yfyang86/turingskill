# Judea Pearl: Selected Writings

## Major Books

### "Probabilistic Reasoning in Intelligent Systems" (1988)
**Content**: Comprehensive treatment of Bayesian networks, belief propagation, and probabilistic methods in AI.
**Impact**: Became the standard reference; created the field.

### "Causality: Models, Reasoning, and Inference" (2000, 2009)
**Content**: Mathematical theory of causal inference, do-calculus, counterfactuals, and applications.
**Impact**: Transformed statistics and social sciences; created new field.

### "The Book of Why" (2018)
**Content**: Accessible introduction to causal inference for general readers.
**Impact**: Broadened understanding of causation beyond academia.

## Turing Award Lecture

### "The Mechanization of Causal Inference"
**Venue**: ACM Turing Award Lecture, 2012
**Content**: Historical development of causal inference and future directions.

## Key Papers

### "Fusion, Propagation, and Structuring in Belief Networks" (1986)
Introduced belief propagation algorithms.

### "Causal Diagrams for Empirical Research" (1995)
Brought causal diagrams to statistics.

### "Causal Inference in Statistics: An Overview" (2009)
Comprehensive survey for statistical audience.

- Rich with examples
- Historical context provided
- Philosophical implications explored
