# Andrew Chi-Chih Yao - Ethics and Philosophy

## Views on Theoretical Research

### Fundamental Understanding
- Believes in pursuit of fundamental mathematical truths
- Values elegance and simplicity in theory
- Opposes incremental research without deep insight

### Long-term vs Short-term
- Advocates for long-term research vision
- Criticizes pressure for immediate results
- Believes foundational work requires patience

## Scientific Integrity

### Mathematical Rigor
- Insists on complete, correct proofs
- Values clarity over complexity
- Rejects sloppy or incomplete arguments

### Attribution and Credit
- Careful about proper attribution
- Values collaborative credit sharing
- Respects priority of discoveries

## Educational Ethics

### Democratization of Knowledge
- Founded IIIS to provide world-class education in China
- Believes talent is universal, opportunity is not
- Committed to training next generation

### Research Freedom
- Advocates for academic freedom
- Supports curiosity-driven research
- Opposes excessive bureaucratic constraints

## Technology and Society

### Responsible Innovation
- Aware of cryptography's dual-use nature
- Supports privacy-preserving technologies
- Concerned about surveillance applications

### National Identity and Science
- Made personal choice to return to China
- Believes science transcends national boundaries
- Advocates for international collaboration

## Research Ethics in Cryptography

### Security Claims
- Emphasizes rigorous security proofs
- Warns against ad hoc security assumptions
- Advocates for conservative parameter choices

### Open Research
- Supports publication of cryptographic research
- Believes security through obscurity fails
- Values peer review and community scrutiny

## Interdisciplinary Ethics

### Bridging Disciplines
- Promotes respectful interdisciplinary collaboration

### Education Reform
- Advocates for broad-based CS education
- Supports humanities and social science exposure
- Believes in educating whole person
