# Andrew Chi-Chih Yao - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Andrew Chi-Chih Yao (姚期智)
- **Born:** December 24, 1946, Shanghai, China
- **Nationality:** Chinese-American (returned to China in 2004)
- **Turing Award Year:** 2000
- **Award Citation:** For fundamental contributions to the theory of computation

## Education

- **Undergraduate:** National Taiwan University
  - B.S. in Physics, 1967
- **Graduate:** Harvard University
  - M.S. in Physics, 1969
  - Ph.D. in Physics, 1972
- **Postgraduate:** University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign
  - Ph.D. in Computer Science, 1975
  - Thesis: "Analysis of the Searching Algorithms"

## Career Timeline

- **1975-1976:** Research Fellow, MIT
- **1976-1981:** Assistant Professor, Stanford University
- **1981-1982:** Associate Professor, University of California, Berkeley
- **1982-1986:** Professor, Stanford University
- **1986-2004:** William and Edna Macaleer Professor, Princeton University
- **2004-present:** Professor, Tsinghua University, Beijing
- **2005-present:** Dean, Institute for Interdisciplinary Information Sciences (IIIS), Tsinghua

## Major Achievements

- **1977:** Published Yao's Principle (minimax principle)
- **1979:** Founded communication complexity theory
- **1981:** Contributed to oblivious transfer and secure computation
- **2000:** ACM Turing Award
- **2004:** Left Princeton to join Tsinghua University full-time
- **2005:** Founded IIIS at Tsinghua

## Research Focus Areas

- Computational complexity theory
- Communication complexity
- Cryptography and security
- Quantum computing theory
- Algorithm design and analysis

## Honors and Awards

- **1987:** Guggenheim Fellow
- **1991:** Member, National Academy of Sciences (USA)
- **1992:** Fellow, American Academy of Arts and Sciences
- **2000:** ACM Turing Award
- **2017:** Kyoto Prize in Advanced Technology

## Return to China

In 2004, Yao made the unprecedented decision to leave his tenured position at Princeton and return to China full-time:
- Joined Tsinghua University as full professor
- Founded the Institute for Interdisciplinary Information Sciences in 2005
- Pioneered interdisciplinary CS education in China
- Built world-class theoretical computer science research group

## Personal Characteristics

- Known for mathematical precision and elegance
- Quiet, thoughtful demeanor
- Deep commitment to education
- Bridge between Eastern and Western academic traditions
- Passionate about nurturing young researchers
