# Bob Metcalfe: Key Publications

## Foundational Paper

### "Ethernet: Distributed Packet Switching for Local Computer Networks" (1976)
**Authors**: Robert M. Metcalfe, David R. Boggs
**Publication**: Communications of the ACM, Vol. 19, No. 7
**Significance**: The original Ethernet paper
**Impact**: One of most cited papers in networking

Key contributions:
- CSMA/CD protocol description
- System architecture
- Performance analysis
- Design rationale

## Technical Reports and Memos

### Xerox PARC Technical Reports (1973-1975)
- Original Ethernet design documents
- Patent filings
- Internal PARC memos on networking

These documents, while not formally published, contain the earliest descriptions of Ethernet.

## Books

### *Internet Collapses and Other InfoWorld Punditries* (2000s)
Collection of Metcalfe's columns from InfoWorld magazine:
- Technology commentary
- Industry analysis
- Predictions (including the famous wrong one)

## Standards Documents

### DIX Ethernet Specification (1980)
**Authors**: DEC, Intel, Xerox consortium
**Significance**: First multi-vendor Ethernet standard
**Metcalfe's role**: Led Xerox contribution

### IEEE 802.3 Standard (1983)
**Organization**: IEEE
**Significance**: International standard
**Metcalfe's role**: Advocated for openness

## Articles and Columns

### InfoWorld Columns (1990s-2000s)
Metcalfe wrote hundreds of columns on:
- Networking technology
- Industry trends
- Startup companies
- Technology predictions

### Harvard Business Review
Articles on networking business and technology strategy.

## Patent

### "Multipoint Data Communication System with Collision Detection" (1977)
**Inventors**: Robert Metcalfe, David Boggs
**Patent Number**: US 4,063,220
**Significance**: Fundamental Ethernet patent

## Citation Impact

The 1976 CACM paper is one of the most cited in computer networking:
- Thousands of citations
- Referenced in virtually every networking textbook
- Foundation for follow-on research

## Publication Philosophy

Metcalfe's approach:
- **Implementation over papers**: Built working systems
- **Standards over publications**: Focused on adoption
- **Business over academia**: Commercialized inventions
- **Commentary over research**: Later career punditry

## Key Insight

Metcalfe's publication record is unusual for a Turing Award winner:
- Few academic papers
- Focus on standards and patents
- Business and commentary writing
- Impact through implementation

This reflects his role as an inventor-entrepreneur rather than a traditional academic researcher.
