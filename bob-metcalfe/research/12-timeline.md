# Bob Metcalfe: Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1946 | Born in Brooklyn, New York |
| 1969 | BS Electrical Engineering, MIT |
| 1970 | MS, Harvard University |
| 1972 | Joins Xerox PARC |
| 1973 | Invents Ethernet (with David Boggs) |
| 1973 | PhD Computer Science, Harvard |
| 1975 | Ethernet patent filed |
| 1976 | Ethernet paper published (CACM) |
| 1977 | Ethernet patent issued |
| 1979 | Founds 3Com Corporation |
| 1980 | DIX Ethernet standard (DEC-Intel-Xerox) |
| 1983 | IEEE 802.3 standard approved |
| 1984 | 3Com IPO |
| 1990 | Leaves 3Com |
| 1990s | InfoWorld columnist and pundit |
| 1995 | Predicts Internet collapse (wrong) |
| 1996 | IEEE Medal of Honor |
| 1997 | Eats his words (admits wrong prediction) |
| 2001 | Marconi Fellow |
| 2003 | National Medal of Technology |
| 2003 | Marconi Prize |
| 2010 | HP acquires 3Com for $2.7 billion |
| 2011 | Joins University of Texas faculty |
| 2022 | **Turing Award** |
| Present | Professor, investor, speaker |

## Career Periods

### Education (1965-1973)
- MIT undergraduate
- Harvard graduate school
- Thesis on packet switching
- PARC exposure to personal computing

### Xerox PARC (1972-1979)
- Ethernet invention
- Patent filing
- Paper publication
- Foundational networking research

### 3Com (1979-1990)
- Company founding
- Product development
- IPO and growth
- Industry leadership

### Pundit and Investor (1990-2011)
- InfoWorld columns
- Technology predictions
- Venture capital (Polaris)
- Industry commentary

### Academia (2011-present)
- University of Texas professor
- Teaching and research
- Continued speaking
- Mentoring students

- **1973**: Ethernet invented
- **1976**: Paper published
- **1983**: IEEE standard
- **1990s**: Ubiquitous deployment
- **2000s**: Dominant LAN technology
- **2010s**: Data center and cloud backbone
- **2022**: Turing Award recognition

Metcalfe's career spans the entire history of computer networking, from ARPANET to the modern Internet, with Ethernet as his enduring contribution.
