# Niklaus Wirth - Biography

## Early Life (1934-1954)

**Birth and Education**
- Born February 15, 1934, in Winterthur, Switzerland
- Grew up near Zurich in German-speaking region
- Father: Walter Wirth, high school geography teacher
- Mother: Hedwig Wirth

**Primary and Secondary Education**
- Attended local schools in Winterthur
- Strong aptitude for mathematics and physics
- Interest in electronics and model building
- Graduated Gymnasium (high school) 1954

**Early Technical Interests**
- Built electronic devices as hobby
- Amateur radio operator
- Mechanical and electrical engineering curiosity
- Foundation for computer engineering thinking

## University Education

**Swiss Federal Institute of Technology (ETH Zurich) (1954-1958)**
- B.S. in Electrical Engineering (1958)
- Focus on electronics and control systems
- First encounter with digital computers
- Z4 computer (Zuse) at ETH

**Université Laval, Quebec (1958-1960)**
- M.S. degree studies
- Exposure to North American computing
- Different academic culture
- Master's degree awarded

**University of California, Berkeley (1960-1963)**
- PhD in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (1963)
- Advisor: Harry Huskey (ALGOL pioneer)
- Dissertation: "A Generalization of ALGOL"
- Extended ALGOL with record structures

## Academic Career

**Stanford University (1963-1967)**
- Assistant Professor of Computer Science
- Participated in PL360 development
- Became involved in ALGOL 68 committee
- Left due to ALGOL 68 disappointment

**University of Zurich (1967-1968)**
- Brief return to Switzerland
- Assistant Professor position
- Continued language design work

**ETH Zurich (1968-1999)**
- Professor of Computer Science (1968)
- Built world-renowned CS department
- Developed Pascal, Modula, Oberon
- Retired as Professor Emeritus (1999)

## The Pascal Era (1968-1975)

**Language Design Philosophy**
- Reaction to ALGOL 68 complexity
- Simplicity and clarity priorities
- Structured programming support
- Teaching-oriented design

**Pascal Development (1968-1970)**
- Published Pascal User Manual and Report (1970)
- Collaborated with Kathleen Jensen
- Designed for teaching structured programming
- UCSD Pascal brought it to microcomputers

**Pascal Adoption**
- Apple Lisa and early Mac development
- Turbo Pascal revolution (Borland, 1983)
- Educational standard worldwide
- Transition to systems programming

## Modula and Systems Programming (1975-1985)

**Modula-2 Development**
- Systems programming needs
- Module concept introduction
- Separate compilation support
- Concurrent programming features

**Lilith Computer (1978-1982)**
- Custom workstation design
- Modula-2 as system language
- Bitmapped display pioneer
- Complete hardware-software system

**Medos Operating System**
- Written entirely in Modula-2
- Real-time capabilities
- Network support
- Demonstrated systems language viability

## Oberon Project (1985-1999)

**Oberon Language**
- Simplification beyond Modula-2
- Object-oriented extensions
- Component-oriented design
- Ultra-compact implementation

**Cerberus and Oberon Systems**
- Hardware-software co-design
- Unique user interface concepts
- Text as universal interface
- Minimalist philosophy

**Educational Focus**
- Teaching computer architecture
- System construction courses
- "Build from scratch" methodology
- Influenced generations of students

## Wirth's Law

**The Observation**
> "Software is getting slower more rapidly than hardware becomes faster"

**Context**
- Response to software bloat
- Criticism of inefficient practices
- Call for efficient design
- Still relevant today

**Reception**
- Widely quoted in computing
- Resonates with practitioners
- Counterpoint to Moore's Law
- Enduring observation
