# Alan J. Perlis - Timeline

## Early Life and Education

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1922 | Born April 1, Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania |
| 1943 | B.S. in Chemistry, Carnegie Institute of Technology |
| 1943-1946 | U.S. Army service (World War II) |
| 1949 | M.S. in Mathematics, MIT |
| 1950 | Ph.D. in Mathematics, MIT |

## Early Career

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1950-1952 | Research mathematician, Project Whirlwind (MIT) |
| 1952-1956 | Research mathematician, Tufts University |
| 1955 | Begins work on compiler construction |

## Carnegie Mellon Years

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1956 | Joins Carnegie Institute of Technology |
| 1956-1958 | Develops IT (Internal Translator) compiler |
| 1958 | Leads U.S. ALGOL 58 committee participation |
| 1958-1962 | Director, Carnegie Tech Computation Center |
| 1960 | ALGOL 60 Report published |
| 1962 | Becomes Professor of Mathematics |
| 1962-1964 | Chairman, Graduate Program in Computer Science |
| 1965 | Founds first U.S. CS department; becomes first department head |
| 1966 | **Receives First ACM Turing Award** |
| 1966 | Delivers Turing Award Lecture |
| 1968 | Leads ACM Curriculum 68 development |

## Yale University Years

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1971 | Joins Yale University |
| 1971-1984 | Chairman, Yale Computer Science Department |
| 1978 | "American Side of ALGOL Development" published |
| 1982 | "Epigrams on Programming" published (SIGPLAN Notices) |
| 1984 | Named Eugene Higgins Professor |
| 1984-1990 | Eugene Higgins Professor of Computer Science |

## Awards and Recognition

| Year | Award |
|------|-------|
| 1966 | ACM Turing Award (First recipient) |
| 1978 | Member, National Academy of Engineering |
| 1984 | IEEE Computer Pioneer Award |
| 1985 | ACM Distinguished Service Award |

## Later Years and Legacy

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
