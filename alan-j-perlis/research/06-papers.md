# Alan J. Perlis - Key Papers and Publications

## Foundational Papers

### Compiler Construction
- **"The IT Language"** (1958)
  - Internal Translator at Carnegie Tech
  - Early algebraic compiler design
  - ACM National Conference Proceedings

- **"Epigrams on Programming"** (1982)
  - SIGPLAN Notices, Vol. 17, No. 9
  - 130 aphorisms on programming
  - Most widely read CS publication

### ALGOL Contributions
- **"Preliminary Report: International Algebraic Language"** (1958)
  - With ALGOL committee, Communications of ACM
  - Foundation of ALGOL 58 specification

- **"Report on the Algorithmic Language ALGOL 60"** (1960)
  - With P. Naur et al., Communications of ACM
  - Edited by Peter Naur
  - Perlis led U.S. delegation

## Books and Monographs

### Textbooks
- **"Introduction to Computer Science"** (1972)
  - Co-authored with Keith Smith
  - Influential early CS textbook
  - Covered programming fundamentals

- **"Programming Languages: A Grand Tour"** (1983)
  - Edited comprehensive survey
  - Covered major language paradigms
  - Used in graduate courses

## Curriculum Development

### ACM Curriculum Reports
- **"Curriculum 68: Recommendations for Academic Programs in Computer Science"** (1968)
  - Co-authored with curriculum committee
  - Communications of ACM
  - Defined CS education standards

### Position Papers
- **"The Synthesis of Algorithmic Systems"** (1966)
  - Turing Award Lecture
  - Published in Journal of ACM
  - Vision for programming systems
