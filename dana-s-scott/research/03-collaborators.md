# Dana S. Scott: Key Collaborators

## Michael Rabin (1957)
The Turing Award collaboration:
- **Context**: IBM Research summer internship
- **Work**: Finite automata and decision problems
- **Outcome**: Introduction of nondeterminism
- **Duration**: Brief but extraordinarily impactful
- **Relationship**: Remained colleagues for decades

## Alonzo Church (1954-1958)
Scott's doctoral advisor at Princeton:
- **Mentorship**: Guidance in mathematical logic
- **Lambda calculus**: Church's work influenced Scott's domain theory
- **Legacy**: Scott became steward of Church's tradition
- **Connection**: Later years at Princeton (1969-1972)

## Christopher Strachey (1970s)
The programming language semantics collaboration:

### Denotational Semantics Partnership
- **Meeting**: Oxford, early 1970s
- **Complementarity**: Strachey (programming) + Scott (logic)
- **Work**: Mathematical semantics for programming languages
- **Tragedy**: Strachey died in 1975, cutting short the collaboration

### Impact
Their joint work created a field and influenced all subsequent programming language theory.

## Richard Montague (1960s-1970s)
Modal logic collaboration:
- **Scott-Montague semantics**: Possible worlds approach
- **Work at UCLA**: Both in California in 1960s
- **Influence**: Philosophical and computer science logic

## John Lemmon (1960s-1970s)
Modal and tense logic:
- Co-authored modal logic textbook (published posthumously)
- Tense logic work
- Applications to temporal reasoning in CS
