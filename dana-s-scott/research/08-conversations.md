# Dana S. Scott: Interviews and Dialogues

## On the Rabin Collaboration
"Michael and I were both at IBM that summer. We started discussing automata and realized nondeterminism simplified many problems. The equivalence proof came together naturally."

## On Domain Theory
"I was obsessed with finding models for the lambda calculus. Church had created this beautiful system, but it lacked mathematical meaning. When I discovered the right topological structures, everything clicked."

## On Strachey
"Christopher had deep programming insight but wanted rigor. I had the mathematical tools. Together we created something neither could do alone. His death was a terrible loss."

## On Church
"Working with Alonzo was extraordinary. He had created the lambda calculus in the 1930s. To help give it mathematical meaning decades later was an honor."

## On Interdisciplinary Work
"Logic is the foundation. Mathematics builds on it. Computer science applies it. Philosophy reflects on it. I see these as continuous, not separate."

## Teaching Philosophy
"Students need to see the beauty, not just the machinery. A proof should be elegant, not just correct. That's what makes mathematics worthwhile."

## On Retirement
"Berkeley is home. I continue working on modal logic - problems I first thought about decades ago. There's always more to understand."

His conversations reflect patience, depth, and appreciation for mathematical beauty across a long career.
