# Dana S. Scott: Key Decisions

## 1. Princeton PhD with Church (1954-1958)
Studied with the legendary logician; shaped his entire career.

## 2. IBM Summer with Rabin (1957)
Collaboration producing automata theory breakthrough.

## 3. Moving Through Institutions (1958-1972)
Chicago → Berkeley → Stanford → Amsterdam → Princeton
Each move expanded his mathematical foundation.

## 4. Oxford Professorship (1972)
First Professor of Mathematical Logic; met Strachey.

## 5. Domain Theory Development (1969-1972)
Solved lambda calculus semantics problem.

## 6. Strachey Collaboration (1970s)
Created denotational semantics.

## 7. Moving to CMU (1981)
Joined growing CS program; influenced generations.

## 8. Sustained Mentorship (1981-2003)
Supervised 51 PhD students.

## 9. Retirement to Berkeley (2003)
Returned to birthplace; continued research.

## Decision Pattern
Scott consistently:
- Followed mathematical depth
- Sought collaboration opportunities
- Built institutions
- Invested in students
- Maintained research across decades
