# Dana S. Scott: Key Publications

## Foundational Papers

### "Finite Automata and Their Decision Problems" (1959)
**With Michael Rabin**, IBM Journal of R&D
Turing Award-winning introduction of nondeterministic automata.

### "A Proof of the Independence of the Continuum Hypothesis" (1967)
**Mathematical Systems Theory**
Boolean-valued model approach to set theory.

### "Continuous Lattices" (1972)
**Lecture Notes in Mathematics**
Foundational domain theory paper.

### "Data Types as Lattices" (1976)
**SIAM Journal on Computing**
Comprehensive domain theory treatment.

## Books

### "Introduction to Modal Logic" (1977)
**With John Lemmon** (completed after Lemmon's death)
Standard reference for modal logic.

### "Outline of a Mathematical Theory of Computation" (1970)
**Technical Monograph**
Early denotational semantics manifesto.

## Key Contributions

### Domain Theory Papers (1969-1976)
- Models for lambda calculus
- Solution to self-application problem
- Foundations for denotational semantics

### Strachey Collaboration (1970s)
- Programming language semantics
- Oxford programming research group
- Mathematical foundations

### Modal Logic Works
- Scott-Montague semantics
- Tense logic
- Applications to CS

## Turing Award Lecture

### "Logic and Programming Languages" (1976)
Communications of the ACM
Articulated connection between logic and computation.

## Recent Work
Continued publications on modal logic and semantics into 2000s-2010s.
