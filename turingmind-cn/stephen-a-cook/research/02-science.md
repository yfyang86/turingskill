# Stephen A. Cook - Scientific Contributions

## NP-Completeness Theory (1971)

**The Foundational Paper**
- "The Complexity of Theorem-Proving Procedures"
- Presented at STOC 1971, published in proceedings
- Introduced concept of NP-completeness
- Defined polynomial-time reduction

**Key Definitions**
- **Class P**: Decision problems solvable in polynomial time
- **Class NP**: Decision problems with polynomial-time verifiable solutions
- **NP-complete**: Problems in NP to which all NP problems reduce
- **Polynomial reduction**: Efficient transformation between problems

**The SAT Result**
- Proved Boolean satisfiability is NP-complete
- Every NP problem reduces to SAT in polynomial time
- Demonstrated technique applicable to other problems
- Established first NP-complete problem

## Implications of NP-Completeness

**Problem Classification Framework**
- Thousands of problems classified as NP-complete
- Unified understanding of computational difficulty
- Guidance for algorithm design (heuristics for hard problems)
- Foundation for approximation algorithms

**The P vs NP Question**
- Central open problem in computer science
- One of seven Millennium Prize Problems ($1M reward)
- Implications for cryptography, optimization, AI
- Cook's conjecture: P ≠ NP

**Practical Impact**
- Algorithm designers know when to expect difficulty
- Cryptography assumes certain problems are hard
- SAT solvers: surprisingly effective in practice
- Approximation algorithms for NP-hard problems

## SAT and Applications

**Satisfiability Problem**
- Given Boolean formula, is there satisfying assignment?
- First problem proved NP-complete
- Cornerstone of complexity theory
- Surprisingly effective solvers exist

**Extensions**
