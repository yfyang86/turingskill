# Stephen A. Cook - Key Publications

## Foundational Papers

### The Complexity of Theorem-Proving Procedures (1971)
- **Conference**: STOC 1971 (3rd Annual ACM Symposium on Theory of Computing)
- **Pages**: 151-158
- **Publisher**: ACM
- **Impact**: Introduced NP-completeness, proved SAT is NP-complete
- **Citations**: 10,000+
- **Significance**: Most influential paper in computational complexity

### The Relative Efficiency of Propositional Proof Systems (1979)
- **Co-author**: Robert Reckhow
- **Journal**: Journal of Symbolic Logic, Vol. 44, No. 1
- **Pages**: 36-50
- **Content**: Formalized proof complexity, Cook-Reckhow thesis
- **Impact**: Founded proof complexity as research area

### Characterizations of Pushdown Machines in Terms of Time-Bounded Computers (1971)
- **Journal**: Journal of the ACM, Vol. 18, No. 1
- **Pages**: 4-18
- **Content**: Time complexity of context-free languages
- **Significance**: Early complexity classification result

## Complexity Theory

### On the Minimum Computation Time of Functions (1966)
- **Thesis**: PhD dissertation, Harvard University
- **Advisor**: Hao Wang
- **Content**: Time complexity of arithmetic operations
- **Result**: Cook-Andersen theorem on multiplication

### An Observation on Time-Storage Trade-off (1974)
- **Journal**: Journal of Computer and System Sciences, Vol. 9, No. 3
- **Pages**: 308-316
- **Content**: Space-time tradeoffs for computation
- **Impact**: Pebble games and complexity

### Deterministic CFL's Are Accepted Simultaneously in Polynomial Time and Log Squared Space (1979)
- **Co-author**: Ravi Sethi
- **Conference**: STOC 1976
- **Journal**: Information and Control, Vol. 45, No. 3
- **Result**: Efficient recognition of deterministic context-free languages
