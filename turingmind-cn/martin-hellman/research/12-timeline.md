# Martin Hellman: Timeline

## Early Life and Education (1945-1969)

|------|-------|--------------|

| 1967 | M.S. Electrical Engineering, Stanford | Graduate studies begin |
| 1969 | Ph.D. Electrical Engineering, Stanford | Doctoral work in information theory |

## Early Academic Career (1969-1971)
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1969 | Assistant Professor, MIT | First academic position |
| 1969-1971 | Information theory research | Continues doctoral work |

## The Public-Key Breakthrough Era (1971-1976)
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1971 | Assistant Professor, Stanford | Position enables Diffie collaboration |
| 1974 | Meets Whitfield Diffie | Partnership that changes cryptography |
| 1974-1976 | Collaboration with Diffie | Development of public-key concepts |
| 1976 | "New Directions in Cryptography" published | Foundational paper |
| 1976 | Diffie-Hellman protocol formalized | Practical key exchange method |

## Post-Breakthrough Cryptography (1976-1985)
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1977 | DES critique with Diffie | Questioning government standards |
| 1978 | Merkle-Hellman knapsack system | First public-key encryption implementation |
| 1980 | Time-memory trade-off paper | Cryptanalysis methodology |
| 1976-1985 | Continued cryptographic research | Building on breakthrough work |
| 1976-1996 | Teaches Applied Cryptography | Educates generations of security professionals |

## Shift to Peace Research (1985-2000)
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|

| 1980s | Founds Engineering Risk Research Group | New research direction at Stanford |
| 1980s-2000 | Nuclear risk analysis research | Applying engineering to existential risk |
| 1996 | Retires from full-time teaching | End of regular academic career |
