# Ronald L. Rivest - Key Decisions and Their Impact

## Career-Defining Decisions

### 1. Stanford PhD (1974)
**Context:** Yale math undergrad, choosing CS
**Decision:** Stanford Computer Science PhD with Robert Floyd
**Impact:** Algorithm foundation; cryptography interest
**Outcome:** World-class training

### 2. MIT Faculty (1974)
**Context:** Multiple offers after PhD
**Decision:** Join MIT
**Impact:** Career-long home; research environment
**Result:** 50+ years at MIT

### 3. RSA Development (1977)
**Context:** Working with Shamir and Adleman
**Decision:** Collaborate on public-key cryptosystem
**Impact:** Most important cryptography algorithm
**Result:** Fundamental Internet security

### 4. CLRS Textbook (1980s-1990s)
**Context:** Teaching algorithms at MIT
**Decision:** Collaborate on comprehensive textbook
**Impact:** Standard graduate text; millions of students
**Effort:** Multi-decade commitment

### 5. RSA Data Security (1980s)
**Context:** RSA algorithm commercial potential
**Decision:** Co-found company
**Impact:** Commercialized cryptography; industry foundation
**Trade-off:** Academic vs entrepreneurial time

### 6. Voting Research (2000s)
**Context:** Election integrity concerns
**Decision:** Apply cryptography to voting
**Impact:** ThreeBallot; election security field
**Motivation:** Democratic values

### 7. Privacy Advocacy (Ongoing)
**Context:** Crypto Wars, surveillance debates
**Decision:** Public policy engagement
**Impact:** Privacy protection; policy influence
**Principle:** Democratic values
