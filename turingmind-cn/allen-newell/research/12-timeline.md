# Allen Newell: Timeline

## 1927-1950: Education and Early Formation
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1927 | Born March 19, San Francisco | Academic family background |
| 1949 | BS Physics, Stanford University | Scientific foundation |
| 1949-1950 | Graduate studies in Mathematics, Princeton | Exposure to game theory; left before PhD |

## 1950-1961: The RAND Years
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1950 | Joined RAND Corporation | Access to advanced computing; systems problems |
| 1954 | Met Herbert Simon | Began legendary collaboration |
| 1954 | Attended Selfridge seminar on pattern recognition | Convinced intelligent systems possible |
| 1955-1956 | Developed Logic Theorist with Shaw and Simon | First AI program; birth of field |
| 1956 | Presented at Dartmouth Conference | AI named and launched as discipline |
| 1956-1957 | Enrolled at Carnegie Tech for PhD | Academic collaboration with Simon |
| 1957 | PhD Industrial Administration | Dissertation on chess/NSS system |

## 1961-1980: CMU and Major Systems
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1961 | Joined CMU faculty | Building AI research program |
| 1957-1972 | GPS development with Simon | General Problem Solver; cognitive simulation |
| 1972 | "Human Problem Solving" published | Magnum opus; established cognitive science |
| 1975 | ACM Turing Award with Simon | Recognition as AI founder |
| 1976 | Turing Award lecture: "Computer Science as Empirical Inquiry" | Physical Symbol Systems Hypothesis |
| 1971-1980 | C.mmp multiprocessor project | Hardware architecture contribution |
| Late 1970s | Began HCI work with Card and Moran | Expansion to human-computer interaction |
| 1980 | William James Lectures at Harvard | Precursor to "Unified Theories of Cognition" |
| 1982 | CMU campus network created | Infrastructure contribution |

## 1980-1992: SOAR and Final Years
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1978 | Laird joins as graduate student | Future SOAR collaborator arrives |
| 1980s | SOAR project begins | Unified theory of cognition effort |
| 1983 | "Psychology of Human-Computer Interaction" published | Established HCI as science |
| 1987 | William James Lectures published preparation | Based on Harvard lectures |
| 1990 | "Unified Theories of Cognition" published | Culminating theoretical statement |
| 1992 | National Medal of Science | Highest US scientific honor |
| 1992 | Died July 19, Pittsburgh | End of career; legacy assessment begins |
