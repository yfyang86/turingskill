# John L. Hennessy - Timeline

## 1950s-1970s: Early Life and Education

### 1952
- **September 22:** Born in Huntington, New York

### 1973
- **Bachelor's degree:** Villanova University
- **Major:** Electrical engineering

### 1975
- **Master's degree:** Stony Brook University
- **Focus:** Computer science

### 1977
- **Ph.D.:** Stony Brook University
- **Thesis:** Compiler optimization

## 1977-2000: Early Academic Career

### 1977
- **Joined Stanford University**
- **Position:** Assistant Professor of Electrical Engineering

### 1981
- **MIPS project begins**
- **Goal:** RISC processor research

### 1982
- **Promoted:** Associate Professor

### 1984
- **MIPS Computer Systems founded**
- **Co-founders:** Chris Rowen, others
- **Role:** Co-founder, Chief Scientist

### 1986
- **Promoted:** Full Professor
- **Tenure:** Permanent position

### 1988
- **MIPS IPO:** Successful public offering
- **Commercial success:** Validated

### 1990
- **"Computer Architecture" published**
- **Co-author:** David A. Patterson
- **Impact:** Became standard text

### 1992
- **MIPS acquired:** By SGI
- **Result:** Successful exit

### 1994
- **"Computer Organization and Design" published**
- **Co-author:** David A. Patterson

### 1996
- **Department Chair:** Stanford CS
- **Tenure:** Through 1999

### 1999
- **Dean of Engineering:** Stanford School of Engineering
- **Selected:** As president-elect

## 2000-2016: Stanford Presidency

### 2000
- **Became President:** Stanford University
- **Tenth president:** In Stanford history
- **Inauguration:** October

### 2001
- **September 11 response:** Campus leadership
- **International student concerns:** Visa issues

### 2004
- **Google IPO:** Board member
- **Strategic relationship:** Stanford-Google

### 2005
- **Financial aid initiative:** Need-blind commitment
- **Fundraising:** Major campaign

### 2008
- **Financial crisis management:** Budget challenges
- **Leadership:** Steady guidance through recession

### 2010
- **Biosciences initiative:** Major expansion
- **Knight Campus:** Planned

### 2011
- **Coursera founding:** Online education
- **Co-founders:** Andrew Ng, Daphne Koller

### 2012
- **Campaign success:** Exceeded fundraising goal

### 2016
- **Announced resignation:** June
- **Stepped down:** September 1
- **Successor:** Marc Tessier-Lavigne

## 2016-Present: Post-Presidency

### 2016
- **Knight-Hennessy Scholars:** Program launch
- **Fundraising:** Major gift secured
- **Goal:** Graduate fellowships

### 2017
- **Research return:** Active scholarship
- **Teaching:** Reconnect with students

### 2017
- **March 21:** **ACM A.M. Turing Award announced**
- **Shared with:** David A. Patterson
- **Citation:** "For systematic, quantitative approach"

### 2018
- **"Leading Matters" published:** Leadership book
- **Publisher:** Stanford University Press
- **Tour:** Book promotion

### 2018
- **Joined Google:** Technical advisor
- **Alphabet board:** Elected

### 2019-Present
- **Google partnership:** Continued
- **Knight-Hennessy:** Program development
- **Investments:** Personal venture activities
- **Advisory:** Multiple companies
