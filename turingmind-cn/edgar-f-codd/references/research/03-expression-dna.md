# Edgar F. Codd - Expression DNA and Communication Style

## Linguistic Characteristics

**Precise Technical Vocabulary**
- Mathematical precision in terminology
- Distinguishes "relation" from "table"
- Careful use of "tuple" vs "row"
- Insisted on "attribute" not "column"

**Formal Writing Style**
- Third-person academic voice
- Minimal personal anecdotes
- Logical progression of ideas
- Mathematical notation when helpful

**Understated British Manner**
- Avoided self-promotion
- Let technical merit speak
- Polite but firm in critiques
- Understated achievement descriptions

## Communication Patterns

**Structured Presentation**
- Introduction-Body-Conclusion format
- Numbered lists and hierarchies
- Tabular comparisons preferred
- Visual diagrams for complex concepts

**Defensive Precision**
- Anticipated misinterpretations
- Provided clarifying examples
- Distinguished theory from implementation
- Qualified statements carefully

**Evolution of Style**
- 1960s: Abstract mathematical
- 1970s: Accessible technical
- 1980s: Advocacy and critique
- 1990s: Standards enforcement

## Key Phrases and Formulations

**Signature Statements**
- "Data independence is the key"
- "A relation is a set of tuples"
- "Users should specify what, not how"
- "True relational systems must..."

**Critique Formulations**
- "Fails to meet the relational criterion"
- "Misleading marketing claims"
- "Departure from relational principles"
- "Theoretically unsound approach"

**Teaching Formulations**
- "Consider the following example..."
- "This can be understood as..."
- "The essential point is..."
- "Formally speaking..."

## Writing Evolution

**Academic Period (1968-1974)**
- Dense mathematical notation
- Formal theorem-proof structure
- Limited examples
- Peer-reviewed journal style

**Advocacy Period (1975-1985)**
- More accessible explanations
- Industrial relevance emphasized
- Competitive positioning
- Magazine article format

**Standards Period (1986-2003)**
- Prescriptive rule formulations
- Compliance evaluation criteria
- Vendor critique language
- Consulting report style

## Rhetorical Strategies

**Appeal to Logic**
- Mathematical foundations
- Logical consequences
- Proof by contradiction
- Deductive reasoning chains

**Appeal to History**
- Pre-relational system failures
- Evolution of data management
- Lessons from the past
- Inevitability of relational model

**Appeal to Authority**
- Mathematical logic tradition
- IBM research credibility
- Turing Award recognition
- Academic consensus building

## Personal Communication Style

**Meeting Behavior**
- Prepared detailed notes
- Listened before speaking
- Asked clarifying questions
- Summarized decisions precisely

**Email and Letters**
- Formal salutations
- Complete sentences
- Careful proofreading
- Professional closing

**Public Speaking**
- Read from prepared text
- Minimal improvisation
- Projected slides with care
- Answered questions methodically
