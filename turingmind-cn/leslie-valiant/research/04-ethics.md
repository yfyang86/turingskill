# Leslie Valiant: Ethics and Responsibility

## Theoretical Research Ethics
Valiant's work on PAC learning carries ethical dimensions:

### Machine Learning Ethics
- **Bias**: What can be learned reflects training data biases
- **Fairness**: Learned models may discriminate
- **Transparency**: Complex models lack interpretability
- **Accountability**: Who is responsible for learned decisions?

Valiant's theoretical framework provides tools to analyze these questions rigorously.

## Scientific Integrity
Valiant's research practices:
- **Rigorous proofs**: No claims without justification
- **Honest about limitations**: Clear about what theory doesn't address
- **Attribution**: Fair credit to predecessors
- **Openness**: Publishing results freely

## Dual-Use Considerations
PAC learning enables:
- **Positive**: Medical diagnosis, scientific discovery, efficiency
- **Concerning**: Surveillance, manipulation, autonomous weapons

Valiant's response: The theory is neutral; ethical constraints apply at application level.

## Education and Access
Valiant's contributions:
- Making ML theory accessible through clear frameworks
- Training diverse students
- Publishing openly
- Clear exposition in papers and books

## Evolvability and Ethics
Valiant's work on computational evolution touches:
- **Genetic engineering**: Understanding evolutionary computation
- **Synthetic biology**: Design principles
- **Climate adaptation**: Evolutionary responses

## Responsibility of Theorists
Valiant's career suggests theoretical researchers should:
- Create rigorous foundations
- Understand practical implications
- Educate about capabilities and limits
- Support beneficial applications

Valiant's work demonstrates theoretical excellence can serve ethical goals through enabling understanding and responsible application.
