# Leslie Valiant: Key Publications

## Foundational Papers

### "A Theory of the Learnable" (1984)
**Author**: L.G. Valiant
**Venue**: Communications of the ACM, 1984
**Significance**: Introduced PAC learning. Created computational learning theory. One of most influential papers in CS.

### "The Complexity of Computing the Permanent" (1979)
**Author**: L.G. Valiant
**Venue**: Theoretical Computer Science, 1979
**Significance**: Proved permanent is #P-complete. Created counting complexity.

### "Completeness Classes in Algebra" (1979)
**Author**: L.G. Valiant
**Venue**: STOC 1979
**Significance**: Defined VP and VNP. Established algebraic complexity framework.

## PAC Learning Extensions

### "Learning Disjunctions of Conjunctions"
**Authors**: M. Kearns, L.G. Valiant
**Venue**: IJCAI 1989
**Significance**: Cryptographic hardness of learning.

### "Toward a Computational Theory of Perceptual Learning"
**Author**: L.G. Valiant
**Venue**: Various, 1990s
**Significance**: PAC applied to cognitive learning.

## Parallel Computing

### "A Bridging Model for Parallel Computation"
**Author**: L.G. Valiant
**Venue**: Communications of the ACM, 1990
**Significance**: Introduced BSP model.

### "General Purpose Parallel Architectures"
**Author**: L.G. Valiant
**Venue**: Handbook of Theoretical Computer Science, 1990
**Significance**: Comprehensive treatment of parallel models.

## Later Work

### "Evolvability" (2009)
**Author**: L.G. Valiant
**Venue**: Journal of the ACM, 2009
**Significance**: Computational theory of evolution.

### "Probably Approximately Correct" (2013)
**Author**: L.G. Valiant
**Publication**: Book, Basic Books
**Significance**: Accessible exposition of PAC, evolvability, neuroidal models.
