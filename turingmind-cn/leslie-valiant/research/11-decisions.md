# Leslie Valiant: Key Decisions

## Educational Path

### Study Mathematics at Cambridge
Chose mathematics over applied fields. Result: Strong foundation for theoretical CS.

### Ph.D. in Computer Science
Pursued CS Ph.D. when field was young. Result: Became founding figure in theoretical CS.

## Career Decisions

### Move to Harvard (1982)
Joined Harvard as professor. Result: Built world-class theory group; remained for career.

### Stay in Academia
Declined industry opportunities. Result: Freedom to pursue foundational research.

## Research Direction Decisions

### Invent PAC Learning (1984)
Created new framework for machine learning. Risk: New field might not be accepted. Result: Created computational learning theory.

### Prove Permanent is #P-Complete (1979)
Tackled hard problem others avoided. Result: Created counting complexity field.

### Develop BSP Model (1990s)
Proposed new parallel model. Result: Influenced practical parallel computing.

### Move to Biology/Neuroscience (2000s)
Ventured into new fields. Risk: Leaving established expertise. Result: New insights on evolution and brain.

## Decision Style
- **Intellectual courage**: Tackling hard, important problems
- **Long-term thinking**: Pursuing deep foundations
- **Interdisciplinary openness**: Crossing field boundaries
- **Independence**: Following own research vision

Valiant's decisions show willingness to create new fields rather than work within existing ones.
