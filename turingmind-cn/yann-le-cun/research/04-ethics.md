# Yann LeCun - Ethics and Philosophy

## Open Research Advocacy

### Open Source Commitment
- Champions open publication of AI research
- Supports releasing model architectures and datasets
- Founded FAIR with open research mandate
- Critiques excessive secrecy in AI development

### Critique of Closed AI
- **Target:** OpenAI, DeepMind's commercial approaches
- **Argument:** Concentration of power in few companies is dangerous
- **Stance:** AI should be open and accessible
- **Warning:** Proprietary AI creates asymmetric power structures

## AI Safety Perspectives

### Optimistic Outlook
- Believes AI will benefit humanity overall
- Downplays existential risk scenarios
- **Quote:** "The fear of AI is overblown"
- Focus on near-term practical issues

### Risk Assessment
- Acknowledges risks of misuse
- Emphasizes regulation over research pause
- Supports AI for social good applications
- Advocates for robust safety testing

### Disagreement with Doomers
- **Public debates:** With Elon Musk, Stuart Russell
- **Argument:** AI existential risk is science fiction
- **Counter-argument:** Focus on real harms like bias, privacy
- **Position:** "Existential risk is premature concern"

## Bias and Fairness

### Acknowledgment of Bias Issues
- Recognizes training data bias problems
- Supports research into fair algorithms
- Advocates for diverse training datasets
- Emphasizes transparency in AI systems

### Technical Solutions
- Research into debiasing techniques
- Adversarial training for fairness
- Differential privacy methods
- Open discussion of limitations

## Autonomous Weapons Stance

### Opposition to Lethal Autonomous Weapons
- Signed open letters against autonomous weapons
- Supports international regulation
- **Position:** Human control must be maintained
- **Action:** Advocates for AI weapons bans

## Privacy and Surveillance

### Facial Recognition Concerns
- Acknowledges surveillance risks
- Supports regulation of facial recognition
- **Nuanced view:** Technology itself isn't the problem
- **Concern:** Government and corporate misuse

### Data Rights
- Supports user control over personal data
- Advocates for data portability
- Emphasizes consent in data collection
- Critical of exploitative data practices

## Labor and Economic Impact

### Job Displacement Views
- Acknowledges automation will displace jobs
- Optimistic about new job creation
- **Advocacy:** Universal basic income consideration
- **Policy:** Support for education and retraining

## Scientific Integrity

### Reproducibility Standards
- Insists on reproducible research
- Publishes code and data
- Criticizes unreproducible results
- Supports peer review rigor

### Credit Attribution
- Generous with co-author credit
- Acknowledges predecessors
- Supports young researchers
- Transparent about contributions

## Public Communication Ethics

### Accessibility of Communication
- Explains AI concepts for general public
- Active on social media (Mastodon, Twitter/X)
- **Approach:** Demystify AI, reduce fear
- **Risk:** Sometimes controversial statements

### Correction of Misinformation
- Corrects AI hype and misconceptions
- Debunks "AI sentience" claims
- Clarifies technical capabilities
- Opposes AI anthropomorphization

## Corporate AI Ethics at Meta

### Content Moderation
- Oversaw AI-driven content moderation systems
- Balanced free expression vs. harm reduction
- **Criticism:** Inadequate moderation on Meta platforms
- **Defense:** Technical challenges of scale

### Responsible AI Team
- Supported creation of Meta's Responsible AI team
- Research into fairness and transparency
- **Tension:** Business interests vs. safety

## Future of AI Ethics

### World Model Vision
- Proposes objective-driven AI architecture
- **Goal:** AI that learns like humans
- **Safety:** World models enable better planning
- **Hope:** More controllable AI systems
