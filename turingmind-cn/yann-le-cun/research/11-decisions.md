# Yann LeCun - Key Decisions

## Early Career Decisions

### 1983: Ph.D. Topic Choice
- **Context:** Neural networks were unfashionable
- **Alternative:** More accepted AI approaches
- **Decision:** Pursue neural network research despite skepticism
- **Outcome:** Foundation of lifelong work

### 1988: Joining AT&T Bell Labs
- **Offer:** Prestigious research position
- **Context:** Postdoc with Geoffrey Hinton
- **Decision:** Accept Bell Labs offer
- **Environment:** World-class research, resources
- **Result:** LeNet development, DjVu creation

### Staying During AI Winter (1990s)
- **Context:** Neural networks fell out of favor
- **Alternative:** Switch to more popular methods
- **Decision:** Continue neural network research
- **Cost:** Less recognition, funding challenges
- **Outcome:** Vindicated by deep learning revolution

## Bell Labs/Lucent Era

### LeNet Deployment Decision (1990s)
- **Challenge:** Convince AT&T to deploy neural networks
- **Application:** Check recognition for NCR ATMs
- **Risk:** Technology unproven at scale
- **Decision:** Push for production deployment
- **Result:** Processed 10-20% of US checks
- **Significance:** First commercial CNN deployment

### DjVu Development (1996-2001)
- **Context:** Image compression for documents
- **Decision:** Develop alternative to PDF
- **Technical:** Wavelet-based compression
- **Outcome:** Millions of documents archived
- **Legacy:** Demonstrated practical applications

## Career Transition Decisions

### 2003: Moving to NYU
- **Context:** AT&T Labs restructuring, Lucent struggles
- **Alternative:** Other industry labs
- **Decision:** Return to academia
- **Role:** Founding director, Center for Data Science
- **Impact:** Established data science as discipline

### 2013: Joining Facebook (Meta)
- **Offer:** Create and lead AI research lab
- **Alternative:** Stay at NYU, other industry offers
- **Decision:** Accept Facebook offer
- **Conditions:** Open research mandate
- **Outcome:** Founded FAIR, transformed industry practice

## Research Direction Decisions

### Focus on ConvNets (1989-2012)
- **Alternative:** Other neural architectures
- **Decision:** Double down on convolutional networks
- **Criticism:** "Neural networks don't work"
- **Persistence:** Continued despite skepticism
- **Vindication:** ImageNet 2012 breakthrough

### Self-Supervised Learning Pivot (2016-)
- **Observation:** Limits of supervised learning
- **Decision:** Advocate for self-supervised approach
- **Research:** Energy-based models, contrastive learning
- **Current:** JEPA architecture development
- **Risk:** May be early again

### JEPA Architecture (2022-)
- **Context:** LLM dominance in AI
- **Decision:** Propose alternative to autoregressive models
- **Approach:** Joint embedding predictive architecture
- **Reception:** Mixed - some interest, some skepticism
- **Status:** Ongoing research direction

## Open Research Advocacy

### FAIR Open Research Model (2013)
- **Decision:** Mandate open publication at FAIR
- **Contrast:** Other corporate labs (more secrecy)
- **Rationale:** Research advances faster when shared
- **Impact:** Industry-wide shift toward openness

### Opposition to GPT-4 Secrecy (2023)
- **Context:** OpenAI's closed approach with GPT-4
- **Stance:** Public criticism of lack of transparency
- **Argument:** Concentration of power is dangerous
- **Result:** Debate on AI development norms
