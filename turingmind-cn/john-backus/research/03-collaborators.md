# John Backus: Key Collaborators

## The FORTRAN Team (1954-1957)
Backus assembled a remarkable team at IBM:

### Key Members
- **Irv Ziller**: Language design
- **Roy Nutt**: System integration
- **Harlan Herrick**: Documentation
- **Robert Nelson**: Optimization
- **Lois Haibt**: First woman on team; compiler development
- **David Sayre**: Mathematical expertise

### Team Dynamics
- Young, enthusiastic programmers
- Working against skepticism about high-level languages
- Collaborative problem-solving
- Extended hours and dedication
- Celebrated success together

### The Result
Their collaboration produced the first successful high-level language and optimizing compiler.

## Peter Naur (1960)
Collaboration on BNF:
- **Context**: ALGOL 60 report
- **Contribution**: Naur refined and applied Backus's notation
- **Result**: Backus-Naur Form
- **Nature**: Brief but historically significant collaboration

## IBM Colleagues
Throughout his IBM career:
- **IBM 704 designers**: Hardware-software co-design
- **Fellow IBM researchers**: On functional programming
- **FL project team**: Post-FP language development

## ALGOL Committee Members
International collaboration:
- **European computer scientists**: Language design discussions
- **American colleagues**: Standards coordination
- **Academic advisors**: Theoretical input

## Influences
People who influenced Backus:
- **Howard Aiken**: Early computing pioneer; context for FORTRAN
- **John von Neumann**: Architecture; later critiqued by Backus
- **Kenneth Iverson**: APL influenced FP development

## Students and Successors
Though not an academic in traditional sense:
- **IBM researchers**: Colleagues influenced by his ideas
- **Functional programming community**: Inspired by FP
- **Compiler writers**: Learned from FORTRAN techniques

## Collaboration Style
Backus was known for:
- **Team leadership**: Assembling and motivating groups
- **Intellectual openness**: Sharing ideas freely
- **Persistence**: Sustained effort on long projects
- **Self-deprecation**: Downplaying his role

## The Backus Pattern
His major works involved:
1. Identifying fundamental problem
2. Assembling talented team (FORTRAN)
3. Or collaborating with peer (Naur)
4. Sustained effort to completion
5. Willingness to challenge orthodoxy (later work)

## Later Career Relationships
As IBM Fellow:
- **More independent**: Solo work on functional programming
- **Selective collaboration**: Choosing specific colleagues for FL project
- **Mentoring**: Younger IBM researchers

## Legacy Through Indirect Influence
Many influenced by his work without direct collaboration:
- **Functional language designers**: Haskell, ML creators
- **Compiler researchers**: Building on his optimization work
- **Language standard committees**: Using BNF

Backus's collaborative legacy is the infrastructure of modern programming - created through teamwork, refined through partnership, and extended through community.
