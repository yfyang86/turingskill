# Fernando J. Corbato: External Perspectives

## Peer Recognition

Ken Thompson (Unix creator):
> "Multics was complex, but it introduced concepts we took to Unix. Corbato's team showed what was possible."

Jerome Saltzer (Multics colleague):
> "Corby led Multics with vision and persistence. The project succeeded because of his leadership."

## Historical Assessments

**Operating systems textbooks**:
Corbato's CTSS and Multics are always covered as foundations of interactive computing.

**Computer History Museum**:
Recognizes Corbato as pioneer of time-sharing and interactive computing.

## Recognition
- 1990: ACM Turing Award
- National Academy of Engineering
- IEEE, ACM Fellow

## Legacy Assessment
Corbato is regarded as:
- Father of time-sharing
- Leader of Multics project
- Pioneer of interactive computing
- Password authentication inventor

His influence is seen in every interactive computing session worldwide.
