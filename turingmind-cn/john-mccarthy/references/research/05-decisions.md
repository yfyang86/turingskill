# John McCarthy - Key Decisions

## Career Decisions

### Creating LISP (1958)
- Designed language for AI research
- Revolutionary features (garbage collection, etc.)
- Made freely available
- Changed programming forever

### Dartmouth Conference (1956)
- Co-organized founding AI conference
- Coined "Artificial Intelligence"
- Defined research agenda
- Established field

### Stanford over MIT (1962)
- Left MIT AI Lab he co-founded
- Founded Stanford AI Laboratory (1965)
- Built SAIL as major center
- West Coast AI establishment

### Staying in Academia
- Resisted industry offers
- Focused on research and teaching
- Built academic institutions
- Influenced through students

## Technical Decisions

### LISP Design Choices
- S-expressions for code/data unity
- Garbage collection essential
- Dynamic typing
- Functional paradigm

### AI Formalisms
- Situation calculus for actions
- Circumscription for defaults
- Logical AI approach
- Formal reasoning methods

### Time-Sharing Advocacy
- Early proponent (1959)
- Interactive computing vision
- Resource sharing concepts
- Cloud computing precursor
