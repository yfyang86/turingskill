# Richard M. Karp - Biography

## Early Life (1935-1956)

**Birth and Family**
- Born January 3, 1935, in Boston, Massachusetts
- Jewish family background
- Father: Abraham Karp, teacher and businessman
- Mother: Rose Karp
- Grew up in Boston area

**Education**
- Excellent public schools in Boston
- Strong mathematics aptitude from early age
- Harvard University admission (1952)
- A.B. in Mathematics, summa cum laude (1955)
- M.A. in Mathematics (1956)

**Harvard Years**
- Studied under Andrew Gleason
- Interest in logic and algebra
- Won Harvard scholarship prizes
- National Science Foundation fellowship

## Graduate Studies and Early Career

**Harvard PhD (1955-1959)**
- PhD in Applied Mathematics (1959)
- Dissertation: "On the Application of Network Flow Theory to the Transportation Problem"
- Advisor: Anthony Oettinger
- Operations research focus

**IBM Watson Research Center (1959-1968)**
- Joined as research mathematician
- Operations research group
- Combinatorial optimization
- Algorithm design focus

**Harvard Return (1968-1994)**
- Gordon McKay Professor of Computer Science
- Electrical Engineering and Computer Science
- Built theoretical CS group
- Taught algorithms and complexity

## The 21 NP-Complete Problems (1972)

**The Paper**
- "Reducibility Among Combinatorial Problems"
- Presented at Symposium on Complexity of Computer Computations (1972)
- Organized by Raymond Miller and James Thatcher

**The Impact**
- Demonstrated power of Cook's framework
- Showed 21 diverse problems NP-complete
- Included: SAT, 3-SAT, CLIQUE, Hamiltonian Cycle, TSP
- Created field of NP-completeness studies

**Collaboration with Cook**
- Built on Cook's 1971 definition
- Popularized NP-completeness concept
- Complementary contributions
- Mutual recognition and respect
