# Marvin Minsky - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Marvin Lee Minsky
- **Born:** August 9, 1927, New York City, New York, USA
- **Died:** January 24, 2016, Boston, Massachusetts, USA
- **Nationality:** American
- **Turing Award Year:** 1969

## Education

- **Bronx High School of Science:** Early interest in science
- **Harvard University:**
  - B.A. in Mathematics, 1950
  - Interest in genetics, physics, music
- **Princeton University:**
  - Ph.D. in Mathematics, 1954
  - Thesis: "Neural Nets and the Brain Model Problem"
  - Built first neural network learning machine (SNARC)

## Career Timeline

- **1951:** Built SNARC (Stochastic Neural Analog Reinforcement Calculator)
- **1954-1957:** Harvard Society of Fellows (Junior Fellow)
- **1957:** Co-founded MIT Artificial Intelligence Project with John McCarthy
- **1958-2016:** MIT Faculty member
  - 1958: Assistant Professor
  - 1962: Associate Professor
  - 1965: Professor of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science
  - 1985: Toshiba Professor of Media Arts and Sciences
  - 1990: Donner Professor of Science
- **1959:** Co-founded MIT AI Laboratory (with John McCarthy)
- **1970s-1990s:** Directed MIT AI Lab

## Major Achievements

- Co-founded field of Artificial Intelligence (1956 Dartmouth Conference)
- Built first neural network learning machine (SNARC, 1951)
- Invented confocal microscopy (1957)
- Developed Society of Mind theory
- Pioneered robotics and computer vision
- Mentored generation of AI researchers
- Co-designed Logo programming language

## Personal Characteristics

- Brilliant, wide-ranging intellect
- Played piano and composed music
- Interest in philosophy and cognitive science
- Challenged conventional thinking
- Polyglot intellect spanning many fields
- Controversial views on consciousness and AI

## Interests Beyond AI

- Music composition and piano
- Mathematics and geometry
- Philosophy of mind
- Education reform
- Virtual reality early concepts
