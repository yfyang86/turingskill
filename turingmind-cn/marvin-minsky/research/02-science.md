# Marvin Minsky - Scientific Contributions

## Artificial Intelligence Foundations

### Dartmouth Conference (1956)
- Co-organized with John McCarthy, Claude Shannon, Nathaniel Rochester
- Coined term "Artificial Intelligence"
- Defined field's initial research agenda
- Brought together future AI leaders

### Neural Networks (1950s)
- Built SNARC (1951): first learning machine
- Stochastic neural network using vacuum tubes
- Hebbian learning principles
- Foundation for connectionism

### Perceptron Critique (1969)
- "Perceptrons" book with Seymour Papert
- Mathematical analysis of neural network limitations
- Showed single-layer limitations
- Influenced AI direction for decades
- Later acknowledged deep learning potential

## Robotics and Computer Vision

### MIT Robot Projects
- 1960s-1970s robot arm projects
- Block-stacking robot (copy demo)
- Computer vision for manipulation
- Hand-eye coordination research

### Vision Research
- Early computer vision algorithms
- Scene analysis techniques
- Edge detection methods
- 3D reconstruction from 2D

## Knowledge Representation

### Frame Theory (1975)
- "Framework for Representing Knowledge"
- Structured knowledge representation
- Slots and fillers paradigm
- Influenced object-oriented programming
- Semantic networks and frames

### Semantic Networks
- Early knowledge representation schemes
- Graph-based representation of concepts
- Inference mechanisms
- Natural language processing applications

## Society of Mind Theory

### The Society of Mind (1986)
- Intelligence from non-intelligent agents
- Mind as society of simple processes
- Emergence of consciousness
- Distributed cognition model
- Influenced cognitive science broadly

### The Emotion Machine (2006)
- Emotional intelligence models
- Critics and reflection in thinking
- Higher-level cognitive architectures
- Resource allocation in cognition

## Educational Technology

### Logo Programming Language
- Co-designed with Seymour Papert
- Turtle graphics for children
- Constructionist learning
- Educational robotics
- Worldwide impact on CS education

### One Laptop Per Child
- Advisor to Nicholas Negroponte
- Educational technology advocacy
- Computing access for all children

## Inventions and Innovations

### Confocal Microscopy (1957)
- Patented while at Harvard
- 3D imaging of biological specimens
- Nobel Prize awarded to later developers (2014)
- Minsky's patent predated widespread use

### Other Inventions
- Early mechanical hands
- First head-mounted graphical display
- Early virtual reality concepts
- Musical instruments and devices
