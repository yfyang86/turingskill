# Jack Dongarra: Interviews and Dialogues

## On Linear Algebra

> "I'm a mathematician. To me, everything is linear algebra. This is the material we use to build everything else."

Dongarra's mathematical view of computing:
- LU factorization solves systems
- Eigenvalues reveal stability
- SVD enables compression and analysis
- These appear in virtually every computation

## On Software Development

> "The software is the ultimate publication. Papers describe it, but the code is what people use."

Emphasizing that working software matters more than publication count.

## On Performance Benchmarking

> "What matters is not the peak performance, but what you can actually achieve on real problems."

On the importance of practical benchmarks like LINPACK over theoretical peak speeds.

## On TOP500

> "The TOP500 is a snapshot of the state of high-performance computing. It shows us where we are and where we're going."

The list as both competition and progress tracker.

## On Hardware Evolution

> "Every time hardware changes, we have to rethink the algorithms. What worked on vector machines doesn't work on GPUs."

On the continuous adaptation required in HPC software.

## On Open Source

> "Our software is used everywhere because it's open. Anyone can use it, improve it, optimize it for their hardware."

On why open source matters for scientific software.

## On Collaboration

> "The libraries we build are the result of hundreds of people over decades. I'm fortunate to be part of that community."

Emphasizing collaborative nature of scientific software.

## On GPU Computing

> "GPUs changed everything. Suddenly we had massive parallelism for the masses. Our job was to make them accessible through familiar interfaces."

On the GPU revolution and the role of libraries like MAGMA.

## On Exascale

> "Exascale computing isn't just about speed. It's about solving problems we couldn't solve before—climate, medicine, energy."

On the motivation for ever-faster supercomputers.

## On Receiving the Turing Award

> "I'm honored to receive this recognition, but it's really a recognition of the field of high-performance computing and the teams I've worked with over the years."

Deflecting personal credit to community and collaborators.

## On Autotuning

> "The idea behind ATLAS was simple: let the computer figure out the best way to run on this specific machine. The results surprised us—it often beat vendor libraries."

On the power of automated optimization.

## On Mixed Precision

> "We showed that you could use lower precision for parts of the computation and still get accurate results. Now it's essential for machine learning."

On pioneering techniques now widespread in AI.

## On Machine Learning

> "Deep learning is really linear algebra at scale. The same libraries we've built for scientific computing are now powering the AI revolution."

Connecting traditional HPC to modern AI.

## Advice to Students

> "Learn the fundamentals deeply. Linear algebra, algorithms, computer architecture. The specific technologies change, but the fundamentals endure."

On education for HPC careers.

## On the Future

> "We're approaching exascale, but the challenges are shifting. Power consumption, data movement, reliability at scale—these are the new frontiers."

On HPC challenges beyond raw speed.

## On International Competition

> "HPC has always been competitive, but it's also collaborative. We all benefit when the field advances, wherever it happens."

Balancing national competitiveness with scientific cooperation.
