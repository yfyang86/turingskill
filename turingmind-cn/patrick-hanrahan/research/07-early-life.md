# Patrick Hanrahan - Early Life and Education

## Childhood in Wisconsin
- Born 1955 in Wisconsin
- Grew up in Midwestern American environment
- Early interest in science and mathematics
- Exposure to computing during high school years

## University of Wisconsin-Madison

### Nuclear Engineering (BS, 1977)
- Undergraduate focus on nuclear engineering
- Strong mathematics and physics foundation
- First exposure to scientific computing
- Realized interest in computer graphics during this period

### Transition to Computer Science
- Recognized graphics as intersection of art and science
- Changed focus toward computing and visualization
- Pursued graduate studies in computer science

### Master's in Computer Science (1981)
- Joined the Computer Science department
- Worked under guidance of graphics researchers
- Developed interest in rendering algorithms
- Thesis work on early graphics problems

### PhD in Biophysics (1985)
- **Dissertation**: Work on 3D reconstruction from microscope images
- **Advisors**: Biophysics and computer science faculty
- **Focus**: Visualization of scientific data
- **Significance**: Combined graphics with scientific applications

## Doctoral Research
- Reconstruction of 3D structures from 2D projections
- Computational methods for electron microscopy
- Early work in volume rendering
- Interdisciplinary approach that defined career

## NYIT Computer Graphics Lab
- Visited and joined during PhD studies
- Influenced by cutting-edge graphics research
- Worked with pioneers in the field
- Transitioned to full-time after completing PhD

## Key Influences During Education

### Wisconsin Faculty
- Strong scientific computing tradition

- Emphasis on practical applications

### NYIT Connection
- Exposure to industry-leading graphics work

- Mathematical foundations for graphics

## Educational Philosophy
Hanrahan's own education shaped his approach to:
- Combining theory with practical applications
- Crossing disciplinary boundaries
- Publishing while building real systems
- Mentoring the next generation
