# Maurice V. Wilkes - Timeline

## Early Life and Education

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1913 | Born June 26, Dudley, Worcestershire, England |
| 1934 | B.A. in Mathematics, St. John's College, Cambridge |
| 1936 | Ph.D. in Physics (radio wave propagation), Cambridge |
| 1936-1937 | Research at Cambridge Mathematical Laboratory |

## Early Career and War Years

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1937 | Appointed University Demonstrator, Mathematical Laboratory |
| 1939-1945 | WWII Service: radar and operational research |
| 1943 | Worked on radar at Telecommunications Research Establishment |
| 1945 | Returned to Cambridge Mathematical Laboratory |

## EDSAC Development

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1946 | Attended Moore School Lectures (summer) |
| 1946 | Began EDSAC design with Bill Renwick |
| 1947 | EDSAC construction begins |
| 1949 | **EDSAC operational (May 6)** - first practical stored-program computer |
| 1949 | First programming textbook with Wheeler and Gill |
| 1951 | Invented microprogramming (published 1955) |

## Cambridge Leadership

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1945-1980 | Director, Cambridge Mathematical/Computer Laboratory |
| 1951 | "Automatic Digital Computers" published |
| 1955 | Microprogramming paper published |
| 1958 | EDSAC 2 becomes operational |
| 1962 | With Needham, published Atlas virtual memory paper |
| 1965 | Titan (Atlas 2) project begins |
| 1967 | **Receives ACM Turing Award** |

## Awards and Recognition

| Year | Award |
|------|-------|
| 1967 | ACM Turing Award |
| 1968 | Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS) |
| 1974 | AFIPS Pioneer Award |
| 1980 | Faraday Medal (IEEE) |
| 1980 | Eckert-Mauchly Award (IEEE/ACM) |
| 1985 | "Memoirs of a Computer Pioneer" published |
| 1997 | Fellow of Computer History Museum |
| 2000 | Knighted (Sir Maurice Wilkes) |
| 2001 | Fellow of Royal Academy of Engineering |

## Later Career

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1980-1986 | Professor, Cambridge; Adjunct Professor, MIT |
| 1980 | Stepped down as Laboratory Director |
| 1986-1990 | Professor Emeritus, Cambridge |
| 1990 | Retired from regular academic duties |

## Death and Legacy

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2010 | Died November 29, Cambridge, England (age 97) |
| 2013 | EDSAC Replica Project launched |
| 2016 | EDSAC Replica nearing completion (National Museum of Computing) |
