# Maurice V. Wilkes - Legacy and Impact

## Institutional Legacy

### Cambridge Computer Laboratory
- Founded and led for 35 years (1945-1980)
- Established premier computer science institution
- Created model for academic computing research
- Trained generations of computer scientists
- International reputation for excellence

### Cambridge Tradition
- Practical engineering approach
- Balance of theory and implementation
- Strong industry connections
- Collaborative research culture
- Continues to influence British computing

## Technical Legacy

### Stored-Program Computer
- EDSAC proved stored-program concept practical
- Model for all subsequent computers
- Universal adoption of stored-program architecture
- Foundation of modern computing

### Microprogramming
- Universal in processor design
- Enables complex instruction sets
- Foundation of CISC processors
- Still used in modern CPUs (microcode updates)
- Simplified processor development

### Subroutine Libraries
- Concept universal in programming
- Foundation for software reuse
- Influenced object-oriented programming
- Standard in all programming environments

## Scientific Impact

### Computer Architecture
- Established architecture as discipline
- Influenced IBM System/360 design
- Shaped commercial mainframe development
- Concepts in modern RISC and CISC designs

### Programming Methodology
- First programming textbook (Wilkes, Wheeler, Gill)
- Subroutine conventions still used
- Assembly language concepts
- Documentation standards

## Educational Impact

### Textbooks and Writing
- "The Preparation of Programs for an Electronic Digital Computer" (1951)
  - First programming textbook ever
  - Translated into multiple languages
- "Automatic Digital Computers" (1956)
- "Time-Sharing Computer Systems" (1968)
- "Memoirs of a Computer Pioneer" (1985)

### Cambridge Alumni
- Trained computer science leaders worldwide
- David Wheeler, Roger Needham, and many others
- Influenced computer science programs globally
- International network of collaborators

## Awards and Honors

- **1967:** ACM Turing Award
- **1968:** Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS)
- **1980:** Faraday Medal (IEEE)
- **1980:** Eckert-Mauchly Award (IEEE/ACM)
- **1997:** Fellow of the Computer History Museum
- **2000:** Knighted (Sir Maurice Wilkes)
- **2001:** Fellow of the Royal Academy of Engineering

## Modern Relevance

- Stored-program concept still fundamental
- Microprogramming in every CPU
- Subroutine libraries universal
- Cambridge model copied worldwide
- First programming textbook still referenced

## Cultural Impact

- Symbol of British computing excellence
- Bridged wartime and modern computing
- Connected British and American computing
- Preserved computing heritage through memoirs
- Model for academic computer science leadership
