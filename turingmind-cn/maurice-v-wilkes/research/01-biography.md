# Maurice V. Wilkes - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Maurice Vincent Wilkes
- **Born:** June 26, 1913, Dudley, Worcestershire, England
- **Died:** November 29, 2010, Cambridge, England
- **Nationality:** British
- **Turing Award Year:** 1967

## Education

- **King Edward VI College, Stourbridge:** Preparatory education
- **St. John's College, Cambridge:**
  - B.A. in Mathematics, 1934
  - M.A., 1938
  - Ph.D. in Physics, 1936
  - Research on radio wave propagation

## Career Timeline

- **1937-1945:** University Demonstrator, Cambridge Mathematical Laboratory
- **1945-1980:** Director, Cambridge Mathematical Laboratory (later Computer Laboratory)
- **1949:** EDSAC operational - world's first practical stored-program computer
- **1951:** EDSAC 2 development begins
- **1956:** Invented microprogramming
- **1965:** Titan (Atlas 2) computer project
- **1980-1986:** Professor and Head, Computer Laboratory
- **1986-1990:** Professor Emeritus, Cambridge
- **1980-1986:** Adjunct Professor, MIT

## Major Achievements

- Built EDSAC (1949): first practical stored-program computer
- Invented microprogramming (1951, published 1955)
- Developed subroutine library concepts
- Led development of EDSAC 2 and Titan computers
- Pioneered computer architecture education
- Key figure in British computer science establishment

## Personal Characteristics

- Practical, engineering-focused approach
- Effective administrator and builder
- Excellent writer and communicator
- Bridge between theory and implementation
- Patient teacher and mentor
- Knighted in 2000 (Sir Maurice Wilkes)
