# Maurice V. Wilkes - Ethics and Philosophy

## Engineering Philosophy

### Practical Focus
- Believed computers should solve real problems
- Engineering elegance over theoretical purity
- Build what works, improve incrementally
- Theory guided by practical constraints

### Design Principles
- Simplicity in architecture
- Reliability through good engineering
- Maintainability as key design goal
- User needs drive technical decisions

## Professional Responsibility

### Research Integrity
- Careful documentation of work
- Credit to collaborators appropriately
- Honest reporting of capabilities
- Acknowledgment of influences

### Mentorship Ethics
- Committed to student development
- Created collaborative environment
- Shared credit generously
- Supported career advancement

## Technology and Society

### Responsible Innovation
- Aware of computing's societal impact
- Supported computers for scientific progress
- Cautious about military applications
- Advocated for civilian research focus

### Democratization of Computing
- Made computing accessible at Cambridge
- Supported time-sharing for broader access
- Advocated for computer literacy
- Believed in educational mission

## Competition and Collaboration

### Collegial Attitude
- Respect for competing efforts (Manchester, NPL)
- Shared knowledge with American colleagues
- Participated in open academic discourse
- Supported healthy competition

### Industry Relations
- Balanced academic and commercial interests
- Consulted while maintaining independence
- Supported British computer industry
- Avoided conflicts of interest

## Views on Computing Culture

### Programming as Craft
- Valued skilled programming
- Supported professional development
- Advocated for systematic methods
- Respected practical experience

### Academic Standards
- Rigorous approach to research
- High standards for publications
- Careful experimental validation
- Peer review importance

## Ethical Positions

### Intellectual Property
- Supported open sharing of research
- Published extensively for community
- Defensive patenting only when necessary
- Believed in scientific publication

### Recognition and Credit
- Generous with credit to others
- Acknowledged von Neumann influence
- Recognized team's contributions
- Team success over personal fame

### On Computing's Future
- Concerned about complexity

- Supported fundamental principles

- Wrote memoirs to document history
