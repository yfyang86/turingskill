# Maurice V. Wilkes - Key Collaborators

## EDSAC Development Team

### Core Team (1946-1949)
- **David J. Wheeler:** PhD student, subroutines, assembly language
  - Invented "Wheeler Jump" for subroutine calling
  - Co-authored first programming textbook
  - Later professor at Cambridge and Illinois

- **Stanley Gill:** Research student, early programming
  - Co-authored EDSAC programming book
  - Later worked on computer applications
  - Industry career after Cambridge

- **Bill Renwick:** Chief Engineer
  - Hardware design and construction
  - Oversaw EDSAC construction
  - Essential to project success

## Cambridge Colleagues

### Mathematical Laboratory Era
- **Lennard-Jones:** Laboratory founder (1930s)
- **John Ashby:** Early computing pioneer
- **Roger Needham:** Successor as Lab Director
  - Security and operating systems
  - Continued Cambridge tradition

### Academic Collaborators
- **Douglas Hartree:** Mathematical physicist, computing advocate
- **John von Neumann:** Intellectual influence
  - Met at Moore School Lectures (1946)
  - EDVAC report inspired EDSAC design
- **Max Newman:** Manchester computer pioneer

## International Collaborations

### Moore School Connection
- **J. Presper Eckert and John Mauchly:** ENIAC builders
- **Herman Goldstine:** von Neumann collaborator
- Attended famous Moore School Lectures (1946)
- Brought ideas back to Cambridge

### American Collaborations
- **MIT:** Adjunct professor (1980-1986)
- **DEC:** Consulted on VAX architecture
- **IBM:** Microprogramming discussions
- **IEEE Computer Society:** Active member
