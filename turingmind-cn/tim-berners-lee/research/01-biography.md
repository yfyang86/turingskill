# Tim Berners-Lee: Biography

## Early Life and Education

Timothy John Berners-Lee was born on June 8, 1955, in London, England. He grew up in an intellectually stimulating environment where science and technology were part of daily life.

## Family Background

Both of Tim's parents were mathematicians who worked on the Ferranti Mark I, the first commercial computer. His father, Conway Berners-Lee, and mother, Mary Lee Woods, met while working at Ferranti. This unique upbringing meant young Tim had access to computer manuals and technical discussions from an early age, fostering his fascination with electronics and computing.

## Education

Berners-Lee attended Sheen Mount Primary School and later Emanuel School in London. In 1973, he entered The Queen's College, Oxford, to study Physics. While at Oxford, he built a computer out of an old television set, soldering iron, and microprocessor—demonstrating his hands-on approach to technology. He graduated in 1976 with a first-class degree in Physics.

## Early Career

After graduation, Berners-Lee worked at Plessey Telecommunications as a software engineer. He then joined D.G. Nash, where he developed typesetting software and an operating system. These experiences honed his programming skills and understanding of information systems.

## CERN Years (1980, 1984-1994)

Berners-Lee first worked at CERN in 1980 as a consultant, developing software to help researchers share information. During this period, he created ENQUIRE, a hypertext system that would later influence the World Wide Web.

He returned to CERN in 1984 as a Fellow, working on distributed real-time systems. By 1989, frustrated by the inefficiency of information sharing among researchers, he proposed the World Wide Web. Despite initial skepticism, he received approval and built the first web browser and server in 1990.

## MIT and W3C (1994-Present)

In 1994, Berners-Lee joined MIT's Laboratory for Computer Science (now CSAIL) and founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) to oversee web standards development. He has remained at MIT as a senior research scientist and professor.

## Academic Recognition

Berners-Lee holds professorships at the University of Southampton (Computer Science) and Oxford University. He is a Fellow of the Royal Society and recipient of numerous honorary degrees.

## Personal Life

Berners-Lee married Nancy Carlson in 1990; they divorced in 2011. He married Rosemary Leith in 2014. He maintains a relatively private personal life while remaining actively engaged in web advocacy and research.

## Current Work

Today, Berners-Lee continues his work on web decentralization through the Solid project and remains a vocal advocate for an open, accessible web for all humanity.
