# Whitfield Diffie: Other Writings

## Forewords and Introductions
Diffie has written numerous forewords for books on cryptography and security, lending his authority and historical perspective to works by other authors. These include:
- Forewords to editions of David Kahn's "The Codebreakers"
- Introductions to technical cryptography texts
- Prefaces to historical works on cryptographic history

His forewords typically provide:
- Personal recollections of the field's development
- Historical context for the work being introduced
- Connections between historical and modern cryptography

## Encyclopedia and Reference Articles
Diffie has contributed to major reference works:
- **Encyclopedia of Cryptography and Security**: Articles on public-key cryptography history
- **Encyclopedia of Computer Science**: Entries on cryptographic protocols
- **Various handbooks**: Chapters on key management and security architecture

These reference contributions help establish authoritative technical explanations for future researchers.

## Testimony and Policy Submissions

### Congressional Testimony
During the "Crypto Wars" of the 1990s, Diffie testified before U.S. Congressional committees on:
- The Clipper Chip proposal
- Export controls on cryptography
- The relationship between cryptography and civil liberties

His testimony combined technical explanation with constitutional argument, helping legislators understand both the capabilities and implications of cryptographic technology.

### Federal Communications Commission Filings
Diffie submitted comments to the FCC regarding:
- Communications privacy
- Encryption in telecommunications
- The role of cryptography in protecting communications

### International Policy Contributions
Beyond U.S. policy, Diffie has contributed to:
- **OECD cryptography policy discussions**: Advising on international approaches
- **European policy consultations**: Providing technical expertise on encryption debates
- **Internet governance forums**: ICANN and related organizations

Diffie's non-academic writings consistently:

Even in popular writing, Diffie addresses:
- The relationship between technology and power
