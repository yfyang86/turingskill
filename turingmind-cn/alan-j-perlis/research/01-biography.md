# Alan J. Perlis - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Alan Jay Perlis
- **Born:** April 1, 1922, Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, USA
- **Died:** February 7, 1990, New Haven, Connecticut, USA
- **Nationality:** American
- **Turing Award Year:** 1966 (First Ever Recipient)

## Education

- **Undergraduate:** Carnegie Institute of Technology (now Carnegie Mellon)
  - B.S. in Chemistry, 1943
- **Graduate:** MIT
  - M.S. in Mathematics, 1949
  - Ph.D. in Mathematics, 1950
  - Thesis: "On Integral Equations with Almost-Periodic Kernels"

## Career Timeline

- **1952-1956:** Research Mathematician, Tufts University
- **1956-1971:** Professor, Carnegie Institute of Technology
  - Founded computer science department
  - Led ALGOL development team
- **1958-1962:** Director, Carnegie Tech Computation Center
- **1962-1964:** Chairman, Carnegie Tech Computer Science Department
  - First computer science department in United States
- **1971-1984:** Professor and Chairman, Yale University Computer Science
  - Established Yale's computer science department
- **1984-1990:** Eugene Higgins Professor, Yale University

## Major Achievements

- **1966:** First recipient of the Turing Award
- Pioneered compiler construction theory
- Led development of ALGOL 58 and ALGOL 60
- Founded first U.S. computer science department
- Established ACM Programming Languages Conferences
- Authored influential epigrams on programming

## Personal Characteristics

- Known for wit and intellectual brilliance
- Passionate teacher and mentor
- Advocate for programming as intellectual discipline
- Prolific writer of computer aphorisms
- Bridge between mathematics and computer science
