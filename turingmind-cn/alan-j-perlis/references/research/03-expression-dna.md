# Alan J. Perlis - Expression and Thought Patterns

## Communication Style

### Aphoristic Expression
- Master of concise, memorable statements
- "Epigrams on Programming" showcases style
- 130 epigrams covering programming philosophy
- Wit combined with technical insight

### Key Aphorisms
- "Syntactic sugar causes cancer of the semicolon"
- "A language that doesn't affect the way you think is not worth knowing"
- "LISP programmers know the value of everything and the cost of nothing"
- "Don't have good ideas if you aren't willing to be responsible for them"

## Intellectual Patterns

### Bridge-Building Approach
- Connected mathematics with programming
- Linked theory to practical implementation
- Bridged academia and industry
- United American and European computing

### Teaching Philosophy
- Programming as intellectual discipline
- Emphasis on fundamental principles
- Rejection of cookbook approaches
- Value of elegance and simplicity

## Writing Characteristics

- Used humor to make points memorable
