# Yoshua Bengio: Key Papers and Publications

## Foundational Papers

### "A Neural Probabilistic Language Model" (2000)
Bengio, Y., Ducharme, R., Vincent, P., & Jauvin, C. (2003). Journal of Machine Learning Research, 3, 1137-1155.

Actually published in 2003, though preliminary versions appeared earlier. This landmark paper:
- Introduced neural network language models
- Proposed distributed word representations
- Showed how to overcome curse of dimensionality
- Established foundation for modern NLP

One of the most cited papers in machine learning (>10,000 citations).

### "Greedy Layer-Wise Training of Deep Networks" (2007)
Bengio, Y., Lamblin, P., Popovici, D., & Larochelle, H. (2007). Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems.

Key paper on training deep architectures:
- Layer-wise pretraining
- Unsupervised learning
- Overcoming vanishing gradients
- Sparking renewed interest in deep learning

### "Learning Deep Architectures for AI" (2009)
Bengio, Y. (2009). Foundations and Trends in Machine Learning, 2(1), 1-127.

Comprehensive survey of deep learning:
- Theoretical foundations
- Training methods
- Applications
- Research directions

## The "Deep Learning" Survey Paper

### "Deep Learning" (2015)
LeCun, Y., Bengio, Y., & Hinton, G. (2015). Nature, 521(7553), 436-444.

Landmark survey co-authored with fellow Turing Award winners:
- Overview of deep learning revolution
- Published in premier scientific journal
- Reached broad scientific audience
- Consolidated field's progress

Highly cited (>30,000 citations), introducing deep learning to scientific mainstream.

## Representation Learning

### "Representation Learning: A Review and New Perspectives" (2013)
Bengio, Y., Courville, A., & Vincent, P. (2013). IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence, 35(8), 1798-1828.

Comprehensive review of representation learning:
- Unsupervised feature learning
- Deep learning principles
- Transfer learning
- Disentangled representations

### "Generalized Denoising Auto-Encoders as Generative Models" (2013)
Bengio et al. Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems.

Contributed to generative modeling with neural networks.
