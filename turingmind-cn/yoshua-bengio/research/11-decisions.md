# Yoshua Bengio: Key Decisions and Turning Points

## Decision to Pursue Neural Networks (1980s)
### Context
Other AI approaches were dominant; neural networks were unfashionable.

### The Decision
Focus on neural networks and machine learning.

### Consequences
- Positioned for deep learning revolution
- Years of limited recognition
- Development of foundational ideas
- Ready when conditions changed

## Decision to Join AT&T Bell Labs (1991-1992)
### Context
Postdoctoral opportunity with leading researchers.

### The Decision
Accept position at Bell Labs.

### Consequences
- Collaboration with LeCun
- Exposure to industrial research
- Network building
- Different perspective from academia

## Decision to Join Université de Montréal (1993)
### Context
Multiple academic opportunities available.

### The Decision
Accept position at UdeM in Montreal.

### Consequences
- Lifelong institutional home
- Building local AI ecosystem
- Creating MILA
- Canadian AI leadership

## Decision to Persist Through the Neural Network Winter (1990s-2000s)
### Context
Neural networks out of favor; other approaches dominant.

### The Decision
Continue neural network research despite limited support.

### Consequences
- Maintained expertise
- Trained key researchers
- Developed foundational techniques
- Prepared for revolution

## Decision to Focus on Neural Language Models (2000)
### Context
NLP dominated by statistical methods.

### The Decision
Pursue neural approaches to language.

### Consequences
- Foundational 2003 paper
- Influenced word embeddings
- Enabled modern NLP
- Shaped field direction

## Decision to Build MILA (2013)
### Context
Deep learning gaining traction; opportunity to formalize group.

### The Decision
Create formal institute for learning algorithms.

### Consequences
- Major research institution
- Global AI hub
- Economic development
- Training center

## Decision to Co-found Element AI (2016)
### Context
Deep learning commercial boom; opportunity to create company.

### The Decision
Launch AI company with co-founders.

### Consequences
- Economic impact for Montreal
- Industry experience
- Eventually sold to ServiceNow
- Lessons about commercialization

## Decision to Increase Focus on AI Safety (Late 2010s)
### Context
Deep learning success raising safety questions.

### The Decision
Pivot significant research effort to safety.

### Consequences
- Legitimizing safety research
- Changing research priorities
- Policy engagement
- Potential impact on field direction

## Decision to Stay in Academia
### Context
Numerous industry opportunities, especially after Turing Award.

### The Decision
Remain at university despite lucrative alternatives.

### Consequences
- Maintained research independence
- Continued educational mission
- Open publication
- Influence through teaching

## Decision on Open Science Commitment
### Context
Pressure to commercialize, keep research proprietary.

### The Decision
Maintain commitment to open research.

### Consequences
- Broad dissemination of ideas
- Educational accessibility
- Field advancement
- Potential lost commercial opportunities

Each decision reflects Bengio's values: fundamental research, educational mission, Canadian commitment, and social responsibility.
