# Richard W. Hamming - Key Decisions

## Career Decisions

### Joining Bell Labs (1946)
- Left academia for industrial research
- Opportunity to work on practical problems
- Access to cutting-edge computing
- Led to error-correcting codes discovery

### Invention of Hamming Codes (1947-1950)
- Persisted through frustrating calculation errors
- Committed to solving practical problem
- Published seminal paper in 1950
- Established foundation of coding theory

### Moving to Naval Postgraduate School (1976)
- Left Bell Labs after 30 years
- Desire to teach and write
- Focus on education and mentorship
- Created "Art of Doing Science" course

### Writing Textbooks
- Invested significant time in educational writing
- "Numerical Methods" became classic
- Multiple editions over decades
- Educational legacy as important as research
