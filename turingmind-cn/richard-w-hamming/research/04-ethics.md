# Richard W. Hamming - Ethics and Philosophy

## Research Philosophy

### The Hamming Question
- "What are the most important problems in your field?"
- "Are you working on one of them?"
- Weekly research discussions at Bell Labs
- Focus on significant contributions

### Great Problems Focus
- Tackle important, not just interesting problems
- Impact over novelty for its own sake
- Long-term thinking in research
- Service to scientific progress

## Professional Ethics

### Research Integrity
- Insisted on clear, honest presentation
- Acknowledged limitations of work
- Careful error analysis
- Reproducible results

### Teaching Ethics
- Genuine concern for student learning
- Challenging students to excel
- Preparing students for real problems
- Honest assessment of difficulties

## Views on Science and Engineering

### The Art of Doing Science
- Science and engineering as creative arts
- Requires both talent and dedication
- Importance of clear thinking
- Communication as essential skill

### Technical Excellence
- Excellence as ethical obligation
- Not settling for mediocrity
- Continuous improvement
- High professional standards

## Technology and Society

### Responsible Innovation
- Aware of technology's impact
- Supported beneficial applications

- Emphasis on scientific advancement

### Knowledge Sharing
- Prolific writing and teaching
- Open sharing of ideas
- Friday research discussions open to all
- Mentorship as obligation
