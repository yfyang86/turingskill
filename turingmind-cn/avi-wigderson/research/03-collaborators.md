# Avi Wigderson: Key Collaborators

## Noam Nisan

### Relationship
Long-term collaborator at Hebrew University and beyond.

### Key Work
- **Nisan-Wigderson Generator** (1994): Landmark derandomization result
- Pseudorandomness theory
- Hardness vs. randomness trade-offs

### Impact
The Nisan-Wigderson generator is one of the most influential constructions in complexity theory.

## Russell Impagliazzo

### Relationship
Frequent collaborator on randomness and complexity.

### Key Work
- **Impagliazzo-Wigderson Theorem** (1997): P = BPP under hardness assumptions
- Hardness amplification
- Average-case complexity

### Significance
Their work established the hardness-randomness connection as a central paradigm.

## László Lovász

### Relationship
Co-recipient of 2021 Abel Prize.

### Collaboration
- Bridging mathematics and computer science
- Graph theory and algorithms
- Theoretical foundations

### Abel Prize
Shared "for their foundational contributions to theoretical computer science and discrete mathematics."

## Omer Reingold and Salil Vadhan

### Relationship
Collaborators on expander graphs.

### Key Work
- **Zig-Zag product** (2002): Novel expander construction
- **SL = L** (Reingold): Undirected connectivity in log space
- Pseudorandom walks

### Impact
The zig-zag product led to breakthrough results in space complexity.
