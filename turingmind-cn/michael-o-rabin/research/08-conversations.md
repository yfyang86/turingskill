# Michael O. Rabin: Interviews and Dialogues

## On the Scott Collaboration (2007 interview)
"Dana and I were both at IBM Research that summer. We started talking about automata and decision problems. The idea of nondeterminism emerged naturally - it simplified many proofs. We didn't realize at the time how fundamental it would become."

## On Primality Testing (2010)
"Gary Miller had this beautiful deterministic test, but it required an unproven hypothesis. I wondered: what if we accept a small probability of error? The result was surprisingly efficient - and practical for cryptography."

## On Randomized Algorithms
"Randomness is a computational resource. Sometimes it's easier to find a needle in a haystack if you sample randomly rather than searching systematically. This insight applies across computer science."

## On Cryptography
"Good cryptography should be provably secure. The Rabin cryptosystem has the property that breaking it is as hard as factoring. This 'provable security' paradigm has guided modern cryptography."

## On Research Style
"I move between pure mathematics and practical problems. The primality test came from number theory but became essential for cryptography. Good theory often finds unexpected applications."

## On the Turing Award
"Sharing it with Dana was special. The automata paper was early work, but it provided foundations. It's satisfying when theoretical contributions stand the test of time."

## On Israel and Academia
"Hebrew University has been my academic home for decades. Building computer science there has been important to me. At the same time, Harvard offers different opportunities and colleagues. Both enrich my work."

## On Continuing Research (2022, age 91)
"There are always interesting problems. Recently I've been thinking about Banach spaces again - coming full circle to my mathematical roots. Age doesn't diminish curiosity."
