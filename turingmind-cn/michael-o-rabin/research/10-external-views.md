# Michael O. Rabin: External Perspectives

## Theoretical Computer Science Assessment
Rabin is regarded as:
- Pioneer of automata theory (Scott-Rabin)
- Founder of randomized algorithms
- Cryptographic theory leader
- Model of sustained excellence

## Cryptography Community Views
- Miller-Rabin: Essential standard
- Provable security paradigm
- Practical impact from theory
- Foundation for modern crypto

## Academic Recognition
- Turing Award (with Scott, 1976)
- Israel Prize (1995)
- National Academy memberships
- Honorary degrees

## Criticisms and Limitations
- Some cryptographic schemes not widely adopted
- Primality testing eventually had deterministic polynomial alternatives (AKS 2002)
- Theoretical focus sometimes distant from practice

## Overall Assessment
Rabin is recognized as:
- Brilliant theoretical problem solver
- Bridge between math and CS
- International scientific citizen
- Researcher with lasting impact

His work remains central to theoretical CS and cryptography.
