# Geoffrey Hinton: Interviews and Conversations

## 2023-2024: The Warning Period

### CBS 60 Minutes (October 2023)
- **Interviewer:** Scott Pelley
- **Key quotes:**
  - "I regret my life's work"
  - Comparisons to Oppenheimer and the atomic bomb
  - Discussion of AI consciousness and understanding
- **Significance:** Widely viewed; brought AI risk to mainstream American audience

### MIT Technology Review - "Godfather of AI" (May 2023)
- **Interviewer:** Will Knight
- **Content:** First major interview after leaving Google
- **Key topics:**
  - Why he left Google
  - Existential risk concerns
  - The pace of AI development
  - Regret and responsibility

### The Guardian - Multiple Interviews (2023)
- Series of interviews following departure
- Topics: regulation, safety, the future of AI
- Emphasis on international coordination

### BBC Newsnight (May 2023)
- **Format:** Television interview
- **Key message:** Urgency of addressing AI risks
- **British context:** Discussion of UK AI regulation

### New York Times - "The Godfather of AI" (May 2023)
- **Authors:** Cade Metz and Gregory Schmidt
- **Content:** Profile accompanying resignation announcement
- **Impact:** Broke the story globally

## Notable Interview Characteristics

### Communication Style
- British understatement (see also expression-dNA.md)
- Frequent qualifiers: "I might be wrong but..."
- Technical precision even for general audiences
- Willingness to say "I don't know"

### Recurring Themes Across Interviews
1. **Biological inspiration:** Consistent emphasis on learning from brains
2. **Long-term perspective:** References to AI winters and resurgence
3. **Intellectual humility:** Acknowledging mistakes and uncertainty
4. **Concern about competition:** Race dynamics in AI development
5. **Hope for solutions:** Not purely pessimistic; believes problems solvable

---

## Interview Impact

Hinton's interviews have:
- Shaped public understanding of AI capabilities and risks
- Influenced policy discussions at governmental level
- Created narrative around "AI godfathers" warning of dangers
- Legitimized AI safety as mainstream concern
