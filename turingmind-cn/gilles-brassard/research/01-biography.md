# Gilles Brassard - Biography

## Overview
Gilles Brassard (born April 20, 1955) is a Canadian computer scientist and cryptographer at the Université de Montréal. He shared the 2025 ACM A.M. Turing Award with Charles Bennett for their foundational work on quantum cryptography and quantum information.

## Early Life
- **Born**: April 20, 1955 in Montreal, Quebec, Canada
- **Family**: French-Canadian
- **Grew up**: Montreal area
- **Early Interests**: Mathematics, cryptography

## Education
- **BSc** (1975): Université de Montréal, Computer Science
- **MSc** (1976): Université de Montréal, Computer Science
- **PhD** (1979): Cornell University, Computer Science
- **PhD Advisor**: John Hopcroft

## Academic Career

### Université de Montréal (1979-present)
- **Professor**: Computer Science
- **Department**: DIRO (Département d'informatique)
- **Research Chair**: Canada Research Chair (2001-2022)
- **Status**: Active professor at age 70

## Major Contributions
- Quantum cryptography (BB84)
- Quantum teleportation
- Quantum information theory
- Cryptography theory

## Quantum Cryptography
