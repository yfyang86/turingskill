# Gilles Brassard: Interviews and Dialogues

## On Meeting Bennett

> "I met Charles Bennett in 1979 while swimming in Puerto Rico. He proposed an idea for bank notes that couldn't be counterfeited based on quantum mechanics. That conversation started our 40-year collaboration."

## On BB84

> "BB84 showed that two parties could establish a secret key protected by the laws of physics. Any eavesdropping changes the quantum state and leaves traces."

## On Quantum Cryptography

> "Quantum cryptography offers perfect security without trusting computational difficulty—a major advance over classical methods."

## On Receiving the Turing Award

> "This recognition underscores the importance of fundamental research driven by curiosity about how the universe works."

## On the Breakthrough Prize

> "Sharing the Breakthrough Prize with Charles was an incredible honor. It recognized our foundational work in quantum information."

## Advice to Researchers

> "Follow your curiosity. The most important discoveries come from understanding how the universe works, not from seeking applications."

## On Quantum Teleportation

> "Quantum teleportation shows how entanglement enables capabilities impossible with classical physics. It became a cornerstone of quantum communication."
