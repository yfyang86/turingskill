# Gilles Brassard: Legacy and Impact

## Founding Quantum Cryptography

Brassard is recognized as the first researcher to pursue quantum information science systematically.

### BB84 Protocol (1984)
Co-invented with Charles Bennett:
- First practical quantum cryptography protocol
- Uses quantum mechanics for security
- Unconditionally secure against any computation
- Foundation for quantum key distribution (QKD)

## Quantum Teleportation (1993)

Co-discovered method to transmit quantum states using entanglement:
- Enabled quantum communication
- Foundation for quantum networks
- Demonstrated quantum entanglement utility

## Quantum Information Science

Brassard helped establish quantum information as a rigorous scientific discipline bridging physics and computer science.

## Awards and Recognition

- **Turing Award** (2025, shared with Charles Bennett)
- **Breakthrough Prize in Fundamental Physics** (2022, shared with Bennett)
- **Gerhard Herzberg Gold Medal** (2009, Canada's highest science honor)
- **Rank of Officer of the Order of Canada**

## Honors

- Canada Research Chair in Quantum Information (2001-2021)
- Fellow of Royal Society of Canada
- Fellow of ACM

## Long-Term Impact

Brassard's work enables:
- Quantum-secure communications
- Future quantum internet
- Post-quantum cryptography transition
- New understanding of quantum mechanics

## Summary

Gilles Brassard's legacy is founding the field of quantum information science and creating the first practical quantum cryptography system.
