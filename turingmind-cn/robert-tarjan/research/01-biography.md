# Robert Tarjan - Biography

## Early Life (1948-1969)

**Birth and Education**
- Born April 30, 1948, in Pomona, California
- Grew up in California
- Exceptional mathematical talent from early age
- International Mathematical Olympiad (1964-1966)

**High School Achievements**
- Three-time IMO participant
- Multiple medals (silver, gold)
- Mathematical problem-solving excellence
- Caltech admission (1964)

**California Institute of Technology (1964-1969)**
- B.S. in Mathematics (1969)
- Undergraduate research in switching theory
- Interest in algorithms and complexity
- Exceptional academic record

## Graduate Studies

**Stanford University (1969-1972)**
- M.S. in Computer Science (1971)
- PhD in Computer Science (1972)
- Advisor: Robert Floyd (Turing Award 1978)
- Dissertation on efficient planarity testing

**Cornell Connection (1971-1972)**
- Met John Hopcroft at Stanford
- Collaborated on graph algorithms
- Planarity testing breakthrough
- Joint research continued

## Academic Career

**Cornell University (1972-1973)**
- Assistant Professor
- Continued Hopcroft collaboration
- Graph algorithm research
- Brief but productive period

**University of California, Berkeley (1973-1975)**
- Miller Research Fellow
- Independent research development
- Data structure innovations
- Algorithm analysis methods

**Stanford University (1974-1980)**
- Assistant Professor (1974)
- Associate Professor (1977)
- Professor (1978)
- Splay trees invention

**Princeton University (1985-2014)**
- James S. McDonnell Distinguished University Professor
- Computer Science Department
- Continued research excellence
- Teaching and mentorship

**InterTech Corporation (1985-2015)**
- Chief Scientist
- Industrial research application
- Algorithm commercialization
- Industry-academia bridge

## Major Research Contributions

**Union-Find (Disjoint Set Union)**
- Path compression optimization
- Union by rank heuristic
- Inverse Ackermann complexity
- "Amortized analysis" paper (1985)

**Splay Trees (1985)**
- Self-adjusting binary search trees
- "Amortized complexity" concept
- Daniel Sleator co-inventor
- Optimal performance properties

**Graph Algorithms**
- Strongly connected components (linear time)
- Planarity testing (with Hopcroft)
- Biconnected components
- Depth-first search applications

**Data Structures**
- Fibonacci heaps (with Fredman)
- Link-cut trees
- Dynamic trees
- Persistence concepts
