# David A. Patterson: Legacy and Impact

## The RISC Architecture Legacy
Patterson's RISC work fundamentally changed processor design:

### Universal Adoption of RISC Principles
RISC concepts are now in virtually all processors:
- **ARM processors**: Billions of mobile devices
- **Modern x86**: Intel and AMD use RISC-like micro-ops internally
- **Embedded systems**: MIPS, PowerPC, and others
- **Specialized processors**: GPUs, TPUs use RISC principles

### The Performance Revolution
RISC enabled performance gains that drove:
- Personal computing
- Mobile devices
- High-performance servers
- Embedded systems

## The RAID Storage Legacy

### Ubiquitous Storage Technology
RAID is fundamental to modern computing:
- Enterprise storage arrays
- Database servers
- Web and cloud infrastructure
- Network-attached storage
- Personal computer storage (some levels)

### Industry Standard
The RAID taxonomy (levels 0-5) Patterson developed:
- Universal terminology in storage
- Taught in computer science curricula
- Used by storage professionals worldwide
- Referenced in product specifications

## Educational Impact

### The Standard Textbook
"Computer Architecture: A Quantitative Approach":
- Trained generations of computer architects
- Established common methodology
- Influenced industry practices
- Continues through multiple editions

### Berkeley Computer Science
Patterson's 40 years at Berkeley contributed to:
- Premier computer science program
- Numerous successful graduates
- Strong systems research tradition
- Industry-academic connections

## The 2017 Turing Award
The ACM recognized:
"For pioneering a systematic, quantitative approach to the design and evaluation of computer architectures with enduring impact on the microprocessor industry."

This placed Patterson among computing's most honored figures.

## Research Methodology Legacy

### Quantitative Systems Research

- Based on measurement
- Rigorously quantitative
- Open to validation
- Honest about limitations

### This methodology influenced:
- How architecture research is conducted

- Educational approaches
