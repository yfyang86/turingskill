# David A. Patterson: Key Decisions and Turning Points

## Decision to Pursue Computer Science (1960s-1970s)
### Context
Mathematics background, emerging field of computer science.

### The Decision
Focus on computer architecture and systems.

### Consequences
- Positioned for RISC and RAID breakthroughs
- Combined mathematical rigor with systems thinking
- Academic career in growing field

## Decision to Join Berkeley (1976)
### Context
Multiple academic opportunities after Ph.D.

### The Decision
Accept assistant professor position at UC Berkeley.

### Consequences
- 40-year career at premier public university
- Part of vibrant intellectual community
- Platform for major contributions
- Commitment to public education

## Decision to Pursue RISC (1980)
### Context
Established research in architecture, could have continued incrementally.

### The Decision
Launch ambitious RISC project challenging established wisdom.

### Consequences
- Groundbreaking research
- Competitive pressure on industry
- SPARC commercialization
- Academic recognition
- Risk of failure in new direction

## Decision to Collaborate with Hennessy on Textbook (1980s)
### Context
Stanford-Berkeley rivalry, potential competition.

### The Decision
Propose joint textbook combining perspectives.

### Consequences
- Influential educational resource
- Ongoing collaboration across rivalry
- Joint Turing Award foundation
- Decades of partnership

## Decision on RAID Research (1987)
### Context
Storage becoming bottleneck, expensive solutions dominant.

### The Decision
Pursue research on redundant arrays of inexpensive disks.

### Consequences
- Fundamental storage technology
- Widespread industry adoption
- Student success (Gibson, etc.)
- Practical impact

## Decision to Stay at Berkeley (Multiple Points)
### Context
Offers from other institutions, industry opportunities.

### The Decision
Remain at Berkeley throughout career.

### Consequences
- Institutional loyalty and stability
- Investment in Berkeley's success
- Missed opportunities elsewhere
- Deep relationships and impact

## Decision to Focus on Teaching (Ongoing)
### Context
Successful research career could have de-emphasized teaching.

### The Decision
Maintain strong commitment to teaching alongside research.

### Consequences
- Exceptional teacher reputation
- Textbook development
- Student mentorship
- Time away from research

## Decision to Write Textbook (1990)
### Context
Could have continued with research papers only.

### The Decision
Commit to comprehensive textbook with Hennessy.

### Consequences
- Educational impact on generations
- Ongoing time commitment (6 editions)
- Influence on field's methodology
- Reduced research time

## Decision to Continue Textbook Updates (1996-2019)
### Context
After initial success, could have stopped or handed off.

### The Decision
Continue updating through multiple editions over three decades.

### Consequences
- Sustained educational influence
- Significant time investment
- Keeping current with field
- Partnership with Hennessy

## Decision to Join Google (2016)
### Context
Retirement from Berkeley, options for continued engagement.

### The Decision
Accept position as Google distinguished engineer.

### Consequences
- Experience at massive scale
- Industry research continuation
- Bridge between academia and practice
- Time away from traditional research

## Decision to Retire from Google (2020)
### Context
Successful 4 years at Google, could have continued.

### The Decision
Retire from full-time work.

### Consequences
- End of formal career
- Time for other pursuits
- Continued influence through writing/speaking
- Transition to emeritus role

## Decision on Work-Life Balance (Throughout Career)
### Context
Demanding academic career with high expectations.

### The Decision
Maintain focus on priorities without total work dominance.

### Consequences
- Sustainable long-term career
- Personal relationships maintained
- Health and well-being
- Possibly missed opportunities

Each decision reflects Patterson's values: quantitative thinking, educational commitment, collaborative spirit, and long-term perspective.
