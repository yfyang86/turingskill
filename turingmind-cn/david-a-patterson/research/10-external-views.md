# David A. Patterson: External Perspectives and Assessments

## Academic and Professional Assessments

### The Turing Award Citation (2017)
The ACM recognized Patterson and Hennessy "for pioneering a systematic, quantitative approach to the design and evaluation of computer architectures with enduring impact on the microprocessor industry."

The citation noted they "created a renaissance in computer architecture" and that their textbook "trained a generation of engineers."

## Critical Perspectives

### RISC Debate Critics
During RISC vs. CISC debates:
- Some argued RISC advantages were overstated
- Intel's continued success challenged RISC claims
- Benchmark selection was questioned
- Architectural vs. implementation factors debated

These technical disputes ultimately converged rather than producing clear winners.

### Academic Career Critiques
Some questioned:
- Time devoted to textbook vs. research
- Industry engagement vs. pure research
- High-profile presence vs. depth in specific areas

Patterson's defenders note his sustained research contributions alongside other activities.

### RAID Limitations
Critics and practitioners note RAID's:
- Write penalty in some configurations
- Complexity at scale
- Not suitable for all workloads
- Superseded by newer technologies in some contexts

Patterson acknowledged these limitations while defending RAID's historical importance.
