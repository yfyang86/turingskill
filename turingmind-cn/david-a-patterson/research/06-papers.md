# David A. Patterson: Key Papers and Publications

## Foundational RISC Papers

### "The Case for the Reduced Instruction Set Computer" (1982)
Patterson, D. A., & Ditzel, D. R. (1982). Computer Architecture News, 10(6), 25-33.

This seminal paper articulated the arguments for RISC architecture. It analyzed why complex instruction sets were inefficient and made the case for simplicity, measurement, and compiler optimization.

### "RISC I: A Reduced Instruction Set VLSI Computer" (1982)
Patterson, D. A., & Sequin, C. H. (1982). Proceedings of the 8th Annual Symposium on Computer Architecture.

Described the first Berkeley RISC processor, demonstrating the viability of the RISC approach in hardware.

### "A VLSI RISC" (1982)
Patterson, D. A. (1982). IEEE Computer, 15(9), 8-21.

Comprehensive description of RISC principles, implementation, and performance.

## RAID Papers

### "A Case for Redundant Arrays of Inexpensive Disks (RAID)" (1988)
Patterson, D. A., Gibson, G., & Katz, R. H. (1988). Proceedings of SIGMOD.

The foundational RAID paper that introduced the concept and taxonomy of RAID levels. One of the most influential papers in storage systems.

### "Introduction to Redundant Arrays of Inexpensive Disks (RAID)" (1989)
Various follow-up papers elaborating on RAID concepts and implementations.

## Quantitative Architecture Papers

### "How to Make a Computer that Meets the Requirements for a General-Purpose Computer" (Various)
Patterson's papers consistently emphasized quantitative analysis and measurement.

## The Textbook

### "Computer Architecture: A Quantitative Approach" (1990-Present)
Hennessy, J. L., & Patterson, D. A.

Six editions (1990, 1996, 2003, 2007, 2012, 2019).

The definitive computer architecture textbook, translated into multiple languages.

### "Computer Organization and Design: The Hardware/Software Interface"
Patterson, D. A., & Hennessy, J. L.

Undergraduate-level companion text, also multiple editions.

## Network of Workstations Papers

### "The Case for NOW (Networks of Workstations)" (1995)
Anderson, T. E., et al. (Patterson as co-author). IEEE Micro.

Argued for using commodity workstations as a parallel computing platform.

### Various NOW Project Papers
Research on:
- GLUnix
- xFS file system
- Active Messages
- Cluster computing infrastructure

## Recovery-Oriented Computing Papers

### "Recovery-Oriented Computing (ROC): Motivation, Definition, Techniques, and Case Studies" (2002)
Brown, A. B., & Patterson, D. A. (2002). UC Berkeley Computer Science Technical Report.

Introduced the ROC concept focusing on fast recovery rather than failure prevention.

### Various ROC Papers
Research on:
- Microreboots
- Failure injection
- Recovery mechanisms
- System reliability

- Readable by broad audiences

- Heavy use of data
- Statistical analysis

### Honest and Balanced
- Acknowledging limitations
- Presenting counterarguments
- Fair evaluation of alternatives

### Practical Orientation
- Focus on working systems
