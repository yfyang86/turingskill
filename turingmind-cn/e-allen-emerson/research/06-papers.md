# E. Allen Emerson: Key Publications

## Foundational Papers

### "Design and Synthesis of Synchronization Skeletons Using Branching-Time Temporal Logic"
**Authors**: E.M. Clarke, E.A. Emerson
**Venue**: Logic of Programs Workshop, 1981
**Significance**: The foundational paper on model checking. Introduced the basic algorithm for checking whether a finite-state system satisfies a CTL formula. Established that model checking for CTL is decidable in linear time.

### "Using Branching Time Temporal Logic to Synthesize Synchronization Skeletons"
**Authors**: E.A. Emerson, E.M. Clarke
**Venue**: Science of Computer Programming, 1982
**Significance**: Extended the 1981 work with emphasis on synthesis—automatically generating correct synchronization code from specifications. Demonstrated the practical potential of the approach.

### "'Sometimes' and 'Not Never' Revisited: On Branching Versus Linear Time"
**Authors**: E.A. Emerson, Joseph Halpern
**Venue**: POPL 1983
**Significance**: Definitive analysis comparing linear temporal logic (LTL) and branching temporal logic (CTL). Established expressiveness results showing that certain properties are naturally expressed in branching time but not linear time, and vice versa.

## Symbolic Model Checking

### "Symbolic Model Checking: 10^20 States and Beyond"
**Authors**: J.R. Burch, E.M. Clarke, K.L. McMillan, D.L. Dill, L.J. Hwang
**Venue**: Information and Computation, 1992
**Significance**: While Emerson was not first author, this paper from the Clarke group (with whom Emerson collaborated closely) demonstrated how BDD-based symbolic model checking could verify enormously complex systems. Emerson contributed to the theoretical foundations.

### "Efficient Generation of Counterexamples and Witnesses in Symbolic Model Checking"
**Authors**: H. Jin, K. Ravi, F. Somenzi
**Venue**: DAC 2002
**Significance**: Emerson's influence on the field is shown through citations—his foundational work is referenced in nearly every significant paper in symbolic model checking.

## Automata-Theoretic Methods

### "An Automata-Theoretic Decision Procedure for the Propositional Mu-Calculus"
**Authors**: E.A. Emerson, C. Lei
**Venue**: Information and Computation, 1986
**Significance**: Established automata-theoretic foundations for the modal mu-calculus, showing connections between temporal logics, automata, and fixed-point calculi. This unified perspective influenced decades of subsequent research.

### "Modal Logics for Mobile Processes"
**Authors**: E.A. Emerson, R. Trefler
**Venue**: Theoretical Computer Science, 1999
**Significance**: Extended model checking techniques to mobile and dynamically reconfiguring systems, addressing limitations of static finite-state approaches.

## Parameterized Verification

### "Parameterized Verification of Ring Networks"
**Authors**: E.A. Emerson, V. Kahlon
**Venue**: VMCAI 2000
**Significance**: Addressed the challenge of verifying systems with arbitrary numbers of identical processes—a major theoretical advance beyond finite-state model checking.

### "Model Checking Parameterized Protocols"
**Authors**: E.A. Emerson, K. Namjoshi
**Venue**: CAV 2000
**Significance**: Developed methods for verifying protocols that work for any number of participating nodes, with applications to cache coherence and mutual exclusion protocols.

## Surveys and Retrospectives

### "The Birth of Model Checking"
**Authors**: E.M. Clarke, E.A. Emerson, J. Sifakis
**Venue**: 25 Years of Model Checking, 2008
**Significance**: Joint retrospective by the three Turing Award winners, tracing the development of model checking from their independent perspectives and reflecting on its transformation into industrial practice.
