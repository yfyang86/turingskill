# Charles W. Bachman: Biography

## Early Life and Education
Charles William "Charlie" Bachman was born on December 11, 1924, in Manhattan, Kansas. His upbringing in the American Midwest instilled a practical, engineering-oriented mindset that would define his career. He attended Michigan State College (now Michigan State University), earning a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering in 1948. He continued his education at the University of Pennsylvania, receiving a master's degree in mechanical engineering in 1950 from the Towne School, while also attending the Wharton School of Business.

## Early Career at Dow Chemical (1950-1960)
Bachman began his professional career at Dow Chemical Corporation, where he worked for a decade. This experience exposed him to the complexities of industrial data management and the challenges of organizing large-scale business information. His work involved operations research and systems analysis, giving him firsthand insight into how businesses actually used (and struggled with) data.

## The GE Years and Birth of IDS (1960-1970)
In 1960, Bachman joined General Electric Company, where he would create his masterpiece: the Integrated Data Store (IDS). Working with Homer Carney, Bachman developed IDS between 1961-1963 as a random-access database management system for GE's low voltage switch gear department. IDS became the first production database management system, featuring:
- A database language integrated with COBOL
- Virtual memory storage to transcend physical limitations
- Network-oriented data structures mirroring business models
- An integrated transaction-oriented monitor

In 1963, Bachman introduced "Bachman diagrams" - visual maps of data structures that became an industry standard for database design.

## Industry Leadership and Standards (1970-1983)
Bachman joined Honeywell Information Systems in 1970, where he continued database development and became deeply involved in standards work. From 1978-1982, he chaired ANSI and ISO committees that standardized the OSI seven-layer reference model, providing a conceptual alternative to Internet protocols. His networking architecture work at Honeywell became the foundation for the OSI model adopted in 1983.

## Entrepreneurship: Bachman Information Systems (1983-1991)
In 1983, Bachman founded Bachman Information Systems, pioneering computer-aided software engineering (CASE) tools. The company's products automated the design and publication of Bachman diagrams and generated database schemas through forward and reverse engineering. This entrepreneurial venture demonstrated his continued commitment to practical tools for data management professionals.

## Honors and Later Years
- 1973: ACM Turing Award (8th recipient)
- 1977: Distinguished Fellow, British Computer Society
- 2014: National Medal of Technology and Innovation (awarded by President Obama)

Bachman passed away on July 13, 2017, at age 92, leaving a legacy as a bridge-builder between engineering practice and computer science theory.

## Character and Philosophy
Bachman famously said: "I have said all through my career I have been happy to have the jobs I have had, because I've had a lot of freedom to do what I wanted to do, and had the secondary advantage of what I wanted to do seemed to be useful." This pragmatic philosophy - valuing useful solutions over theoretical elegance - defined his approach to computing.
