# Charles W. Bachman: Key Collaborators

## Homer Carney (GE, 1960-1963)
Carney worked directly with Bachman on the original IDS implementation at General Electric. Together they built the first working database management system, with Carney handling much of the technical implementation while Bachman architected the overall system design. Their collaboration established the foundation for all subsequent network database systems.

## CODASYL Database Task Group Members
As chair of this influential group, Bachman collaborated with:
- **IBM representatives**: Who brought competing hierarchical perspectives
- **Hardware vendors**: Burroughs, Univac, Honeywell engineers who implemented CODASYL systems
- **Government users**: Who provided requirements from large-scale applications
- **Academic advisors**: Who ensured theoretical soundness

The CODASYL collaboration (1965-1971) resulted in the first database standard, though tensions between industry pragmatists and theoretical purists were constant.

## ANSI/ISO Standards Colleagues
During his chairmanship of ANSI SPARC Study Group on Data Base Management Systems (1975-1977), Bachman worked with:
- Database researchers developing the three-schema architecture
- Network protocol specialists
- International standards delegates from ISO member nations

This collaboration produced recommendations for DBMS architecture that influenced database theory and practice.

## ISO TC97/SC16 Open Systems Interconnection
As chairman of this committee (1978-1982), Bachman navigated complex international politics to develop the OSI reference model. Key collaborators included:
- European PTT representatives advocating for their national networks
- Computer manufacturers seeking competitive advantage
- Academic researchers contributing architectural principles

The initial opposition from IBM (who argued the chair should be neutral rather than affiliated with a vendor) tested Bachman's diplomatic skills, but he proved effective as "an aggressive chairman" who drove the work to completion.

## Bachman Information Systems Team (1983-1991)
As founder and chairman, Bachman built a team that:
- Developed CASE tools for database design
- Pioneered reverse engineering of database schemas
- Created automated diagramming tools

This entrepreneurial phase required collaboration with software engineers, marketers, and venture capitalists - a different kind of partnership than his earlier technical collaborations.

## The Bachman-Codd Tension
Though not a collaboration, Bachman's relationship with Edgar F. Codd (IBM) defined much of his career context. At the 1974 ACM workshop debate, Bachman represented engineering pragmatism while Codd advocated mathematical elegance. Their intellectual opposition drove both schools of thought to refinement, ultimately benefiting the entire field despite the competitive tension.
