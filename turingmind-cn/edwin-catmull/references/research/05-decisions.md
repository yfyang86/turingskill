# Edwin Catmull - Key Decisions

## Early Career Decisions

### 1974: Joining Lucasfilm
- **Context:** Left academic security
- **Risk:** Unknown CGI commercial viability
- **Decision:** Accept George Lucas's offer
- **Outcome:** Graphics industry foundation

### 1986: Pixar Spin-off
- **Context:** Computer Division for sale
- **Options:** Continue, join buyer, start company
- **Decision:** Co-found Pixar with Jobs
- **Outcome:** Independent path

## Technology Strategy

### RenderMan Development (1980s-90s)
- **Decision:** Create and license software
- **Alternative:** Keep proprietary
- **Outcome:** Industry standard

### Hardware vs. Software (1990)
- **Context:** Selling Pixar Image Computer
- **Decision:** Exit hardware
- **Focus:** Animation software
- **Outcome:** Toy Story enabled

## Business Decisions

### Disney Partnership (1991)
- **Context:** Financial struggles
- **Deal:** Three-picture co-production
- **Terms:** Disney distribution
- **Outcome:** Toy Story funding

### IPO Decision (1995)
- **Timing:** Week before Toy Story
- **Risk:** Movie failure = IPO failure
- **Decision:** Proceed
- **Outcome:** $140 million raised

### Disney Acquisition (2006)

#### Key Decisions:
- **Autonomy:** Negotiated creative independence
- **Culture:** Pixar remains separate
- **Leadership:** Take Disney roles
- **Outcome:** $7.4 billion, culture preserved

## Creative Decisions

### Toy Story Production
- **Decision:** Embrace CGI limitations
- **Style:** Plastic look as aesthetic
- **Risk:** Audience rejection
- **Outcome:** CGI visual language

### Braintrust System
- **Decision:** Peer review without hierarchy
- **Innovation:** Directors critique each other
- **Risk:** Ego conflicts
- **Outcome:** Sustained quality

### Sequel Strategy Evolution
- **Initial:** No sequels stance
- **Evolution:** Selective sequels
- **Criteria:** Story potential
- **Balance:** Originals vs. sequels

## Organizational Decisions

### Pixar University
- **Decision:** 4 hours/week education
- **Scope:** All employees
- **Content:** Art, improvisation, technical
- **Outcome:** Culture reinforcement

### Emeryville Campus
- **Vision:** "Building Jobs built"
- **Features:** Central atrium
- **Purpose:** Force interactions
- **Result:** Serendipitous collaboration

### Post-Merger Integration
- **Decision:** Reverse integration
- **Approach:** Disney adopts Pixar ways
- **Risk:** Culture clash
- **Outcome:** Disney Animation Renaissance
