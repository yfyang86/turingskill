# Edwin Catmull - Key Decisions

## Founding and Early Years

### 1974: Joining Lucasfilm
- **Context:** Left secure academic environment
- **Risk:** Unknown commercial viability of CGI
- **Decision:** Accept George Lucas's offer
- **Outcome:** Foundation of modern computer graphics industry

### 1986: Pixar Spin-off
- **Context:** Lucasfilm Computer Division for sale
- **Options:** Continue at Lucasfilm, join buyer, or start company
- **Decision:** Co-found Pixar with Steve Jobs
- **Outcome:** Independent path to CGI feature films

## Technology Strategy

### RenderMan Development (1980s-90s)
- **Decision:** Create and license rendering software
- **Alternative:** Keep proprietary
- **Outcome:** Industry standard, revenue stream, influenced GPU design

### Hardware vs. Software Focus (1990)
- **Context:** Pixar selling Pixar Image Computer
- **Decision:** Exit hardware, focus on animation software and content
- **Risk:** Loss of hardware revenue
- **Outcome:** Enabled Toy Story and feature film focus

## Business Decisions

### Disney Partnership (1991)
- **Context:** Financial struggles, need for capital
- **Deal:** Three-picture co-production deal
- **Terms:** Disney owns distribution, Pixar creates content
- **Outcome:** Toy Story funding, but tensions over terms

### IPO Decision (1995)
- **Timing:** Week before Toy Story release
- **Risk:** If movie failed, IPO would fail
- **Decision:** Proceed with offering
- **Outcome:** $140 million raised, Steve Jobs became billionaire

### Disney Acquisition Negotiations (2006)

#### Key Decision Points:
- **Autonomy preservation:** Negotiated creative independence
- **Culture protection:** Pixar remains separate entity
- **Leadership continuity:** Catmull and Lasseter take Disney roles
- **Outcome:** $7.4 billion acquisition, culture largely preserved

## Creative Decisions

### Toy Story Production Approach
- **Decision:** Embrace computer limitations as style
- **Example:** Plastic look of toys suited CGI
- **Risk:** Audiences might reject CGI aesthetics
- **Outcome:** Established CGI visual language

### Braintrust System Implementation
- **Decision:** Peer review without hierarchy
- **Innovation:** Directors critique each other's work
- **Risk:** Ego conflicts, creative interference
- **Outcome:** Sustained quality across multiple films

### Sequel Strategy
- **Initial stance:** No sequels (pure creative vision)
- **Evolution:** Selective sequels (Toy Story 2, 3, 4)
- **Decision criteria:** Story potential, not just revenue
- **Balance:** Originals vs. sequels portfolio

## Organizational Decisions

### Pixar University Creation
- **Decision:** Invest 4 hours/week in employee education
- **Scope:** All employees, all disciplines
- **Content:** Art, improvisation, technical skills
- **Outcome:** Cross-pollination, culture reinforcement

### Emeryville Campus Design
- **Vision:** "The building that Steve Jobs built"
- **Features:** Central atrium, collaborative spaces
- **Purpose:** Force interactions across departments
- **Result:** Serendipitous collaboration

### Post-Merger Integration
- **Decision:** Reverse integration (Pixar changes Disney)
- **Approach:** Disney Animation adopts Pixar practices
- **Risk:** Culture clash, talent departure
- **Outcome:** Disney Animation Renaissance

## Personal Career Decisions

### 2018 Retirement
- **Timing:** After 12 years managing Disney relationship
- **Succession:** Long-planned transition
- **Legacy:** Left organization in strong position

### Post-Retirement Activities
- **Decision:** Advisory roles, not competitive ventures
- **Focus:** Education, mentoring, speaking
- **Philosophy:** Give back to industry

## Decision-Making Philosophy

### Patterns Observed:
- Long-term orientation over short-term gains
- People protection over project protection
- Collective wisdom over individual genius
- Culture as competitive advantage

### Regrets Acknowledged:
- Some early Disney relationship tensions
- Not addressing certain cultural issues faster
- Balancing scale with intimacy
