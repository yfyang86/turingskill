# Edwin Catmull - Ethics and Philosophy

## Creative Culture Values

### Psychological Safety
- Emphasized safe environment for creative risk-taking
- "Fail fast" philosophy without blame
- Candor and honest feedback encouraged
- Protection from external interference

### Balancing Art and Commerce
- Resisted pressure to compromise creative vision
- Negotiated autonomy in Disney merger
- Protected Pixar culture during acquisitions
- Prioritized long-term quality over short-term profits

## Openness and Transparency

### Information Sharing
- Believed in radical candor between teams
- Encouraged early sharing of incomplete work
- Rejected information silos
- Open door policy for all employees

### Post-Mortem Culture
- Mandatory project reviews
- Honest examination of failures
- Learning-focused rather than blame-focused
- Institutional knowledge preservation

## Research Ethics

### Open Publication
- Encouraged publishing research at SIGGRAPH
- Shared technology with broader industry
- RenderMan licensed to competitors
- Believed in advancing field collectively

### Credit and Attribution
- Proper attribution for technical contributions
- Recognition of team achievements
- Balance between individual and collective credit

## Business Ethics

### Employee Treatment
- Avoided layoffs during difficult periods
- Invested in employee development
- Created Pixar University for continuous learning
- Generous benefits and work-life balance

### Competitive Fairness
- Competed on quality, not corporate espionage
- Respected competitor innovations
- Ethical recruitment practices

## Technology Ethics

### Responsible Innovation
- Considered societal impact of CGI
- Avoided technology for harmful purposes
- Focused on entertainment and education

### Accessibility
- Supported industry standard formats
- Contributed to open standards development
- Educational outreach

## Personal Integrity

- Led by example in humility
- Admitted mistakes publicly
- Continuous learning mindset
- Respect for all contributors regardless of role
