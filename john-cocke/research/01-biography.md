# John Cocke - Biography

## Early Life (1925-1946)

**Birth and Education**
- Born May 30, 1925, in Charlotte, North Carolina
- Grew up in North Carolina
- Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from Duke University (1946)
- Master's degree in Mathematics from Duke University (1956)

**Military Service**
- Served in US Navy during World War II
- Electronics technician training
- Practical engineering experience
- Foundation for later computer work

**Doctoral Studies**
- PhD in Mathematics from Duke University (1953)
- Dissertation on differential geometry
- Mathematical rigor foundation
- Unusual path to computing

## IBM Career (1956-1992)

**Joining IBM Research (1956)**
- Joined IBM Thomas J. Watson Research Center
- Initially worked on cryptographic projects
- Transitioned to computer architecture
- Yorktown Heights, New York location

**Stretch Computer Project (1950s-1960s)**
- IBM 7030 Stretch supercomputer
- Advanced architecture concepts
- Pipelining innovations
- Learned from Stretch challenges

**Compiler Work (1960s-1970s)**
- FORTRAN optimization
- High-level language compilation
- Parsing techniques
- Code generation efficiency

**The RISC Revolution (1970s-1980s)**

**801 Project (1975-1980)**
- Led experimental minicomputer project
- Radical simplification concept
- Reduced Instruction Set Computer (RISC)
- 801 named after building address

**RISC Philosophy Development**
- Simple instructions execute fast
- Compiler handles complexity
- Register-oriented design
- Pipelining optimization

**IBM RT PC (1980s)**
- Commercial RISC implementation
- First RISC workstation
- Proved commercial viability
- Foundation for Power architecture

## Major Innovations

**RISC Architecture**
- Simplicity enables speed
