# Richard W. Hamming - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Richard Wesley Hamming
- **Born:** February 11, 1915, Chicago, Illinois, USA
- **Died:** January 7, 1998, Monterey, California, USA
- **Nationality:** American
- **Turing Award Year:** 1968

## Education

- **University of Chicago:**
  - B.S. in Mathematics, 1937
- **University of Nebraska:**
  - M.A. in Mathematics, 1939
- **University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign:**
  - Ph.D. in Mathematics, 1942
  - Thesis on differential equations

## Career Timeline

- **1942-1945:** Professor, University of Illinois
- **1945-1946:** Assistant Professor, J.B. Speed Scientific School (Louisville)
- **1946-1976:** Bell Telephone Laboratories
  - 1946-1976: Mathematician at Bell Labs
  - Worked on programming and numerical analysis
  - Error-correcting codes research (1947-1950)
- **1976-1997:** Naval Postgraduate School, Monterey
  - 1976-1997: Professor and Adjunct Professor
  - Taught computing and mathematics
  - Wrote influential textbooks

## Major Achievements

- **Hamming Codes (1950):** Error-correcting codes
- **Hamming Distance:** Fundamental metric in coding theory
- **Hamming Window:** Digital signal processing
- **Numerical Methods:** Books widely used
- **Hamming Medal:** IEEE medal named in his honor

## Personal Characteristics

- Brilliant mathematician with practical focus
- Prolific author of textbooks
- Engaging and provocative lecturer
- Known for "Hamming questions"
- Focused on important problems

## Research Philosophy

- Art of Doing Science and Engineering
- Importance of tackling great problems
- Weekly research discussions at Bell Labs
- Emphasis on clear thinking and writing
