# Allen Newell: Key Publications

## Foundational Papers

### "The Logic Theory Machine" (1956)
**Authors**: Newell, Shaw, Simon
**Publication**: IRE Transactions on Information Theory IT-2:3, pp. 61-79
**Significance**: The first AI program. Introduced list processing, heuristic search, and symbolic computation. Proved theorems from Principia Mathematica.

### "GPS: A Program That Simulates Human Thought" (1959)
**Authors**: Newell and Simon
**Publication**: Lernende Automaten, pp. 109-124
**Significance**: Early description of the General Problem Solver and the cognitive simulation approach.

### "Computer Science as Empirical Inquiry: Symbols and Search" (1976)
**Publication**: Communications of the ACM, 19(3):113-126
**Significance**: Turing Award lecture articulating the physical symbol systems hypothesis and the role of heuristic search in AI.

## Major Books

### "Human Problem Solving" (1972)
**Authors**: Newell and Simon
**Publisher**: Prentice-Hall
**Significance**: Comprehensive treatment of GPS and the information processing theory of human cognition. Over 900 pages of detailed theory and experimental results. Remains a cognitive science classic.

### "The Psychology of Human-Computer Interaction" (1983)
**Authors**: Card, Moran, and Newell
**Publisher**: Lawrence Erlbaum Associates
**Significance**: Established the Model Human Processor and GOMS analysis. Created scientific foundations for HCI as a discipline.

### "Unified Theories of Cognition" (1990)
**Author**: Newell
**Publisher**: Harvard University Press
**Significance**: Newell's culminating work, arguing for unified cognitive architectures and presenting SOAR as an example. Based on his William James Lectures at Harvard.

### "Soar: A Cognitive Architecture in Perspective" (1992)
**Editors**: J. Michon and A. Akyurek
**Publisher**: Kluwer
**Significance**: Posthumous collection assessing SOAR's position in cognitive science, with Newell's contributions.

## Key SOAR Papers

### "The SOAR Papers" (1993)
**Editors**: Rosenbloom, Laird, and Newell
**Publisher**: MIT Press
**Significance**: Comprehensive collection documenting SOAR development through 1992.

### "Chunking in SOAR" (1986)
**Authors**: Rosenbloom and Newell
**Significance**: Described SOAR's learning mechanism and how it explained skill acquisition.

## Methodology Papers

### "The Protocol Method as a Tool for Behavioral Research" (1980)
**Significance**: Refined methods for collecting and analyzing think-aloud protocols.

### "The Knowledge Level" (1982)
**Publication**: Artificial Intelligence, 18(1):87-127
**Significance**: Introduced the concept of knowledge as a level of analysis distinct from symbol implementation. Highly influential in AI and philosophy.

## Early Technical Reports
Many of Newell's most influential early work appeared in RAND Corporation research memoranda:
- RM-1951: Logic Theorist documentation
- Various GPS technical reports
- IPL language specifications

These circulated widely and influenced AI researchers before formal publication.

## HCI Papers

### "The Keystroke-Level Model" (1980)
**Authors**: Card, Moran, Newell
**Publication**: International Journal of Man-Machine Studies
**Significance**: Practical predictive model for user interface design.

## Bibliometric Assessment
Newell authored over 250 publications including 10 books. His citation impact:
- "Human Problem Solving": Thousands of citations across psychology and AI
- "The Psychology of Human-Computer Interaction": Foundational HCI text
- "Unified Theories of Cognition": Influential in cognitive science

His papers from the 1950s-1970s remain cited as historical foundations; those from the 1980s-1990s remain relevant to current research.
