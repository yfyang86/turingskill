# Robert W. Floyd - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Robert W Floyd
- **Born:** June 8, 1936, New York City, New York, USA
- **Died:** September 25, 2001, Stanford, California, USA
- **Nationality:** American
- **Turing Award Year:** 1978

## Education

- **Non-traditional path:** Floyd was largely self-taught
- No formal Ph.D. earned
- **University of Chicago:** B.A. in Liberal Arts, 1953 (age 17)
- **Carnegie Institute of Technology:** Some graduate study
- Mostly self-educated in computer science

## Career Timeline

- **1950s:** Early interest in computing as teenager
- **1956-1962:** Senior Programmer, Armour Research Foundation, Chicago
- **1962-1965:** Assistant Professor, Carnegie Institute of Technology
- **1965-1968:** Associate Professor, Carnegie Institute of Technology
- **1968-2001:** Professor, Stanford University
  - 1968-1978: Professor of Computer Science
  - 1978-1994: Professor and Chairman (various periods)
  - 1994-2001: Professor Emeritus

## Major Achievements

- **Floyd's Algorithm:** All-pairs shortest path (1962)
- **Floyd-Warshall Algorithm:** Efficient transitive closure
- **HeapSort:** In-place sorting algorithm (1964)
- **Program Verification:** Floyd-Hoare logic foundations
- **Parsing:** Floyd Productions, operator precedence
- **Grammar Analysis:** Context-free grammar properties

## Personal Characteristics

- Brilliant, largely self-taught mathematician
- Exceptionally clear and elegant thinker
- Warm and encouraging teacher
- Collaborative and generous
- Bridged theory and practice
- Music and art lover

## Interests Beyond Computing

- Classical music (especially Bach)
- Visual arts
- Mathematical puzzles
- Photography
- Hiking and nature
