# Maurice V. Wilkes - External Perspectives

## Colleague Perspectives

### Cambridge Colleagues
- **David Wheeler:** "Wilkes combined engineering genius with administrative skill. His leadership made Cambridge the center of British computing."
- **Roger Needham:** "He created an environment where practical engineering and theoretical work could both flourish."
- **Stanley Gill:** "Patient teacher, exacting standards, always supportive of students."

### International Colleagues
- **John von Neumann:** Mutual respect; von Neumann's EDVAC report inspired Wilkes, but Wilkes made it practical with EDSAC.
- **J. Presper Eckert:** Recognized Wilkes' engineering achievement with EDSAC
- **John Mauchly:** Respected British contribution to stored-program computers
- **John Backus:** Admired his practical approach to compiler and system design

### Industry Perspectives
- **IBM Engineers:** Valued his microprogramming insights; influenced System/360 design
- **DEC Engineers:** Consulted on VAX architecture; respected his architectural judgment
- **Ferranti Management:** Appreciated his support for British computer industry

## Student Perspectives

### PhD Students
- **David Wheeler:** "Taught me that theory must be grounded in working systems"
- **Roger Needham:** "His mentorship shaped my entire career"
- Multiple Cambridge PhDs: uniformly positive about his mentorship

### International Visitors
- American researchers who visited Cambridge praised his hospitality
- Created welcoming environment for international collaboration
- Students from around world sought to study with him

## Academic Perspectives

### Computer Architecture Community
- Viewed as founding father of computer architecture
- Microprogramming recognized as fundamental contribution
- EDSAC cited as first practical stored-program computer
- Cambridge model for CS departments

### British Computing Community
- "Father of British Computing" - widely acknowledged
- Led British response to American computing advances
- Bridge between wartime and modern computing
- Preserved British computing heritage

### International Recognition
- ACM Turing Award validated British contribution
- IEEE awards recognized engineering excellence
- Royal Society Fellowship acknowledged scientific stature
- Knighthood recognized service to British science

## Critical Assessments

### Technical Judgments
- Some considered his approach too conservative
- Others praised his practical engineering
- Microprogramming initially controversial, later universally adopted
- EDSAC slower than Manchester Baby, but more practical

### Manchester Rivalry
- Friendly competition with Williams and Kilburn
- Acknowledged Manchester's speed achievements
- Cambridge's practical approach vs. Manchester's performance
- Both contributed to British computing excellence

## Historical Assessment

### Computing Historians
- William Aspray: "Wilkes made the stored-program computer practical"
- Paul Ceruzzi: "EDSAC proved the concept that changed everything"
- Martin Campbell-Kelly: "Cambridge model shaped British computing"

### Modern Evaluations
- Microprogramming still used in every CPU
- EDSAC recognized as milestone in computing
- First programming textbook remains historically important
- Cambridge Computer Laboratory maintains his legacy
