# Adi Shamir - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Adi Shamir
- **Born:** July 6, 1952, Tel Aviv, Israel
- **Nationality:** Israeli
- **Turing Award Year:** 2002 (with Rivest and Adleman)
- **Award Citation:** For seminal contributions to cryptography

## Education

- **Tel Aviv University:** B.Sc. in Mathematics, 1973
- **Weizmann Institute:** M.Sc. in Computer Science, 1975
- **Weizmann Institute:** Ph.D. in Computer Science, 1977
- **Doctoral Advisor:** Zohar Manna

## Career Timeline

- **1977-1980:** Research Assistant, University of Warwick
- **1980-present:** Professor, Weizmann Institute of Science
- **1980-1984:** Assistant Professor
- **1984-1989:** Associate Professor
- **1989-present:** Full Professor
- **Current:** Paul and Marlene Borman Professor of Applied Mathematics

## Major Achievements

- **1977:** Co-invented RSA algorithm
- **1984:** Differential cryptanalysis (with Biham)
- **1984:** Identity-based cryptography
- **1986:** Secret sharing schemes
- **2002:** ACM Turing Award

## Research Focus Areas

- Cryptanalysis and cryptology
- Cryptographic protocols
- Computer security
- Complexity theory
- Machine learning

## RSA Algorithm Contribution

### Role in Development
- Conceptual insight into trapdoor functions
- Key generation algorithm
- Security analysis
- Mathematical rigor

### Impact
- Foundation of public-key cryptography
- Internet security standard
- Digital signatures
- Secure communications

## Differential Cryptanalysis

### Development (1984)
- With Eli Biham
- Method for analyzing block ciphers
- Applied to DES, FEAL
- Changed cryptanalysis landscape

### Impact
- Understanding of cipher strengths
- Design improvements
- Security evaluation methodology
