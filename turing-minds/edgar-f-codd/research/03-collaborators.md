# Edgar F. Codd - Collaborators and Influence

## IBM Research Colleagues

**Raymond Boyce**
- Co-developed Boyce-Codd Normal Form (BCNF)
- SQUARE query language co-designer
- Died tragically in hiking accident (1974)
- PhD advisor: Codd at Michigan

**Donald Chamberlin**
- System R project lead
- Co-inventor of SQL (with Boyce)
- SEQUEL language designer
- Continued at IBM through DB2 development

**Leonard Liu**
- System R team member
- Query optimization research
- Later founded Wang Labs database division

**Franco Putzolu**
- System R storage manager
- Buffer management innovations
- Access path selection algorithms

## Academic Relationships

**John Holland**
- Codd's PhD advisor at Michigan
- Genetic algorithms pioneer
- Cellular automata research guidance
- Influenced Codd's mathematical rigor

**Michael Stonebraker**
- INGRES project leader at Berkeley
- Academic competitor and colleague
- Extended relational model with POSTGRES
- Later Turing Award winner (2014)

**Chris Date**
- Co-founded Codd & Date consulting
- Prolific author on relational theory
- Advocate for "true relational" systems
- Co-authored The Third Manifesto

**Hugh Darwen**
- Collaborator with Date on relational theory
- Co-authored Tutorial D language specification
- Advocate for relational purity

## IBM Management and Politics

**Charles Bachman**
- Network model advocate (CODASYL/IDS)
- Previous Turing Award winner (1973)
- Professional rival at IBM
- Debated hierarchical vs. relational approaches

**Leonard Baum**
- Watson Lab mathematician
- Early supporter of Codd's ideas
- Speech recognition researcher

**Wally Johnson**
- IBM VP who championed System R
- Overcame internal resistance
- Critical for relational database adoption

## Industry Collaborators

**Larry Ellison**

**Gene Wong**

- Distributed database research

**Bob Epstein**
- Sybase co-founder
- INGRES veteran
