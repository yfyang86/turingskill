# Ole-Johan Dahl - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Ole-Johan Dahl
- **Born:** October 12, 1931, Mandal, Norway
- **Died:** June 29, 2002, Asker, Norway
- **Nationality:** Norwegian
- **Turing Award Year:** 2001 (with Kristen Nygaard)
- **Award Citation:** For ideas fundamental to the emergence of object-oriented programming

## Education

- **University of Oslo:**
- M.S. in Numerical Mathematics, 1957
- Early exposure to computer science through numerical analysis

## Career Timeline

- **1952-1963:** Research Scientist, Norwegian Defense Research Establishment (NDRE)
- **1963-1968:** Research Director, Norwegian Computing Center (NCC)
- **1968-1999:** Professor of Computer Science, University of Oslo
- **1984-1999:** Chair of Informatics, University of Oslo

## Major Achievements

- **1961-1967:** Co-developed Simula I and Simula 67 with Kristen Nygaard
- **1968:** Introduced object-oriented programming concepts to world
- **1970s:** Developed formal verification methods
- **2001:** ACM Turing Award (with Nygaard)

## Research Focus Areas

- Programming language design
- Object-oriented programming
- Discrete event simulation
- Formal methods and program verification
- Operating systems

## Simula Language Development

### Simula I (1962-1964)
- First version for simulation
- Introduced object concepts
- ALGOL 60-based syntax

### Simula 67 (1965-1967)
- Full object-oriented language
- Classes and objects
- Inheritance mechanism
- Virtual procedures

## Honors and Awards

- **1986:** Doctor Honoris Causa, University of Lund, Sweden
- **1990:** Doctor Honoris Causa, Aalborg University, Denmark
- **2000:** IEEE John von Neumann Medal (with Nygaard)
- **2001:** ACM Turing Award (with Nygaard)

## Personal Characteristics

- Quiet, thoughtful demeanor
- Mathematical precision in thinking
- Collaborative spirit with Nygaard
- Dedicated teacher and mentor
- Bridge between theory and practice

## Later Years

- Continued teaching until retirement in 1999
- Remained active in research community
- Died in 2002, shortly after Turing Award
- Award accepted posthumously
