# Tim Berners-Lee: Collaborators and Partnerships

## Robert Cailliau: The WWW Co-Developer

Robert Cailliau was Berners-Lee's most important collaborator in developing the World Wide Web. The Belgian engineer worked alongside Tim at CERN and became the web's first evangelist. Cailliau helped refine the technical architecture, co-authored early documentation, and convinced CERN management to release the web technology royalty-free. He organized the first International WWW Conference in 1994 and remained a passionate advocate for web standards throughout his career.

## CERN Colleagues

### Mike Sendall
Sendall was Berners-Lee's supervisor who approved the 1989 proposal with the famous note "vague but exciting." His support was crucial for the project's initial funding.

### Nicola Pellow
Pellow developed the Line Mode Browser, making the web accessible to computers beyond the NeXT platform—critical for early adoption.

### Jean-François Groff
Groff ported web software to various platforms and helped develop early web protocols, significantly expanding the web's reach.

### Bernd Pollermann
Pollermann contributed to early web server development and helped integrate the web with CERN's existing systems.

## MIT CSAIL Team

When Berners-Lee moved to MIT in 1994, he built a team to develop the World Wide Web Consortium (WC3). Key figures included:
- **Dan Connolly**: HTML standards pioneer and W3C fellow
- **Ralph Swick**: Semantic Web architecture developer
- **Wendy Seltzer**: Policy counsel and standards advocate

## W3C Leadership

Over the years, the W3C has been led by influential figures who extended Berners-Lee's vision:
- **Jeff Jaffe** (CEO): Guided W3C through critical growth periods
- **Sir Nigel Shadbolt**: Semantic Web research leader
- **Wendy Hall**: Hypertext research pioneer and W3C advisor

## Standards Community

Berners-Lee worked closely with Internet pioneers:
- **Vint Cerf** and **Bob Kahn** (TCP/IP creators)
- **Jon Postel** (Internet standards)
- **Dave Raggett** (HTML standards)

## Open Web Advocates

Berners-Lee collaborated with digital rights advocates including:
- **Lawrence Lessig** (Creative Commons)
- **Brewster Kahle** (Internet Archive)
- **Mitchell Baker** (Mozilla Foundation)

These partnerships helped shape the web as an open platform rather than a proprietary system.

## Solid Project Collaborators

For the Solid decentralization project, Berners-Lee works with:
- **John Bruce** (Inrupt CEO)
- **Ruben Verborgh** (Linked Data expert)
- Various researchers at MIT and Oxford
