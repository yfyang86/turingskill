# Geoffrey Everest Hinton: Biography

## Early Life (1947-1970)

**December 6, 1947** — Born in Wimbledon, London, UK

Geoffrey Everest Hinton was born into a distinguished scientific lineage. His father, Howard Everest Hinton, was a renowned entomologist and Fellow of the Royal Society. His great-great-grandfather was George Boole, the mathematician who invented Boolean algebra—the foundation of modern computing. His great-aunt was Joan Boole, and his great-grandfather was Charles Howard Hinton, a mathematician who coined the term "tesseract" and wrote about the fourth dimension.

This heritage of mathematical and scientific thinking would profoundly shape Hinton's intellectual trajectory.

## Education

**Cambridge University (1967-1970)**
- King's College, Cambridge
- BA in Experimental Psychology (1970)
- Briefly studied architecture, then philosophy, before settling on psychology
- Became frustrated with behaviorism's limitations; sought to understand how the brain actually works

**University of Edinburgh (1975-1978)**
- PhD in Artificial Intelligence under Christopher Longuet-Higgins
- Thesis on neural networks and artificial intelligence
- Longuet-Higgins initially advised him to abandon neural networks, but Hinton persisted
- Completed PhD in 1978, becoming one of the few researchers committed to connectionism

## Academic Career

**University of California, San Diego (1978-1981)**
- Postdoctoral researcher
- Joined the UCSD Cognitive Science group
- Met David Rumelhart and collaborated on parallel distributed processing

**Carnegie Mellon University (1982-1987)**
- Professor of Computer Science
- Continued work on connectionist models and Boltzmann machines

**University of Toronto (1987-present)**
- University Professor Emeritus
- Founded the Machine Learning Group
- Established Toronto as a global AI hub
- Supervised generations of influential students

**Google (2013-2023)**
- Distinguished researcher at Google Brain
- Split time between Toronto and Mountain View
- Led teams advancing deep learning applications
- Resigned in May 2023 to speak freely about AI risks

## Personal Life

**Family**
- First wife: Rosalind Zalin (died of ovarian cancer in 1994)
- Second wife: Jacqueline Ford (married 1997, later separated)
- Children include: Thomas Hinton (astronomer at UC Santa Cruz)

**Citizenship**
- Left US in 1987 partly due to opposition to Reagan-era defense funding
- Became Canadian citizen
- Later held dual UK-Canadian citizenship
- Maintained residence in Toronto throughout Google years

## Awards and Recognition

- 2018: ACM A.M. Turing Award (shared with Yann LeCun and Yoshua Bengio)
- 2016: IEEE/RSE James Clerk Maxwell Medal
- 2012: BBVA Foundation Frontiers of Knowledge Award
- 2001: Canada Research Chair in Machine Learning
- Fellow of the Royal Society (1998)
- Fellow of the Royal Society of Canada

## Current Status

As of 2024, Hinton continues to work from Toronto, focusing on AI safety research and public advocacy for responsible AI development.
