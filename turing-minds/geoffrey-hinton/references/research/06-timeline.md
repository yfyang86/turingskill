# Geoffrey Hinton: Timeline

## 1940s-1960s: Origins and Education

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1947 | Born December 6, Wimbledon, London | Enters distinguished scientific lineage (Boole family) |
| 1967 | Enters King's College, Cambridge | Begins shifting studies: architecture → philosophy → psychology |
| 1970 | BA in Experimental Psychology, Cambridge | Rejects behaviorism; seeks mechanistic understanding of mind |

## 1970s: Doctoral Work and Early Career

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1975 | Begins PhD at University of Edinburgh | Under Christopher Longuet-Higgins; persists with neural networks against advice |
| 1978 | PhD in AI completed | One of few researchers committed to connectionism during AI winter |
| 1978 | Postdoc at UC San Diego | Joins cognitive science group; meets David Rumelhart |
| 1979 | First major neural network paper | "Boltzmann Machines" concept development begins |

## 1980s: The Connectionist Revolution

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1982 | Joins Carnegie Mellon University | Faculty position; continues Boltzmann machine work |
| 1983 | "Optimal Perceptual Inference" paper | Formalizes Boltzmann machine with Sejnowski |
| 1985 | Boltzmann Machine paper published | *Cognitive Science*; establishes energy-based learning |
| 1986 | **Nature paper on backpropagation** | With Rumelhart and Williams; foundational to modern AI |
| 1986 | PDP Volumes published | Edited with Rumelhart and McClelland; defines connectionism |
| 1987 | Moves to University of Toronto | Leaves US over defense funding concerns; establishes Canadian base |
| 1988 | Yann LeCun completes PhD | First of many influential students |

## 1990s: Persistence Through AI Winter

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1991-1992 | Yoshua Bengio postdoc at Toronto | Future Turing co-recipient joins lab |
| 1994 | Wife Rosalind Zalin dies | Personal tragedy; continues research |
| 1995 | Marries Jacqueline Ford | Second marriage |
| 1998 | Elected Fellow of the Royal Society | Recognition despite field's low status |
| Late 1990s | Continues neural network research | While most of field has moved to other methods |

## 2000s: The Deep Learning Awakening

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 2001 | IEEE Neural Network Pioneer Award | Early recognition of contributions |
| 2002 | Contrastive divergence paper | Makes Boltzmann machines practical |
| 2006 | **Deep Belief Network paper** | *Neural Computation*; launches modern deep learning era |
| 2006 | **Science paper with Salakhutdinov** | "Reducing dimensionality with neural networks" |
| 2007 | Ilya Sutskever begins PhD | Future OpenAI co-founder joins lab |
| 2009 | Alex Krizhevsky joins as student | ImageNet project begins |

## 2010-2013: The ImageNet Breakthrough

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 2012 | **AlexNet wins ImageNet** | 15.3% error vs 26.2% second place; deep learning proven at scale |
| 2012 | AlexNet paper at NIPS | Launches industry AI revolution |
| 2013 | Sells DNNResearch to Google | $44M; team joins Google Brain |
| 2013 | Becomes Google Distinguished Researcher | Split position with Toronto |

## 2014-2017: Google Years and Technical Innovation

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 2014 | Sequence-to-sequence learning | Major Google Brain paper on machine translation |
| 2016 | Capsule Networks concept | First papers on alternative to CNNs |
| 2017 | Dynamic Routing Between Capsules | NIPS paper with Sabour and Frosst |
| 2017 | Vector Institute founded | Toronto AI hub; Hinton as Chief Scientific Advisor |

## 2018-2020: Recognition and New Directions

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 2018 | **Turing Award** | Shared with LeCun and Bengio; "deep learning godfathers" |
| 2019 | "Deep Learning" review in Nature | With LeCun and Bengio; definitive overview |
| 2020 | GLOM architecture proposal | New approach to part-whole hierarchies |
| 2020-2021 | Growing concern about GPT models | Watching capabilities emerge faster than expected |

## 2021-2022: Increasing Urgency

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 2021 | GLOM detailed paper | arXiv publication on hierarchical representation |
| 2022 | Forward-Forward algorithm | Proposes alternative to backpropagation |
| 2022 | ChatGPT released | Hinton witnesses capabilities firsthand |
| Late 2022 | Internal concerns at Google | Raising alarm about pace of development |

## 2023: The Departure and Warning

| Date | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| May 1, 2023 | Resigns from Google | To speak freely about AI risks |
| May 2, 2023 | NYT publishes departure | "The Godfather of AI Leaves Google and Warns of Danger Ahead" |
| May 2023 | Multiple interviews | CBS, BBC, Guardian, MIT Tech Review |
| June 2023 | Continued advocacy | Testimony and public statements |
| October 2023 | CBS 60 Minutes interview | "I regret my life's work"; mainstream breakthrough |
| November 2023 | UK AI Safety Summit | Speaking at Bletchley Park |
| December 2023 | Continued research | Forward-Forward and safety work from Toronto |

## 2024: Ongoing Work

| Period | Activity | Status |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Early 2024 | Continued advocacy | Policy discussions, interviews |
| 2024 | Technical research | GLOM, Forward-Forward development |
| 2024 | AI safety community | Supporting safety research initiatives |

## Future Milestones (Projected)

| Possible Event | Implications |
|---------------|--------------|

## Timeline Themes

### Perseverance (1970s-2005)
- Continued neural network research when unfashionable
- Key breakthroughs came after decades of persistence

### Vindication (2006-2018)
- Deep learning revolution validates long-term bets
- Turing Award recognizes foundational contributions

### Concern (2019-2023)
- Growing alarm about capabilities and pace
- Transition from builder to critic

### Advocacy (2023-present)
- Using platform for safety advocacy
- Balancing continued research with warnings

## Comparative Timeline Context

### AI Field Milestones During Hinton's Career
- 1980: First AI winter begins
- 1987: Second AI winter
- 1997: Deep Blue defeats Kasparov
- 2011: Watson wins Jeopardy
- 2012: AlexNet (Hinton)
- 2016: AlphaGo defeats Lee Sedol
- 2022: ChatGPT launch
- 2023: GPT-4 release

Hinton's career spans from the depths of AI skepticism to its current dominance, with his work catalyzing the transition.
