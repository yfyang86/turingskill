# Alfred V. Aho: Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1941 | Born in Timmins, Ontario, Canada |
| 1963 | Enters Princeton graduate school; meets Jeffrey Ullman |
| 1967 | Earns PhD; joins Bell Labs |
| 1967-1969 | Works with Ullman at Bell Labs on foundational compiler theory |
| 1974 | Publishes *The Design and Analysis of Computer Algorithms* with Hopcroft and Ullman |
| 1975 | Aho-Corasick string matching algorithm published |
| 1977 | Publishes *Principles of Compiler Design* (Dragon Book) with Ullman |
| 1986 | Dragon Book 2nd edition with Ullman and Sethi |
| 1988 | AWK book published with Kernighan and Weinberger |
| 1992 | *Foundations of Computer Science* with Ullman |
| 1995 | Joins Columbia University as Lawrence Gussman Professor |
| 2003 | IEEE John von Neumann Medal |
| 2007 | Dragon Book 3rd edition with Ullman, Sethi, and Lam |
| 2020 | **Turing Award** (shared with Jeffrey Ullman) |
| Present | Professor Emeritus, Columbia University |

## Career Periods

### Education (1963-1967)
- Princeton University
- MS and PhD in Electrical Engineering/Computer Science
- Early collaboration with Ullman

### Bell Labs Era (1967-1995)
- Member of Technical Staff → VP Computing Sciences Research
- Created YACC, AWK, egrep
- Developed compiler algorithms
- Unix system contributions

### Columbia University (1995-present)
- Lawrence Gussman Professor
- Department Chair
- Continued textbook work
- Mentorship of graduate students
