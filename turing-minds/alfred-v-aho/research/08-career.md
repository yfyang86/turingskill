# Alfred V. Aho - Career Timeline

## Bell Labs (1967-2002)

### Early Years (1967-1974)
- **Joined**: Bell Laboratories, Murray Hill, NJ
- **Department**: Computing Science Research Center
- **Focus**: Formal languages and compiler construction
- **Projects**: Early work on lexical analysis and parsing

### Unix Tools Era (1974-1980)
- **Lex**: Developed with Mike Lesk (1975)
- **Yacc**: Parser generator with Steve Johnson (1975)
- **Algorithm Book**: Published with Hopcroft and Ullman (1974)
- **Dragon Book**: First edition with Ullman (1977)

### AWK Development (1977-1988)
- **AWK Design**: Pattern-action text processing
- **Publication**: AWK book with Kernighan and Weinberger (1988)
- **Impact**: Ubiquitous Unix tool

### Middle Period (1980-1995)
- **Database Theory**: Query optimization research
- **String Algorithms**: Pattern matching advances
- **Dragon Book 2nd Edition**: Planning and development
- **Management**: Research group leadership

### Late Bell Labs (1995-2002)
- **Joint Appointment**: Columbia University begins
- **AT&T Restructuring**: Lucent Technologies spin-off
- **Research Continues**: String algorithms, compilers
- **Transition**: Full-time academic move

## Columbia University (1995-2022)

### Initial Appointment (1995-1997)
- **Professor**: Computer Science Department
- **Chair**: Department Chair (1995-1997)
- **Building**: Established theory and compilers group

### Named Chair (1997-2006)
- **Lawrence Gussman Professor**
- **Dragon Book 2nd Edition**: Published (2006)
- **Research**: String algorithms, computational biology

### Senior Years (2006-2022)
- **Continued Research**: Software engineering, bioinformatics
- **Teaching**: Graduate algorithms and compilers
- **Mentoring**: PhD students and postdocs
- **Service**: University committees, external reviews

### Retirement (2022)
- **Emeritus Status**: Lawrence Gussman Professor Emeritus
- **Continued Activity**: Research and writing
- **Awards**: Turing Award recognition
