# Andrew Chi-Chih Yao - Key Papers and Publications

## Foundational Papers

### Yao's Principle
- **"Probabilistic Computations: Toward a Unified Measure of Complexity"** (1977)
  - FOCS 1977 conference
  - Introduced minimax principle for randomized algorithms
  - Most cited work, foundational for TCS
  - Applications across algorithms, complexity, cryptography

### Communication Complexity
- **"Some Complexity Questions Related to Distributive Computing"** (1979)
  - STOC 1979
  - Founded communication complexity theory
  - Introduced two-party communication model
  - Established basic lower bound techniques

## Cryptography and Security

### Secure Computation
- **"Protocols for Secure Computations"** (1982)
  - FOCS 1982
  - Foundations of multi-party computation
  - Yao's garbled circuits technique
  - Still used in modern secure computation

### Oblivious Transfer
- **"How to Generate and Exchange Secrets"** (1986)
  - FOCS 1986
  - Foundations of oblivious transfer
  - Secure multi-party computation protocols

### Pseudorandomness
- **"Theory and Applications of Trapdoor Functions"** (1982)
  - FOCS 1982
  - Cryptographic applications of complexity theory
  - Hardness vs randomness connections
