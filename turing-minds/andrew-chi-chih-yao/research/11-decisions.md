# Andrew Chi-Chih Yao - Key Decisions and Their Impact

## Career-Defining Decisions

### 1. Physics to Computer Science (1972-1975)
**Context:** Harvard physics PhD, seeking new challenges
**Decision:** Second PhD in computer science at Illinois
**Impact:** Found true intellectual home; applied rigorous physics training to CS
**Alternative:** Could have remained physicist; CS gained foundational theorist

### 2. Joining Stanford Faculty (1976)
**Context:** Fresh CS PhD, multiple opportunities
**Decision:** Stanford assistant professorship
**Impact:** Access to premier theory group; influential colleagues
**Risk:** High expectations at competitive institution

### 3. Yao's Principle (1977)
**Context:** Thinking about randomized algorithms
**Decision:** Pursue fundamental connection to deterministic algorithms
**Impact:** Most influential work; thousands of citations
**Significance:** Transformed analysis of randomized computation

### 4. Communication Complexity (1979)
**Context:** Interest in distributed computation
**Decision:** Create formal framework for communication costs
**Impact:** New research subfield; applications across CS
**Legacy:** Standard topic in graduate curricula

### 5. Move to Princeton (1986)
**Context:** Established researcher, seeking new environment
**Decision:** Leave Stanford for Princeton
**Impact:** Different intellectual environment; continued research excellence
**Trade-off:** Smaller department, more independence

### 6. Return to China (2004)
**Context:** Tenured at Princeton, established career
**Decision:** Leave for Tsinghua University
**Impact:** Transformed Chinese theoretical CS; built IIIS
**Sacrifice:** Left prestigious position, US academic community

### 7. Founding IIIS (2005)
**Context:** Newly at Tsinghua, opportunity to build
**Decision:** Create interdisciplinary research institute
**Impact:** World-class institution; model for CS education
**Challenge:** Starting from scratch in different academic culture

## Research Decisions

**Pattern:** Consistently chose fundamental over applied problems
**Examples:** Yao's Principle, communication complexity
**Outcome:** Lasting impact, wide applicability
**Risk:** Less immediate recognition

### Interdisciplinary Approach
**Decision:** Bridge CS with physics, economics, biology
**Impact:** Unique perspective; innovative applications
**Challenge:** Requires broad expertise

### Collaborative Strategy
**Decision:** Work with students and select colleagues
**Impact:** Strong research lineage
**Style:** Deep, sustained collaborations

## Educational Decisions

**Impact:** Trained generation of researchers

**Pattern:** Fewer papers, each significant
**Result:** High-impact publication record
**Philosophy:** Only publish when truly ready

### Long-term Thinking
**Decision:** Invest in institutional building at Tsinghua
**Impact:** Legacy beyond personal research
**Perspective:** Decades-long commitment

### International Recruitment
**Decision:** Hire globally for IIIS faculty
**Impact:** International reputation
**Risk:** Cultural integration challenges

## Counterfactual Analysis

### If Yao had stayed at Stanford
- Would have continued excellent research
- Might have won Turing Award earlier
- Less institutional impact in China

### If Yao had stayed at Princeton
- Continued world-class research
- Stronger US academic presence
- Missed opportunity to transform Chinese CS

### If Yao had not done second PhD
- Would have been physicist
- CS would lack fundamental contributions
- Different but potentially significant career
