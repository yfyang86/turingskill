# Jack Dongarra: Biography

## Early Life and Education

Jack J. Dongarra was born in 1950. He grew up with an interest in mathematics and science, eventually finding his way to computer science through numerical analysis.

Dongarra earned his Bachelor of Science degree in Mathematics from Chicago State University in 1972. He continued his education at the Illinois Institute of Technology, receiving his Master's degree in Computer Science in 1973.

He completed his PhD in Applied Mathematics from the University of New Mexico in 1980, working on numerical linear algebra—the field that would define his career.

## University of Tennessee (1989-present)

Dongarra joined the University of Tennessee as a University Distinguished Professor in the Electrical Engineering and Computer Science Department. He has been a faculty member for over three decades.

At Tennessee:
- Leads the Innovative Computing Laboratory (ICL)
- Mentors graduate students and postdocs
- Collaborates with researchers worldwide
- Continues active software development

## Oak Ridge National Laboratory (1989-present)

Concurrent with his university position, Dongarra holds a position as Distinguished Research Staff Member at Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL), one of the U.S. Department of Energy's premier research facilities.

At ORNL:
- Works on high-performance computing systems
- Collaborates with supercomputer deployments
- Develops software for leadership-class machines

## University of Manchester (2007-present)

Since 2007, Dongarra has been a Turing Fellow at the University of Manchester in the UK, maintaining strong international research collaborations.

## TOP500 and LINPACK

### LINPACK Benchmark (1979)
Dongarra took over the LINPACK project and developed the benchmark that would become the standard for measuring supercomputer performance.

### TOP500 List (1993)
With Hans Meuer and Erich Strohmaier, Dongarra co-founded the TOP500 list, which ranks the world's fastest supercomputers twice yearly based on LINPACK performance.

The TOP500 has become:
- The authoritative ranking of supercomputers
- A measure of national competitiveness in HPC
- A driver for technological advancement

## Software Libraries

Dongarra has been the primary implementor or principal investigator for:
- **LINPACK** (1970s-80s): Linear equation solving
- **BLAS** (Basic Linear Algebra Subprograms): Standard interface
- **LAPACK** (1990s): Modern linear algebra
- **ScaLAPACK**: Parallel distributed memory version
- **PLASMA**: Multi-core linear algebra
- **MAGMA**: GPU-accelerated linear algebra
- **SLATE**: Exascale linear algebra

## Philosophy

> "I'm a mathematician. To me, everything is linear algebra. This is the material we use to build everything else."

Dongarra views linear algebra as the foundation of computational science, and his life's work has been making linear algebra computations fast, portable, and reliable.
