# Jack Dongarra: External Perspectives

## Turing Award Citation (2021)

> "For pioneering contributions to numerical algorithms and libraries that enabled high performance computational software to keep pace with exponential hardware improvements for over four decades."

ACM recognized Dongarra for:
- Linear algebra libraries (LINPACK, LAPACK, etc.)
- Performance optimization techniques
- Open source contributions
- TOP500 benchmarking

## ACM Recognition

ACM President Gabriele Kotsis:
> "Jack Dongarra played a central part in directing the successful trajectory of this field. His trailblazing work stretches back to 1979, and he remains one of the foremost and actively engaged leaders in the HPC community."

## Industry Perspective

Jeff Dean, Google Senior Fellow:
> "Jack Dongarra's work has fundamentally changed and advanced scientific computing. His deep and important work at the core of the world's most heavily used numerical libraries underlie every area of scientific computing, helping advance everything from drug discovery to weather forecasting, aerospace engineering and dozens more fields."

### Vendor Perspective
Hardware vendors view Dongarra as:

## TOP500 Criticism

Some critiques of the TOP500:
- **Narrow benchmark**: LINPACK doesn't represent all workloads
- **Gaming potential**: Systems optimized specifically for benchmark
- **Power concerns**: Doesn't account for energy efficiency (addressed by Green500)
- **National competition**: Can fuel unproductive rivalries

Dongarra acknowledges these limitations and has worked to address them (HPL-AI, Green500).

## Public Recognition

Dongarra is less known to the general public than some Turing Award winners because:
- His work is infrastructure (libraries, benchmarks)
- No consumer-facing products
- Contributions behind the scenes

However, his impact is arguably greater:
- Every scientific computation uses his libraries
- Every supercomputer is measured by his benchmark
- Weather forecasts, medical research, engineering—all depend on his work

## Overall Assessment

External perspectives consistently view Dongarra as:
- A foundational figure in scientific computing
- An exemplar of open, collaborative research
- A bridge between mathematics and practical computing
- A community builder and mentor

His work demonstrates that infrastructure research—though less visible than applications—can have massive, enduring impact.
