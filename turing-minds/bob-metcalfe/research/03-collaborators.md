# Bob Metcalfe: Key Collaborators

## David Boggs (1950-2022)

### Relationship
Co-inventor of Ethernet. Metcalfe's colleague at Xerox PARC.

### Contribution
- Co-developed the original Ethernet at Xerox PARC
- Co-holder of Ethernet patent
- Implemented early Ethernet hardware

### Quote from Boggs
> "Bob had the vision. I helped make it work."

## Xerox PARC Colleagues

### Alan Kay
PARC researcher working on personal computing. Ethernet connected the Alto computers Kay helped design.

### Butler Lampson
Turing Award winner (1992). Colleague at PARC who contributed to networked personal computing vision.

### Chuck Thacker
Turing Award winner (2009). Designed the Alto computer that Ethernet connected.

## DIX Consortium

### DEC (Digital Equipment Corporation)
Gordon Bell and team at DEC helped standardize Ethernet:
- Contributed to DIX standard
- Promoted Ethernet adoption

### Intel
- Provided semiconductor components
- Helped drive cost down
- Enabled mass production

### Xerox
- Original Ethernet developer
- Contributed patents to standard
- Promoted open approach

## 3Com Team

### Early Employees
Metcalfe founded 3Com in 1979 with early team members who helped commercialize Ethernet:
- Developed Ethernet cards for IBM PC
- Built early networking products
- Created channel partnerships

## IEEE 802.3 Committee

## Collaboration Style

Metcalfe's approach:
- **Visionary**: Sets direction and goals
- **Persuasive**: Convinces others to join
- **Open**: Shares credit and technology
- **Entrepreneurial**: Builds companies around ideas

## Key Pattern

Metcalfe's collaborations often involve:
1. Identifying opportunity
2. Building initial prototype (with Boggs at PARC)
3. Creating standard (with DIX partners)
4. Commercializing (with 3Com team)
5. Promoting adoption (with IEEE, industry)

## Key Insight

Metcalfe's success required collaboration across:
- Research (PARC colleagues)
- Standards (DIX, IEEE)
- Industry (DEC, Intel, 3Com)
- Investment (VC partners)

No single person could have achieved Ethernet's dominance alone. Metcalfe's ability to bring together diverse collaborators was essential.
