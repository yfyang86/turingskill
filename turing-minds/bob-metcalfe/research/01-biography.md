# Bob Metcalfe: Biography

## Early Life and Education

Robert Melancton Metcalfe was born on April 7, 1946, in Brooklyn, New York. He grew up with an interest in technology and innovation, eventually finding his way to computer networking.

Metcalfe earned his Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering from MIT in 1969. He then pursued graduate studies at Harvard University, where he earned his Master's degree in 1970 and his PhD in Computer Science in 1973.

His doctoral thesis at Harvard was on packet switching networks, a topic that would define his career. During his time at Harvard, he also worked at MIT's Project MAC, where he was exposed to early computer networking concepts.

## Xerox PARC (1972-1979)

After completing his PhD, Metcalfe joined Xerox Palo Alto Research Center (PARC), one of the most influential computing research labs in history.

At PARC:
- Worked on the ALOHA network project
- Invented Ethernet in 1973
- Patented the technology (with David Boggs)
- Helped develop the Xerox Alto personal computer

## Ethernet Invention (1973)

Metcalfe's most famous achievement was inventing Ethernet:
- **Problem**: Connect multiple computers in a building
- **Solution**: Carrier Sense Multiple Access with Collision Detection (CSMA/CD)
- **Name**: Inspired by the luminiferous ether (historical medium for light)
- **Speed**: Initial implementation at 2.94 Mbps

The Ethernet patent was filed in 1975 and issued in 1977.

## 3Com Corporation (1979-1990)

In 1979, Metcalfe founded 3Com Corporation to commercialize Ethernet technology:
- **Name**: "Computer Communication Compatibility"
- **First product**: Ethernet adapter for IBM PC
- **Growth**: Became major networking company
- **IPO**: 1984

As CEO, Metcalfe led 3Com through the early years of the networking industry.

## Metcalfe's Law

In addition to Ethernet, Metcalfe formulated Metcalfe's Law:
> "The value of a network is proportional to the square of the number of connected users."

This concept became foundational for understanding network effects in telecommunications and social networks.

## Later Career

After leaving 3Com in 1990:
- **Publisher and pundit**: Wrote for InfoWorld magazine
- **Venture capitalist**: Polaris Venture Partners
- **Academia**: Professor at University of Texas at Austin
- **Speaker**: Technology industry commentator

## Awards and Recognition

- **National Medal of Technology and Innovation** (2003)
- **IEEE Medal of Honor** (1996)
- **Marconi Prize** (2003)
- **Turing Award** (2022)
- Member of National Academy of Engineering
- Member of American Academy of Arts and Sciences

## Personal Style

Metcalfe is known for:
- Charismatic and outspoken personality
- Strong opinions on technology trends
- Willingness to make predictions (sometimes wrong)
- Networking and relationship building
- Entrepreneurial energy

## Predictions and Controversies

Metcalfe made several notable predictions:
- **1995**: Predicted the Internet would collapse (later admitted he was wrong)
- Advocated for various technologies that didn't succeed

He's known for being willing to make bold predictions and admit when wrong.
