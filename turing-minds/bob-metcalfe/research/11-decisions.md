# Bob Metcalfe: Key Decisions

## 1. Pursuing Packet Switching at Harvard (1970-1973)

**Decision**: Choose networking as PhD thesis topic.

**Context**: Early days of computer networking research.

**Impact**: Foundation for Ethernet invention.

## 2. Joining Xerox PARC (1972)

**Decision**: Accept position at legendary research lab.

**Rationale**: Access to cutting-edge personal computing research.

**Impact**: Environment that fostered Ethernet invention.

## 3. Inventing Ethernet (1973)

**Decision**: Create CSMA/CD networking system.

**Innovation**: Distributed access control.

**Impact**: Revolutionary approach to LANs.

## 4. Publishing the Ethernet Paper (1976)

**Decision**: Publish in Communications of the ACM.

**Impact**: Established priority, spread ideas.

## 5. Making Ethernet an Open Standard (1980)

**Decision**: Collaborate with DEC and Intel (DIX).

**Alternative**: Keep proprietary to Xerox.

**Impact**: Enabled Ethernet dominance.

## 6. Founding 3Com (1979)

**Decision**: Leave Xerox to start company.

**Risk**: Startup uncertainty.

**Impact**: Commercialized Ethernet, personal fortune.

## 7. Promoting IEEE Standardization (1983)

**Decision**: Support IEEE 802.3 standard.

**Impact**: International legitimacy.

## 8. Leaving 3Com (1990)

**Decision**: Step down as CEO.

**Reason**: Company had outgrown founder.

**Impact**: Enabled next career phases.

## 9. Becoming a Pundit (1990s)

**Decision**: Write for InfoWorld.

**Impact**: Influenced industry thinking.

## 10. Admitting the Internet Prediction Error (1997)

**Decision**: Publicly eat his words.

**Impact**: Maintained credibility.

## Decision-Making Patterns

### Open vs. Proprietary
Consistently chose openness:
- Ethernet standard
- 3Com interoperability
- Industry collaboration

### Risk Taking
Willing to take entrepreneurial risks:
- Leave Xerox for startup
- Make bold predictions
- Admit public mistakes

### Evolution Over Revolution
Ethernet philosophy:
- Continuous evolution
- Backward compatibility
- Gradual improvement

## Key Trade-offs

| Decision | Alternative | Chosen Path |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Open standard | Proprietary monopoly | Openness |
| Startup | Stay at Xerox | Entrepreneurship |
| Punditry | Academic research | Public commentary |
| Admit error | Defend prediction | Accountability |

## Lessons from Metcalfe's Decisions

1. **Openness wins**: Enabled ecosystem growth
2. **Timing matters**: Ethernet arrived when PCs needed networking
3. **Evolution succeeds**: Continuous improvement beats revolution
4. **Accountability builds trust**: Admitting mistakes enhances credibility
5. **Diversify impact**: Invention, standardization, business, commentary

## Overall Pattern

Metcalfe's career decisions reflect:
- Vision of networked computing
- Belief in open ecosystems
- Entrepreneurial drive
- Public engagement
- Intellectual honesty

These decisions created not just a technology (Ethernet), but a model for technology commercialization.
