# Jeffrey D. Ullman: Key Publications

## Foundational Textbooks

### *Compilers: Principles, Techniques, and Tools* (The "Dragon Book")

**First Edition** (1977): *Principles of Compiler Design*
- Authors: Alfred V. Aho, Jeffrey D. Ullman
- Significance: First comprehensive compiler textbook

**Second Edition** (1986)
- Authors: Aho, Ullman, Ravi Sethi

**Third Edition** (2007)
- Authors: Aho, Ullman, Sethi, Monica Lam

### *Principles of Database Systems* (1980)
**Author**: Jeffrey D. Ullman
**Publisher**: Computer Science Press
**Significance**: Established database theory as an academic discipline
**Impact**: Revolutionized database education

### *The Design and Analysis of Computer Algorithms* (1974)
- Authors: Aho, Hopcroft, Ullman
- Significance: Codified algorithm analysis
- Impact: One of most cited CS books

### *Foundations of Computer Science* (1992)
- Authors: Aho and Ullman
- Significance: Comprehensive CS introduction

## Key Research Papers

### "The Theory of Joins in Relational Databases" (1979)
**Authors**: Aho, Beeri, Ullman
**Publication**: ACM Transactions on Database Systems
**Significance**: Foundational paper on database normalization
**Impact**: Mathematical basis for relational database design

### Automata and Formal Languages

**Books**:
- *Introduction to Automata Theory, Languages, and Computation* (1979, with Hopcroft; later editions with Motwani and others)
- *The Theory of Parsing, Translation, and Compiling* (2 vols., 1972-1973, with Aho)

### Data Integration and Datalog

**Key Papers**:
- "University Information System" (TSIMMIS project)
- Papers on Datalog semantics and implementation
- Data integration with heterogeneous sources
- Semistructured data management

### *Mining of Massive Datasets* (2011, 2nd ed. 2014)
**Authors**: Leskovec, Rajaraman, Ullman
**Publisher**: Cambridge University Press
**Significance**: Comprehensive text on big data algorithms
**Topics**: Link analysis, machine learning at scale, streaming algorithms

## Citation Impact

Ullman's publications are among the most cited in computer science:
- Dragon Book: tens of thousands of citations
- Algorithms book: foundational reference
- Database Systems book: established field
- Automata theory book: standard text

## Publication Philosophy

Ullman's approach:
- **Textbook focus**: Educating generations over paper count
- **Rigorous foundations**: Mathematical correctness
- **Comprehensive coverage**: Leaving no gaps
- **Long-term value**: Books relevant decades later

## Key Insight

Ullman's publication strategy emphasizes depth over breadth. While many researchers optimize for paper count, Ullman created textbooks that:
- Educate millions of students
- Define entire subfields
- Remain relevant for decades
- Influence practice through education

## Notable Quote

On writing books:
> "Never take more than a year to write a book. If you do, the reason you are writing it will have passed."

This reflects Ullman's pragmatic approach to academic productivity—deliver value efficiently so it remains relevant.
