# Jeffrey D. Ullman - Research Philosophy

## Core Principles

### Theory First
- Strong theoretical foundations essential
- Formal methods provide rigor and clarity
- Mathematical abstraction reveals truth
- Theory guides and explains practice

### Education as Mission
- Research must be communicated
- Textbooks highest impact contribution
- Clear writing is ethical obligation
- Democratize access to knowledge

### Rigor and Clarity
- Precise definitions prevent confusion
- Formal proofs ensure correctness
- Clear exposition serves readers
- Quality over quantity in publication

## Research Methodology

### Problem Selection
- Choose fundamental, lasting problems
- Prefer depth over breadth
- Consider educational value
- Think about field development

### Approach
- Start with formal definitions
- Prove theorems to establish truth
- Connect to practical applications
- Write clearly for others to build upon

### Collaboration
- Long-term partnerships most productive
- Complementary expertise valuable
- Generous credit sharing
- Student mentorship as collaboration

## Teaching Philosophy

### Student-Centered
- Start from student knowledge
- Build complexity gradually
- Provide many examples
- Include exercises for practice

### Textbook as Ultimate Teaching
- Reaches more students than courses
- Lasts longer than lectures
- Standardizes knowledge
- Multiplies impact

### Clear Writing
- Precision without unnecessary complexity
- Motivate before formalizing
- Examples illustrate theory
- Exercises reinforce learning

## Views on Computer Science

### Theoretical Foundations
- CS is fundamentally mathematical

### Practical Relevance
- Theory must connect to practice

- Real systems inform good theory
- Industry problems drive research

### Field Evolution
- CS constantly changing
- Foundations remain relevant
- New applications of old theory
- Education must adapt
