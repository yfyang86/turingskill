- Strong mathematics foundation
- Classical education
- King's College, Cambridge admission

## Academic Career

**Swansea University (1968-1971)**
- Lecturer in Computer Science
- Swansea's first CS appointment
- Met Strachey (influence)
- Semantics research beginnings

**Stanford University (1971-1973)**
- Research associate
- Met John McCarthy
- LCF (Logic for Computable Functions) project
- Proof assistant development

**University of Edinburgh (1973-1995)**
- Professor of Computational Logic
- Laboratory for Foundations of Computer Science (founder)
- ML language development
- CCS (Calculus of Communicating Systems)

**University of Cambridge (1995-2001)**
- Professor of Computer Science
- Head of Computer Laboratory
- Built programming language group
- Continued research leadership

## Major Innovations

**LCF (Logic for Computable Functions)**
- Automated theorem proving
- Edinburgh LCF implementation
- Metalanguage (ML) created
- Proof assistant paradigm

**ML Programming Language (1973)**
- Meta Language for LCF
- Polymorphic type system
- Type inference (Hindley-Milner)
- Exception handling
- Garbage collection

**Standard ML (1980s-1990s)**
- ML standardization effort
- SML/NJ implementation
- Module system design
- Language specification

**CCS (Calculus of Communicating Systems)**
- Process algebra (1980)
- Concurrent system modeling
- Bisimulation equivalence
- Hennessy-Milner logic

**Pi-Calculus (1992)**
- With Joachim Parrow and David Walker
- Mobile processes
- Channel passing
- Fundamental concurrency theory
