# Andrew G. Barto: Interviews and Dialogues

## On Reinforcement Learning

> "Reinforcement learning is a computational approach to understanding how agents can learn to behave optimally through interaction with their environment."

## On Connection to Psychology

> "We were inspired by animal learning experiments. The computational models we developed capture principles that apply to both biological and artificial systems."

## On Temporal Difference Learning

> "TD learning allows an agent to learn from the difference between consecutive predictions, without waiting for the final outcome. This is how animals learn too."

## On Sutton

> "Rich and I have been collaborating for over 40 years. His algorithmic insights combined with my interest in biological learning created something new."

## On the Turing Award

> "This recognition is for the field of reinforcement learning. We're proud to see our foundational work enable so many modern AI applications."

## Advice to Students

> "Study psychology and neuroscience alongside computer science. The best AI is inspired by biological intelligence."
