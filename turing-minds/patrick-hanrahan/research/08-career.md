# Patrick Hanrahan - Career Timeline

## New York Institute of Technology (1981-1986)
- **Role**: Researcher at Computer Graphics Lab
- **Key Work**: Early rendering algorithms
- **Mentor**: Edwin Catmull
- **Significance**: Foundation of modern graphics career

## Pixar Animation Studios (1986-1989)
- **Role**: Founding Employee, Rendering Lead
- **Major Achievement**: Created RenderMan
- **Projects**: 
  - "Red's Dream" (1987)
  - "Tin Toy" (1988) - Academy Award winner
- **Impact**: Established technical foundation for CGI animation

## Princeton University (1989-1995)
- **Role**: Assistant Professor of Computer Science
- **Research Focus**: Volume rendering, illumination
- **Teaching**: Computer graphics courses
- **Notable Work**: Continued RenderMan development

## Stanford University (1995-present)

### Associate Professor (1995-2000)
- Joined Stanford Computer Science department
- Established graphics research group
- Continued rendering research

### Full Professor (2000-2005)
- Promotion to full professor
- Expanded research into GPUs and real-time graphics
- Founded Stanford Computer Graphics Laboratory

### Canon USA Professor (2005-present)
- Named chair position
- Continued leadership in graphics research
- Co-founded Stanford Center for Image Systems Engineering

## Tableau Software (2003-2019)
- **Co-founder** with students Chris Stolte and Christian Chabot
- **Role**: Technical advisor and board member
- **IPO**: 2013 (NYSE: DATA)
- **Acquisition**: Salesforce (2019) for $15.7 billion
