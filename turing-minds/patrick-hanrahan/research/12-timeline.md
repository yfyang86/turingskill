# Patrick Hanrahan: Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1955 | Born in Green Bay, Wisconsin |
| 1977 | BS Engineering Physics, University of Wisconsin-Madison |
| 1985 | PhD Biophysics, University of Wisconsin-Madison |
| 1986 | Joins Pixar Animation Studios as founding employee |
| 1986-1989 | Leads design of RenderMan Interface and Shading Language |
| 1989 | Joins Princeton University faculty |
| 1993 | Academy Award (Technical Achievement) for RenderMan |
| 1995 | Moves to Stanford University |
| 2003 | Co-founds Tableau Software |
| 2004 | Publishes *Physically Based Rendering* |
| 2004 | Second Academy Award for subsurface scattering research |
| 2014 | Third Academy Award for *Physically Based Rendering* textbook |
| 2019 | Tableau acquired by Salesforce for $15.7 billion |
| 2019 | **Turing Award** (shared with Edwin Catmull) |
| Present | Professor Emeritus, Stanford; Maxwell/Hanrahan Foundation |

## Career Periods

### Education and Early Career (1977-1986)
- Undergraduate and graduate studies at UW-Madison
- Discovery of computer graphics
- Work at NYIT computer graphics lab

### Pixar Era (1986-1989)
- Founding employee
- RenderMan development
- Early computer animation

### Princeton (1989-1995)
- Assistant/Associate Professor
- Continued rendering research

### Stanford (1995-present)
- Canon Professor
- Computer Graphics Laboratory
- GPU computing research
- Mentorship of 50+ PhD students

### Entrepreneurship (2003-2019)
- Co-founder, Tableau Software
- Visual analytics innovation
- Acquisition by Salesforce
