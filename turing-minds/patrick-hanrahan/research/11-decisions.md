# Patrick Hanrahan: Key Decisions

## 1. Leaving Graduate School for NYIT (Early 1980s)

**Decision**: Interrupt his PhD studies to work at the New York Institute of Technology's computer graphics lab.

**Impact**: Gained hands-on experience in cutting-edge graphics research at one of the pioneering centers in the field.

**Outcome**: Eventually returned to complete his PhD, combining academic credentials with practical experience.

## 2. Joining Pixar as a Founding Employee (1986)

**Decision**: Leave academia to join the fledgling Pixar Animation Studios.

**Rationale**: Opportunity to build rendering systems from the ground up and work with Ed Catmull and the graphics pioneers.

**Impact**: Created RenderMan and the shading language that revolutionized computer graphics.

## 3. Moving to Stanford (1995)

**Decision**: Leave Princeton for Stanford University.

**Rationale**: Stanford's stronger computer graphics program and location in Silicon Valley.

**Impact**: Built one of the world's premier graphics research groups; mentored over 50 PhD students.

## 4. Co-founding Tableau Software (2003)

**Decision**: Launch a startup to commercialize data visualization research.

**Rationale**: Democratatize data analysis through visual interfaces.

**Impact**: Created a new software category; company acquired by Salesforce for $15.7 billion in 2019.

## 5. Focusing on GPU Computing Research (2000s)

**Decision**: Shift research focus to general-purpose computing on GPUs.

**Rationale**: Recognize that GPUs are data-parallel computers, not just graphics accelerators.

**Impact**: The Brook project influenced CUDA and OpenCL; enabled modern AI infrastructure.

## 6. Writing *Physically Based Rendering* (2004)

**Decision**: Invest years in creating a comprehensive textbook on rendering.

**Rationale**: Fill the gap between theory and practice in realistic rendering.

**Impact**: Became the definitive reference; won an Academy Award in 2014.

## Decision-Making Patterns

### Following Curiosity
Hanrahan consistently follows his interests across disciplinary boundaries, from nuclear engineering to biophysics to computer graphics.

### Bridging Theory and Practice
His decisions consistently bridge academic research and practical applications:
- PhD → Industry (Pixar)
- Industry → Academia (Stanford)
- Research → Startup (Tableau)

### Long-term Thinking
- Invested years in RenderMan development
- Spent decades building Stanford's graphics program
- Committed to mentoring over 50 PhD students

### Interdisciplinary Approach
Each major decision reflects willingness to work across boundaries:
- Physics + Computer Science (rendering)
- Art + Technology (Pixar)
- Design + Data (Tableau)

## Key Insight

Hanrahan's career decisions demonstrate that following curiosity and building bridges between disciplines can lead to transformative impact. He has successfully navigated academia, industry, and entrepreneurship while maintaining scientific integrity and a focus on mentorship.
