# Patrick Hanrahan: Key Collaborators

## Edwin Catmull (1945-)

### Relationship
Co-recipient of the 2019 Turing Award. Catmull and Hanrahan shared the award "for fundamental contributions to 3D computer graphics, and the impact of computer-generated imagery (CGI) in filmmaking and other applications."

### Catmull's Role
- Co-founder of Pixar Animation Studios
- Led development of subdivision surfaces (Catmull-Clark algorithm)
- Pioneered texture mapping and Z-buffering
- Focused on the mathematical foundations of computer graphics

### Collaboration
At Pixar, Catmull provided the theoretical foundations while Hanrahan designed the practical rendering systems. Their complementary skills created the technology that enabled the first fully computer-animated feature film.

## Students and Mentees

Hanrahan mentored over 50 PhD students at Stanford, many of whom became leaders in computer graphics:

### Notable Students
- **Peter Schröder**: Professor at Caltech, geometry processing
- **Tamara Munzner**: Professor at UBC, information visualization
- **Maneesh Agrawala**: Professor at Stanford, visualization and graphics
- **Matt Pharr**: Co-author of *Physically Based Rendering*, now at NVIDIA

### Mentorship Philosophy
Hanrahan considers his students his greatest legacy: "Every one of them has been amazingly successful, and that's definitely the thing I'm most proud of."

## Tableau Co-founders

### Christian Chabot and Chris Stolte
Co-founded Tableau Software with Hanrahan in 2003, bringing data visualization to mainstream business users.

## Research Collaborators

### Volume Rendering
- Collaborated with medical imaging researchers
- Worked with scientific visualization practitioners

### GPU Computing
- **Brook project**: With students at Stanford
- Influenced CUDA and OpenCL development

### Physically Based Rendering
Co-authored the definitive textbook *Physically Based Rendering* with Matt Pharr and Wenzel Jakob, which won a Scientific and Technical Academy Award in 2014.

Hanrahan maintained close ties between academia and industry:

Hanrahan's wife and collaborator in the Maxwell/Hanrahan Foundation, supporting fundamental research in underappreciated areas.

Hanrahan's collaborative style emphasizes interdisciplinary connections—bringing together physicists, artists, computer scientists, and domain experts to solve complex visualization challenges.
