# Donald E. Knuth - Interviews & Talks

## Stanford Lectures (Computer Musings)

**Ongoing Series**
- Regular public lectures at Stanford University
- Topics vary: algorithms, typography, puzzles, recent research
- Often includes Q&A sessions
- Available through Stanford channels

**Notable Lectures**
- "The Joy of Asymptotics" - Analysis techniques
- "Dancing Links" - Algorithm X demonstration
- "Mathematical Typography Revisited"
- Updates on TAOCP progress

## Computer History Museum

**Oral History Interview**
- Extensive recorded interview available
- Covers early life through TeX development
- Available at computerhistory.org

**Fellow Award (1998)**
- Inducted for contributions to algorithms and typography
- Acceptance speech recorded
- Part of permanent CHM collection

## ACM Turing Award Lecture (1974)

**Context**
- Awarded at ACM Annual Conference
- Citation emphasized TAOCP contribution
- Youngest recipient at age 36 (record still stands)

**Content**
- Discussed analysis of algorithms
- Philosophy of programming as art
- Future of computer science education

## Kyoto Prize Lecture (1996)

**Advanced Technology Category**
- Awarded by Inamori Foundation
- Lecture on lifetime contributions
- Video available through Kyoto Prize archives

## Knuth Prize Lectures

**Distinction from Turing Award**
- Knuth Prize named in his honor (established 1996)
- Awarded for outstanding contributions to foundations of computer science
- Previous winners include Blum, Yao, Goldreich

## Key Quotes from Talks

**On Programming**
- "Beware of bugs in the above code; I have only proved it correct, not tried it."
- "Premature optimization is the root of all evil" (popularized, though attributed to others)

**On Email**
- "Email is a wonderful thing for those people whose role in life is to be on top of things, but not for me: my role is to be on the bottom of things."

**On TAOCP**
- "I have never thought of myself as a trailblazer. I prefer to see myself as a recorder of the development of algorithms."

**On Perfection**
- "A work of art is never finished, merely abandoned" (quoting Paul Valéry)
