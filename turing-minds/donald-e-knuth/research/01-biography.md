# Donald E. Knuth - Biography

## Early Life (1938-1956)

**Birth and Family**
- Born January 10, 1938, in Milwaukee, Wisconsin
- Father: Ervin Knuth, a high school teacher and church organist who later owned a small printing business
- Mother: Louise Bohning Knuth
- Grew up in a Lutheran family

**The Ziegler Contest (Age 12, 1950)**
- Won a national word puzzle contest sponsored by Ziegler's Candies
- Found over 4,500 words from the letters in "Ziegler's Giant Bar"
- Contest judges only had 2,500 words on their master list
- Prize: Television set for his school, candy bars for entire class
- Feigned illness for two weeks to pore over a dictionary

**High School**
- Attended Milwaukee Lutheran High School
- Graduated with highest GPA in school history
- Played saxophone and tuba in school band
- Strong interest in music composition
- Loved grammar and diagramming sentences

## Education

**Case Institute of Technology (1956-1960)**
- Initially majored in physics (contemplated music)
- Switched to mathematics, finding lab work less appealing than theory
- First encounter with IBM 650 computer - read the manual thoroughly
- Rewrote assembler and compiler code, believing he could improve it
- Created basketball player evaluation program (1958) - featured on CBS Evening News
- Bachelor of Science in Mathematics (1960)
- Awarded simultaneous Master's degree for outstanding undergraduate work

**Caltech (1960-1963)**
- PhD in Mathematics (1963) at age 25
- Thesis: "Finite Semifields and Projective Planes"
- Assistant and Associate Professor at Caltech (1963-1968)
- Worked as consultant for Burroughs Corporation during summers

## Career

**The Art of Computer Programming Genesis (1962)**
- Addison-Wesley approached him to write a book on compilers
- Realized computer science literature was sparse and uneven
- Expanded scope to fundamental programming techniques
- Planned 12 chapters, reorganized into 7 volumes

**Stanford University (1968-present)**
- Joined faculty in 1968 as Professor of Computer Science
- Move motivated partly by Stanford agreeing to hire colleague Robert Floyd
- Special Professor (1977)
- Professor Emeritus (1993) - retired from teaching to focus on writing

**TeX and Digital Typography (1977-1990)**
- Disappointed with computerized typesetting quality for TAOCP revisions
- Began developing TeX typesetting system
- Expected project to take one year; took over a decade

## Personal Life

**Music and Pipe Organ**
- Member of American Guild of Organists since 1965
- Commissioned custom pipe organ "Opus 67" from Abbott and Sieker
- 812 pipes, 16 stops, North German Baroque tradition
- Funded by TAOCP Volume 3 royalties
- Composed "Fantasia Apocalyptica" in retirement
- Owns Bösendorfer grand piano

**Current Life (Age 87+)**
- Lives on Stanford campus
- Still writes several computer programs each week
- Refuses to use email: "Email is a wonderful thing for those people whose role in life is to be on top of things, but not for me: my role is to be on the bottom of things"
- Bicycles to campus and swims regularly
- Continues working on TAOCP Volume 4 fascicles

## Philosophy

- "I have never thought of myself as a trailblazer. I prefer to see myself as a recorder of the development of algorithms."
- Deep commitment to precision and beauty in both mathematics and typography
