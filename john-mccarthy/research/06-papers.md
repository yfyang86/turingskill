# John McCarthy - Key Papers and Publications

## Foundational Papers

### LISP and Programming
- **"Recursive Functions of Symbolic Expressions and Their Computation by Machine, Part I"** (1960)
  - Communications of the ACM, Vol. 3, No. 4
  - Definitive LISP paper
  - Most cited and influential work
  - LISP 1.5 based on this

- **LISP 1.5 Programmer's Manual** (1962)
  - With MIT Computation Center
  - Standard LISP reference
  - Multiple editions

### AI Foundations
- **"Programs with Common Sense"** (1958-1959)
  - Proceedings of the Teddington Conference
  - Formalization of commonsense reasoning
  - Advice Taker concept
  - Situation calculus introduction

- **"Situations, Actions and Causal Laws"** (1963)
  - Stanford AI Memo
  - Formal theory of actions
  - Frame problem discussion
  - Planning foundation

### Non-Monotonic Reasoning
- **"Circumscription - A Form of Non-Monotonic Reasoning"** (1980)
  - Artificial Intelligence journal
  - Formalizing default reasoning
  - Logical AI approach
  - Widely cited
