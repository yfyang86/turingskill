# John McCarthy - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** John McCarthy
- **Born:** September 4, 1927, Boston, Massachusetts, USA
- **Died:** October 24, 2011, Stanford, California, USA
- **Nationality:** American
- **Turing Award Year:** 1971

## Education

- **Caltech:**
  - B.S. in Mathematics, 1948
  - Early interest in mathematics and logic
- **Princeton University:**
  - Ph.D. in Mathematics, 1951
  - Thesis: "Projection Operators and Partial Differential Equations"
  - Worked on mathematical logic

## Career Timeline

- **1951-1953:** Instructor, Princeton University
- **1953-1955:** Assistant Professor, Stanford University
- **1955-1958:** Assistant Professor, Dartmouth College
- **1956:** Co-organized Dartmouth AI Conference
- **1958-1962:** Assistant Professor, MIT
  - 1958: Co-founded MIT AI Laboratory with Marvin Minsky
- **1962-2000:** Professor, Stanford University
  - 1965: Founded Stanford AI Laboratory (SAIL)
  - Director of SAIL for many years
- **2000-2011:** Professor Emeritus, Stanford

## Major Achievements

- **Coined "Artificial Intelligence"** (1956)
- **Invented LISP** (1958) - second oldest high-level language still in use
- **Time-sharing systems** - early pioneer
- **Garbage collection** - automatic memory management
- **Formalization of commonsense reasoning**
- **Situation calculus** for AI reasoning
- **Circumscription** for non-monotonic reasoning

## Personal Characteristics

- Brilliant logician and mathematician
- Original, independent thinker
- Politically engaged (libertarian views)
- Interest in the future of humanity
- Pioneer of internet and cloud computing concepts
- Known for clear, logical reasoning

## Political and Social Views

- Libertarian political philosophy
- Advocate for human space colonization
- Interest in sustainability and resources
- Futurist thinking about technology
- Blogged about political and technical topics
