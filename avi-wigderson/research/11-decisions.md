# Avi Wigderson: Key Decisions

## 1. Focusing on Randomness (1980s)

**Decision**: Make randomness in computation a central research focus.

**Context**: Not a mainstream topic at the time.

**Impact**: Became the defining theme of his career.

## 2. Joining Hebrew University (1986)

**Decision**: Return to Israel to build theoretical CS group.

**Alternative**: Stay in US at stronger established department.

**Impact**: Established Israel as TCS center; built research community.

## 3. Joining IAS (1999)

**Decision**: Move to Institute for Advanced Study.

**Rationale**: Ideal environment for deep research.

**Impact**: Prestigious platform; host international researchers.

## 4. Sustained Collaboration with Nisan

**Decision**: Long-term partnership on pseudorandomness.

**Result**: Nisan-Wigderson generator.

**Impact**: Foundation of derandomization theory.

## 5. Broad Collaboration Strategy

**Decision**: Work with nearly 200 co-authors across many areas.

**Alternative**: Focus on solo or few-key partnerships.

**Impact**: Breadth of contributions; community building.

## 6. Writing the Book (2019)

**Decision**: Invest time in comprehensive book.

**Title**: *Mathematics and Computation*

**Impact**: Comprehensive resource; bridge math and CS.

## Decision-Making Patterns

### Depth and Breadth
- Deep focus on specific problems (randomness)
- Broad connections across areas
- Balance of specialization and integration

### Collaboration
- Extensive co-authorship
- Sustained key partnerships
- Generous credit sharing

### Environment Building
- Hebrew University: Built research group
- IAS: Hosts international community
- Mentorship: Trains next generation

## Key Trade-offs

| Decision | Alternative | Chosen Path |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Hebrew University | Top US department | Build in Israel |
| IAS | Traditional university | Research freedom |
| Broad collaboration | Focused solo work | Extensive partnerships |
| Book writing | More papers | Comprehensive synthesis |

## Lessons from Wigderson's Decisions

1. **Follow fundamental questions**: Randomness was the right focus
2. **Build communities**: Individual success + collective impact
3. **Collaborate generously**: 200 co-authors multiply impact
4. **Bridge fields**: Math-CS connections create unique value
5. **Write for the ages**: Book synthesizes lifetime knowledge

## Overall Pattern

Wigderson's career decisions reflect:
- Commitment to fundamental understanding
- Belief in community and collaboration
- Willingness to build rather than join
- Long-term thinking over quick wins

These decisions enabled:
- Groundbreaking research
- Unique dual recognition (Abel + Turing)
- Lasting institutional impact
- Hundreds of trained researchers
