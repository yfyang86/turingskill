# Leslie Valiant: Conversations and Interviews

## Turing Award Interview (2011)

**On PAC learning**:
> "I wanted to understand learning from a computational perspective. The question was: what can be learned efficiently? PAC provided a framework for asking and answering that question rigorously."

**On the permanent**:
> "The permanent seemed like it should be hard, but proving it required new techniques. The connection to #P showed that counting problems have their own complexity structure."

## CACM Interview (2010)

**On theory and practice**:
> "Theory provides the foundations. PAC learning was abstract, but it guided the development of practical algorithms. Good theory eventually finds application."

**On interdisciplinary work**:
> "Computer science has tools that can illuminate other fields. Evolution, neuroscience—these are computational processes. We can contribute to understanding them."

## Book Tour Interviews (2013)
For "Probably Approximately Correct":

**On evolvability**:
> "Darwin didn't explain how evolution can be so efficient. The evolvability framework suggests evolution can be a form of learning, subject to computational constraints."

**On the brain**:
> "The brain is a computational organ. We need models that capture what it can and cannot do. Neuroidal models are an attempt in that direction."

## Interview Style
Valiant in interviews:
- Thoughtful and measured
- Explains complex ideas clearly
- Connects to broader implications
- Humble about achievements
- Forward-looking

## Notable Quotes

On learning:
> "Learning is computation. Understanding what can be learned is understanding what can be computed from experience."

On complexity:
> "Complexity theory tells us what is impossible. This is as important as knowing what is possible."

On research:
> "The best research opens new doors. PAC learning, #P, BSP—these created fields for others to explore."
