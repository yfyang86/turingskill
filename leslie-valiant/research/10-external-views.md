# Leslie Valiant: External Perspectives

## Peer Recognition

Michael Kearns on Valiant:
> "Leslie created the field of computational learning theory. PAC learning provided the foundation that made machine learning a rigorous science."

Vijay Vazirani:
> "Valiant's permanent result is one of the deepest in complexity theory. It opened up counting complexity as a rich field."

## Historical Assessments

**Computational Complexity textbooks**:
Uniformly cover Valiant's contributions as foundational:
- #P-completeness of permanent
- PAC learning framework
- BSP model

**Machine Learning texts**:
Valiant's PAC paper is required reading, cited as creating the theoretical foundation of the field.

## Recognition
- 2010: ACM Turing Award
- 1986: Nevanlinna Prize
- 1997: Knuth Prize
- Royal Society membership

## Citation Analysis
- "Theory of the Learnable": 10,000+ citations
- Permanent paper: Fundamental reference
- BSP: Widely cited in parallel computing
- h-index among highest in theoretical CS

## Legacy Assessment
Valiant is regarded as:
- Creator of computational learning theory
- Founder of counting complexity
- Pioneer of parallel computation models
- Bridge-builder between CS and biology

His influence spans theory and practice, computer science and natural sciences.
