# Leslie Valiant: Selected Writings

## Books

### "Probably Approximately Correct: Nature's Algorithms for Learning and Prospering in a Complex World" (2013)
**Publisher**: Basic Books
**Content**: Accessible exposition of:
- PAC learning foundations
- Evolvability and evolution
- Neuroidal models of brain
- Unified theory of learning in nature

**Significance**: Made Valiant's theories accessible to general readers.

## Turing Award Lecture

### "The Extent and Limitations of Mechanism"
**Venue**: ACM Turing Award Lecture, 2011
**Published**: Communications of the ACM

**Themes**:
- What can be computed efficiently
- Learning as computational phenomenon
- Evolution as learning
- Limits of mechanistic explanation

## Key Papers (as Writings)

### "A Theory of the Learnable" (1984)
Beyond research contribution, this paper is a model of scientific writing:
- Clear motivation
- Precise definitions
- Elegant theorems
- Visionary implications

### "Evolvability" (2009)
Demonstrates Valiant's writing style:
- Biological motivation
- Formal definition
- Theoretical analysis
- Biological implications

## Writing Characteristics
Valiant's writing:
- **Precision**: Exact definitions
- **Clarity**: Complex ideas made accessible
- **Elegance**: Beautiful theorem statements
- **Vision**: Connecting to broader questions

## Archival Sources
Valiant's papers available through:
- ACM Digital Library
- arXiv (recent work)
- Harvard repositories
- Publisher archives

## Influence on Scientific Writing
Valiant influenced how theoretical CS papers are written:
- Formal definitions first
- Clear theorem statements
- Proof sketches with intuition
- Discussion of implications

His writing style became a model for the field.
