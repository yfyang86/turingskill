# Tim Berners-Lee: Interviews and Public Conversations

## TED Talks

### "The Next Web" (2009)
Berners-Lee's first TED talk presented the Linked Data vision, calling for raw data to be shared openly. He used the memorable phrase "raw data now" to encourage governments and organizations to release structured data. The talk demonstrated his evolution from web inventor to data activist.

### "The Year Open Data Went Worldwide" (2010)
Reflecting on open government data initiatives, Berners-Lee discussed how raw data releases were creating new applications and transparency. He emphasized that data about how governments spend money belongs to citizens.

### "The Next Web of Open, Linked Data" (2009)
An earlier exploration of the Semantic Web vision, explaining how linking structured data creates value beyond individual datasets.

### "The Future of the Web" (2014)
Berners-Lee addressed growing concerns about surveillance, net neutrality, and the need for a more resilient, open web infrastructure.

## Major Interviews

A series of lectures and interviews where Berners-Lee reflected on:

- The concentration of power in tech platforms

## Characteristic Themes in Conversations

Across interviews, Berners-Lee consistently emphasizes:
- The web as a public good requiring stewardship
- Concern about unintended negative consequences
- Optimism about technical solutions (Solid)
- The importance of maintaining net neutrality
- Urgency around data privacy and user control

His conversational style is thoughtful, measured, and accessible—avoiding technical jargon while maintaining intellectual depth.
