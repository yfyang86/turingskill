# Charles P. Thacker: Key Collaborators

## Xerox PARC Team (1970-1983)
Thacker thrived in PARC's collaborative environment:

### Butler Lampson
- Co-designed the Alto hardware and software
- Shared vision for personal computing
- Collaborated on multiple PARC systems
- Joint influence on modern computing

### Robert Metcalfe
- Co-invented Ethernet with Thacker's hardware contributions
- Metcalfe focused on protocol; Thacker on implementation
- Collaborated on network architecture
- Remained friends and colleagues for decades

### Alan Kay
- Kay envisioned the Dynabook; Thacker built the Alto to realize it
- Kay's software (Smalltalk) ran on Thacker's hardware
- Collaborative refinement of graphical interface concepts
- Shared commitment to personal computing vision

### Chuck Geschke and John Warnock
- InterPress (predecessor to PostScript) development
- Laser printer hardware-software co-design
- Later founded Adobe Systems

## DEC Systems Research Center (1983-1997)
At DEC, Thacker collaborated with:
- **Chuck Thacker** (wait, that's him) - DEC SRC team
- Systems researchers on Alpha architecture
- Networking researchers
- Graphics and display specialists

## Microsoft Research (1997-2017)
At Microsoft, Thacker worked with:
- **Gordon Bell**: Architecture research
- **Jim Gray**: Database and systems (Turing Award 1998)
- **Table PC team**: Hardware-software integration
- **Architecture group**: x86 and ARM systems

## Academic Collaborations
Thacker maintained academic connections:
- **MIT**: Visited and collaborated on hardware projects
- **Stanford**: Connections through Silicon Valley
- **University of Washington**: Microsoft Research collaborations
- **Caltech**: Alumni connections and recruiting

## Students and Mentees
Thacker mentored hardware engineers who became leaders:
- At PARC: Engineers who joined Apple, Sun, Silicon Graphics
- At DEC: Alpha processor team members
- At Microsoft: Hardware architects and researchers

## Collaboration Style
Thacker's approach:
- **Team player**: Shared credit generously
- **Mentor**: Developed junior engineers
- **Cross-functional**: Bridged hardware and software
- **Long-term relationships**: Sustained connections over decades

## Notable Partnerships
- **Lampson-Kay-Thacker**: The PARC triad that created modern computing
- **Metcalfe-Thacker**: Ethernet hardware-software partnership
- **DEC SRC team**: Advanced workstation development
- **Microsoft Research**: Late-career innovation

Thacker's collaborative nature amplified his individual brilliance—the Alto, Ethernet, and other achievements were team efforts that his engineering made possible.
