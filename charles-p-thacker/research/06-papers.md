# Charles P. Thacker: Key Publications

## Foundational Papers

### "Alto: A Personal Computer"
**Authors**: Butler W. Lampson, Charles P. Thacker
**Venue**: Computer Structures: Principles and Examples, 1982
**Significance**: Definitive description of the Alto hardware architecture. Established the blueprint for personal computer design.

### "Personal Distributed Computing: The Alto and Ethernet Software"
**Authors**: Butler W. Lampson, David D. Redell
**Note**: Thacker's hardware enabled this software work
**Venue**: Same volume, 1982
**Significance**: Complete description of the PARC personal computing environment.

## Hardware Design Documentation

### "The Dorado: A High-Performance Personal Computer"
**Authors**: Charles P. Thacker, et al.
**Venue**: IEEE Computer, 1982
**Significance**: Description of the high-performance Alto successor using ECL technology.

### "The Dragon: A High-Performance Document Processor"
**Authors**: Charles P. Thacker
**Venue**: Xerox PARC Technical Report
**Significance**: Design of the laser printer controller, predecessor to PostScript.

## Ethernet and Networking

### "The Ethernet: A Local Area Network"
**Authors**: Robert M. Metcalfe, David R. Boggs, Charles P. Thacker, Butler W. Lampson
**Venue**: Communications of the ACM, 1976
**Significance**: Foundational paper on Ethernet. Thacker contributed hardware implementation details.

### "Ethernet: Distributed Packet Switching for Local Computer Networks"
**Venue**: CACM 1976
**Significance**: Complete description of Ethernet concept and implementation.

## Later Work

### "The Microsoft Beehive: A Research Multiprocessor"
**Authors**: Charles P. Thacker
**Venue**: Microsoft Research Technical Report, 2000s
**Significance**: Thacker's late-career hardware research at Microsoft.

### "Toward a Personal Computer"
**Authors**: Charles P. Thacker
**Venue**: Various retrospective talks and writings
**Significance**: Thacker's reflections on the Alto development.

## Turing Award Lecture

### "How the Personal Computer Came to Be"
**Author**: Charles P. Thacker
**Venue**: ACM Turing Award Lecture, 2010
**Published**: Communications of the ACM
**Content**: Historical retrospective on Alto development, PARC culture, and the evolution of personal computing.

## Publication Characteristics
Thacker's publications:
- Focused on system descriptions
- Detailed technical documentation
- Collaborative with PARC colleagues
- Practical rather than theoretical

## Archival Sources
Additional material in:
- Xerox PARC archives

- Microsoft Research publications

## Citation Impact
Thacker's papers:
- Cited in computer architecture texts
- Referenced in HCI literature
- Foundation for networking courses
- Historical documentation of computing

His written legacy is in working systems as much as papers.
