# Michael O. Rabin: Key Publications

## Foundational Papers

### "Finite Automata and Their Decision Problems" (1959)
**Authors**: Michael O. Rabin and Dana Scott
**Publication**: IBM Journal of Research and Development, 3(2):114-125
**Significance**: Turing Award-winning paper introducing nondeterministic finite automata and proving equivalence to deterministic automata. Foundation of automata theory.

### "Probabilistic Automata" (1963)
**Publication**: Information and Control, 6:230-245
**Significance**: Introduced probabilistic automata, foundation for randomized algorithms.

### "Probabilistic Algorithm for Testing Primality" (1980)
**Publication**: Journal of Number Theory, 12(1):128-138
**Significance**: The Miller-Rabin primality test. One of the most important algorithms in cryptography. Enables efficient generation of large primes.

## Cryptography Papers

### "Digitalized Signatures and Public-Key Functions" (1979)
**Publication**: MIT/LCS/TR-212
**Significance**: Rabin cryptosystem; first provably secure public-key encryption.

### "How to Exchange Secrets by Oblivious Transfer" (1981)
**Technical Report**: Harvard University
**Significance**: Foundation for secure multiparty computation; privacy-preserving protocols.

### "Hyper-Encryption and Everlasting Security" (2002)
**Authors**: Aumann, Rabin, Ding
**Significance**: Provably unbreakable encryption using shared randomness.

## Algorithm Papers

### "Efficient Randomized Pattern-Matching Algorithms" (1987)
**Authors**: Richard M. Karp and Michael O. Rabin
**Publication**: IBM Journal of R&D, 31(2):249-260
**Significance**: Rabin-Karp string matching algorithm. Uses hashing for efficient pattern search.

### "Probabilistic Algorithms in Finite Fields" (1980)
**Significance**: Randomized algorithms for algebraic problems.

## Distributed Computing

### "Randomized Byzantine Generals" (1983)
**Authors**: Michael Ben-Or and Michael Rabin
**Significance**: Fault-tolerant randomized consensus protocols.

### "Verifiable Secret Sharing and Multiparty Protocols" (1989)
**Authors**: Rabin, Ben-Or
**Significance**: Secure distributed computation foundations.

## Complexity and Logic

### "Decidability of Second-Order Theories and Automata on Infinite Trees" (1969)
**Publication**: Transactions of the AMS, 141:1-35
**Significance**: Rabin's theorem on tree automata; profound impact on logic and verification.

### "Degrees of Difficulty of Computing a Function" (1960)
**Significance**: Early work on computational complexity hierarchies.

## Turing Award Lecture

### "Complexity of Computations" (1976)
**Significance**: Articulated the importance of computational complexity and probabilistic methods.

## Recent Work

### "Reflexivity of a Banach Space with Countable Vector Space Basis" (2022)
**Significance**: Active research in functional analysis at age 91.

- Mathematical elegance
