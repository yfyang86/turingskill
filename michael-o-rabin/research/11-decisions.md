# Michael O. Rabin: Key Decisions

## 1. Emigrating to Palestine (1935)
Family decision escaping Nazism - shaped his international perspective.

## 2. Princeton PhD (1953-1957)
US education exposed him to leading mathematical environment.

## 3. IBM Summer with Scott (1957)
Collaboration producing automata foundations and Turing Award.

## 4. Returning to Israel (1958)
Commitment to building Israeli academia despite opportunities abroad.

## 5. Hebrew University Leadership (1970s)
Serving as Rector while maintaining research.

## 6. MIT/Harvard Expansion (1975-1981)
Adding US position while keeping Israel ties.

## 7. Primality Testing (1975)
Probabilistic approach that transformed cryptography.

## 8. Cryptography Focus (1980s-90s)
Applied theoretical work to security problems.

## 9. Distributed Computing (1980s-2000s)
New area with Ben-Or, extending impact.

## 10. Continuing Research (2020s)
Active mathematics research at age 90+.

## Decision Pattern
Rabin consistently:
- Followed mathematical elegance
- Pursued fundamental problems
- Maintained international presence
- Combined theory with applications
- Sustained productivity over decades
