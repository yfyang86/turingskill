# James H. Wilkinson - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** James Hardy Wilkinson
- **Born:** September 27, 1919, Strood, Kent, England
- **Died:** October 5, 1986, Teddington, Middlesex, England
- **Nationality:** British
- **Turing Award Year:** 1970

## Education

- **Sir Joseph Williamson's Mathematical School, Rochester:** Scholarship (1930)
- **Trinity College, Cambridge:**
  - Scholar at Trinity College
  - B.A. in Mathematics, 1939
  - War interrupted graduate studies
- **University of Cambridge:**
  - Research in classical analysis
- **Military Service:** World War II (1940-1946)

## Career Timeline

- **1940-1946:** Military service in UK Government
  - Army and then Ministry of Supply
  - Worked on ballistics and mathematical tables
- **1946:** Joined National Physical Laboratory (NPL)
- **1946-1980:** Chief Scientific Officer, NPL
- **1957-1986:** Visiting Professor, various universities
- **1977-1986:** Professor and Head of Numerical Analysis Group, NPL
- **1980-1986:** Senior Visiting Fellow, Stanford University (summers)

## Major Achievements

- Co-invented backward error analysis
- Fundamental contributions to numerical linear algebra
- Algorithms for eigenvalue problems
- Wilkinson's polynomial (numerical stability)
- Organized numerical analysis community
- Built NPL numerical analysis group
- Standardized numerical software

## Personal Characteristics

- Brilliant numerical analyst
- Exceptionally clear expositor
- Patient teacher and mentor
- Bridge between theory and computation
- Modest despite major contributions
- Collaborative and generous

## Military Service

- **1940-1943:** Royal Army Service Corps
- **1943-1946:** Mathematics Division, Ministry of Supply
- Worked with Leslie Fox, other mathematicians
- Began work on numerical methods
- Computed ballistics tables
