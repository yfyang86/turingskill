# David A. Patterson - Timeline

## 1940s-1970s: Early Life and Education

### 1947
- **November 16:** Born in Evergreen Park, Illinois

### 1969
- **Bachelor's degree:** UCLA
- **Major:** Mathematics

### 1970
- **Master's degree:** UCLA
- **Focus:** Computer science

### 1976
- **Ph.D.:** UCLA
- **Thesis:** Programming languages
- **Advisor:** David Martin

## 1976-2016: Berkeley Career

### 1976
- **Joined UC Berkeley**
- **Position:** Assistant Professor of Computer Science

### 1980
- **RISC research begins**
- **Inspiration:** Simplified architecture

### 1981
- **Sabbatical at DEC:** Western Research Lab
- **Influence:** Microprocessor design

### 1982
- **"The Case for RISC" published**
- **Co-author:** David Ditzel
- **Impact:** Sparked revolution

### 1982
- **RISC I completed:** First working prototype
- **Team:** Graduate students

### 1984
- **SPARC architecture:** Sun Microsystems collaboration
- **Commercial success:** Validated RISC

### 1988
- **RAID paper published**
- **Co-authors:** Gibson, Katz
- **Conference:** ACM SIGMOD
- **Impact:** Storage revolution

### 1990
- **"Computer Architecture" published**
- **Co-author:** John L. Hennessy
- **Became:** Standard text

### 1990s
- **CACM Editor-in-Chief**
- **Tenure:** 1990s
- **Impact:** Improved publication

### 1994
- **"Computer Organization and Design" published**
- **Co-author:** John L. Hennessy

### 2002
- **Recovery-Oriented Computing:** ROC project
- **Focus:** Failure recovery

### 2004
- **Computer History Museum interview**
- **Oral history:** Career documentation

### 2010
- **RISC-V project begins**
- **Context:** Open ISA needed
- **Team:** Krste Asanović and students

### 2014
- **RISC-V Foundation created**
- **Goal:** Formal governance

### 2016
- **Retired from Berkeley**
- **Status:** Professor Emeritus
- **Service:** 40 years

## 2016-2020: Google Years

### 2016
- **Joined Google**
- **Title:** Distinguished Engineer
- **Focus:** Warehouse-scale computing

### 2017
- **March 21:** **ACM A.M. Turing Award announced**
- **Shared with:** John L. Hennessy
- **Citation:** "For systematic, quantitative approach"

### 2018
- **Turing Award Lecture**
- **Co-presenter:** Hennessy

### 2020
- **Left Google**
- **Reason:** Focus on RISC-V
