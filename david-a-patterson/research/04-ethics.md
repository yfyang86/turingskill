# David A. Patterson: Ethics and Professional Responsibility

## Academic Integrity in Research

### Quantitative Rigor
Patterson insisted on honest evaluation:
- Avoiding benchmark selection bias
- Reporting negative results
- Distinguishing simulation from measurement
- Reproducible research practices

### Attributing Credit
- Sharing recognition with collaborators
- Acknowledging students' contributions
- Citing prior work properly
- Recognizing parallel independent work

## The RISC Debate Ethics

### Honest Engagement
During the RISC vs. CISC debates:
- Acknowledging valid points on both sides
- Correcting errors when found
- Distinguishing architectural from implementation factors
- Admitting when predictions proved wrong

### Intellectual Honesty
- Changing positions when evidence warranted
- Acknowledging CISC achievements (Intel's success)
- Recognizing that RISC principles were adopted widely, not just in RISC processors
- Honest assessment of limitations
