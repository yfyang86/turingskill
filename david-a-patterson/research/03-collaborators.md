# David A. Patterson: Key Collaborators

## John Hennessy (Stanford University, 1980s-Present)
Patterson's most significant collaboration has been with John Hennessy, spanning the RISC revolution and the influential architecture textbook.

### The RISC Collaboration
- Independent but parallel development of RISC architectures at Berkeley (RISC) and Stanford (MIPS)
- Cross-fertilization of ideas through publications and conferences
- Healthy competition that advanced the field
- Joint Turing Award recognizing both contributions

### The Textbook Partnership
"Computer Architecture: A Quantitative Approach":
- Multiple editions over three decades
- Combined Berkeley and Stanford perspectives
- Created unified approach to architecture education
- Became the standard reference in the field

## Randy Katz (UC Berkeley, 1980s-1990s)
Katz was Patterson's key collaborator on the RAID project.

### The RAID Collaboration
- Joint development of Redundant Arrays of Inexpensive Disks concept
- Co-authors of seminal RAID papers
- Combination of Patterson's systems perspective with Katz's expertise
- Creation of storage paradigm used worldwide

## Garth Gibson (Carnegie Mellon, 1980s)
Gibson was the graduate student who worked with Patterson and Katz on RAID.

### The RAID Team
- Gibson's Ph.D. research on RAID
- The three-way collaboration that created the RAID taxonomy
- Gibson's subsequent leadership in storage research at CMU

## David Ditzel (Various, 1980s)
Ditzel collaborated with Patterson on articulating the case for RISC.

### "The Case for the Reduced Instruction Set Computer"
- Influential paper arguing for RISC approach
- Helped frame the RISC vs. CISC debate
- Articulated quantitative arguments for simplicity
