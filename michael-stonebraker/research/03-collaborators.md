# Michael Stonebraker: Key Collaborators

## Eugene Wong (UC Berkeley, 1970s)
Wong was Stonebraker's original collaborator on the Ingres project. As a colleague in the Electrical Engineering and Computer Science department at Berkeley, Wong shared Stonebraker's interest in database systems. Their partnership established the foundation for Ingres, with Wong contributing significantly to the theoretical underpinnings and system architecture. The Wong-Stonebraker collaboration demonstrated how academic partnerships could produce industrial-strength software.

## Larry Rowe (UC Berkeley, 1980s)
Rowe worked closely with Stonebraker on both Ingres and Postgres. His contributions to the Postgres project were particularly significant in developing the extensible type system and rules capabilities. Rowe later joined the faculty at Berkeley and continued database research. Their collaboration exemplified the mentorship and knowledge transfer that characterized Stonebraker's Berkeley years.

## The Postgres Team (1986-1994)
The Postgres project involved numerous graduate students and researchers who became leaders in their own right:
- **Wei Hong**: Query processing and optimization
- **Jeff Meredith**: Storage manager development
- **Jolly Chen**: Rule system implementation
- **Andrew Yu**: Query language design

Many Postgres team members went on to successful careers in academia and industry, carrying forward Stonebraker's approach to database research.

## Commercial Collaborators
Stonebraker's entrepreneurial ventures involved partnerships with:
- **Robert Epstein** (Sybase): Former Ingres team member who commercialized the system
- **Informix leadership**: Following the Illustra acquisition
- **Various venture capitalists**: Funding for multiple database startups

These partnerships required translating academic research into commercially viable products, often navigating the tension between research ideals and market realities.

## MIT CSAIL Colleagues (2001-Present)
At MIT, Stonebraker collaborated with:
- **Samuel Madden**: Database systems research, particularly stream processing and sensor networks
- **Daniel Abadi**: Column-store research (C-Store) leading to Vertica
- **Stan Zdonik**: Stream processing systems (Aurora, Borealis)
- **David DeWitt**: Parallel database systems (though primarily at Wisconsin, maintained ongoing collaboration)

The MIT environment provided opportunities for cross-disciplinary collaboration, particularly with machine learning researchers for projects like Tamr.

## Academic Competitors and Critics
Stonebraker's career was marked by productive intellectual competition:
- **Jim Gray**: Microsoft's database researcher; their debates about transaction processing and system architecture pushed both to refine their positions
- **David DeWitt**: University of Wisconsin; the "Wisconsin Benchmark" vs. Berkeley Ingres era was competitive but collegial
- **The SQL Standardization Community**: Stonebraker's advocacy for QUEL over SQL put him at odds with the IBM-led standardization effort

These competitive relationships ultimately strengthened the field by ensuring diverse perspectives were heard.

## Industry Partnerships
Stonebraker's research was consistently funded by and partnered with industry:
- **Intel Science and Technology Center**: Major funding for big data research at MIT
- **NSF and DARPA**: Government funding for foundational research
- **Various database companies**: Technology transfer and advisory relationships

His ability to bridge academic research and industry application relied on maintaining productive partnerships across both worlds.
