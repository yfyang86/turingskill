# Michael Stonebraker: Other Writings

## Books and Edited Volumes

### "Readings in Database Systems" (Editor)
Stonebraker edited multiple editions of this influential anthology (3rd through 5th editions). The book collected foundational papers in database research and became a standard graduate text. His introductions and commentary provided context and his own perspective on the field's development.

### "Object-Relational DBMSs: The Next Great Wave" (1996)
Co-authored with Dorothy Moore, this book explained the emerging object-relational database category. It positioned products like Illustra (Stonebraker's company) within a technical framework, explaining how object-oriented and relational concepts could be unified.

### Various Technical Reports and White Papers
Stonebraker has produced numerous technical reports from Berkeley and MIT, often serving as preprints for journal papers or documenting system architectures not fully described in conference publications.

## Blog Posts and Online Commentary

## Characteristics of His Writing Style

### Technical Precision
Stonebraker's writing is characterized by precise technical claims backed by implementation experience. He rarely makes theoretical assertions without grounding them in actual system behavior.

### Willingness to Critique
Unlike many academics who avoid direct criticism, Stonebraker explicitly evaluates competing approaches. This has occasionally generated controversy but advances technical discourse.

### System-Building Perspective
His writings consistently emphasize working systems over theoretical possibilities. This reflects his belief that only implemented and evaluated systems can validate architectural claims.

### Entrepreneurial Framing
Particularly in later years, his writings often connect technical architectures to market opportunities, reflecting his dual identity as researcher and entrepreneur.
