# Donald E. Knuth - What Others Say

## Bill Gates

**Famous Quote**
> "If you think you're a really good programmer... read (Knuth's) Art of Computer Programming... You should definitely send me a résumé if you can read the whole thing."

**Context**
- Appears on cover of TAOCP Volume 1 (3rd edition)
- No one has claimed the résumé offer (TAOCP remains incomplete)
- Demonstrates perceived difficulty and importance of work

**Significance**
- Endorsement from world's most prominent technologist
- Validates TAOCP as ultimate programming challenge
- Widely quoted in programming community

## Turing Award Citation (1974)

**Official Citation**
> "The 1974 A.M. Turing Award was presented to Professor Donald E. Knuth of Stanford University for a number of major contributions to analysis of algorithms and the design of programming languages, and in particular for his most significant contributions to the 'art of computer programming' through his series of well-known books. The collections of technique, algorithms, and relevant theory in these books have served as a focal point for developing curricula and as an organizing influence on computer science."

**B. A. Galler's Commentary**
- Emphasized TAOCP's impact on curricula
- Recognized organizing influence on field
- Youngest recipient at age 36 (record still stands)

## Critical Perspectives

**Accessibility Concerns**
- Some find TAOCP too difficult for beginners
- MIX/MMIX criticized as outdated
- Mathematical prerequisites steep

**Counterpoints**
- Intended for serious practitioners, not casual readers
- MMIX updated specifically for modern relevance
- Prerequisites ensure proper foundation

**Publication Pace**
- Gentle teasing about unfinished volumes
- 60+ years and counting
- Counter: quality over speed

- Knuth allegedly replied "You are full of crap"
- Story debunked but illustrates perceived impossibility of task

**Knuth Checks**
- Recipients frame $2.56 checks rather than cash
- "Among computerdom's most prized trophies" — MIT Technology Review
