# Edwin Catmull - Timeline

## 1940s-1960s: Early Life

### 1945
- **March 31:** Born in Parkersburg, West Virginia

### 1950s
- Grew up in Salt Lake City, Utah
- Developed interest in animation watching Disney films

### 1960s
- Enrolled at University of Utah
- Studied physics and computer science
- Influenced by Ivan Sutherland's computer graphics work

## 1970s: Graduate Studies and Early Career

### 1970
- Bachelor's degree in physics and computer science

### 1972
- Created first computer-animated hand and face
- Early CGI demonstration

### 1974
- **Ph.D. in Computer Science from University of Utah**
- Thesis: "A Subdivision Algorithm for Computer Display of Curved Surfaces"
- **Joined Lucasfilm** as Director of Computer Division

### 1979
- Recruited by George Lucas to build graphics group

## 1980s: The RenderMan Era

### 1980
- Digital paint system development begins

### 1982
- Genesis sequence for Star Trek II (first CGI sequence in film)

### 1984
- Lucasfilm CGI group creates "The Adventures of André and Wally B."

### 1986
- **February:** Steve Jobs acquires Computer Division from Lucasfilm
- **February 3:** Pixar officially founded
- Became Chief Technical Officer

### 1988
- "Tin Toy" wins Academy Award for Best Animated Short
- RenderMan released commercially

## 1990s: Feature Animation Revolution

### 1991
- Disney three-picture deal signed

### 1995
- **November 22:** Toy Story released (first computer-animated feature)
- Pixar IPO ($22/share)

### 1998
- A Bug's Life released

### 1999
- Toy Story 2 released

## 2000s: Expansion and Acquisition

### 2001
- **Promoted to President of Pixar Animation Studios**
- Monsters, Inc. released

### 2003
- Finding Nemo released (Academy Award winner)

### 2004
- The Incredibles released

### 2006
- **January 24:** Disney acquires Pixar for $7.4 billion
- **Named President of Disney and Pixar Animation Studios**

### 2007
- Ratatouille released

### 2008
- WALL-E released

### 2009
- Up released (second animated film nominated for Best Picture)
- **Gordon E. Sawyer Award received**

## 2010s: Legacy Building

### 2010
- Toy Story 3 released

### 2012
- Brave released

### 2014
- **Jean Hersholt Humanitarian Award received**
- **Creativity, Inc. published**

### 2015
- Inside Out released

### 2017
- Coco released

### 2018
- **October 23:** Announced retirement from Disney/Pixar

### 2019
- **March 18:** Officially retired
- **March 18:** **ACM A.M. Turing Award announced** (with Pat Hanrahan)

## 2020s: Post-Retirement

### 2020
- Continues advisory and speaking roles

### 2021-Present
- Active in education and mentoring
- Pixar continues under new leadership
