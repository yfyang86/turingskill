# Edwin Catmull - Biography

## Early Life and Education

- **Born:** March 31, 1945, in Parkersburg, West Virginia, USA
- **Grew up in:** Salt Lake City, Utah
- **Childhood dream:** Wanted to be an animator for Disney
- **Realized limitation:** Could not draw well enough for traditional animation

## Academic Journey

- **University of Utah (1960s):**
  - Bachelor's degree in physics and computer science
  - Master's degree in computer science
  - Ph.D. in computer science (1974)
- **Mentor:** Ivan Sutherland, computer graphics pioneer
- **Dissertation:** Focused on texture mapping and rendering algorithms

## Professional Career Timeline

- **1974:** Joined Lucasfilm as director of Computer Division
- **1979:** Recruited by George Lucas to lead computer graphics development
- **1986:** Co-founded Pixar with Steve Jobs and John Lasseter
- **1986-2001:** Chief Technical Officer at Pixar
- **2001-2006:** President of Pixar Animation Studios
- **2006-2018:** President of Walt Disney and Pixar Animation Studios
- **2019:** Retired from Disney

## Key Milestones

- Led development of RenderMan software
- Oversaw creation of first computer-animated feature film (Toy Story, 1995)
- Managed Pixar's IPO and Disney acquisition
- Guided Pixar through 14 consecutive Academy Award-winning films

## Personal Philosophy

- Believes in blending technology and art seamlessly
- Advocates for "fail fast" culture in creative environments
- Emphasizes psychological safety in teams
- Champion of combining technical excellence with storytelling

## Awards and Recognition

- **2019:** ACM A.M. Turing Award (with Pat Hanrahan)
- **2009:** Gordon E. Sawyer Award (Academy Award)
- **2008:** IEEE John von Neumann Medal
- Multiple Academy Awards for technical achievements
- **2014:** Jean Hersholt Humanitarian Award
