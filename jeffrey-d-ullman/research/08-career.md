# Jeffrey D. Ullman - Career Timeline

## Princeton University (1966-1969)
- **Role**: Assistant Professor of Electrical Engineering
- **Age**: 24-27 (exceptionally young)
- **Focus**: Automata theory, switching theory
- **Publications**: Early papers on formal languages
- **Significance**: Foundation of academic career

## Stanford University - First Period (1969-1979)

### Assistant Professor (1969-1970)
- Joined Stanford CS Department
- One of youngest faculty members

### Associate Professor (1970-1975)
- Early tenure
- Building database theory group
- First PhD students supervised

### Full Professor (1975-1979)
- Established researcher
- Database theory leadership
- Textbook writing begins

### Key Achievements (1969-1979)
- **1974**: "Design and Analysis of Computer Algorithms" published
- **1977**: "Principles of Compiler Design" (First Dragon Book)
- **1979**: "Introduction to Automata Theory" published
- Supervised first generation of PhD students

## Bell Labs (1979-1982)
- **Role**: Member of Technical Staff
- **Location**: Murray Hill, New Jersey
- **Collaboration**: Intensified work with Alfred Aho
- **Research**: Compiler theory, database systems
- **Reason**: Sabbatical and research opportunity

## Stanford University - Return (1982-2002)

### Professor (1982-2002)
- Returned to full-time academic position
- Expanded research scope
- Continued textbook writing

### Department Chair (1990-1994)
- Chairman of Computer Science Department
- Stanford CS growth era
- Building faculty and programs
- Fundraising and administration

### Named Chair
- **Stanford W. Ascherman Professor** of Computer Science
- Recognition of research excellence

### Key Achievements (1982-2002)
- **1986**: Dragon Book (Compilers) published
- **1988-1989**: Database Principles books (2 volumes)
- **1997**: "A First Course in Database Systems"
- **2002**: "Database Systems: The Complete Book"
- 30+ PhD students supervised

## Retirement (2002-present)

### Professor Emeritus (2002)
- Stanford W. Ascherman Professor Emeritus
- Continued research and writing
- Focus on online education

### Post-Retirement Activities
- Online course development
- YouTube lectures
- Textbook updates
- Conference participation
