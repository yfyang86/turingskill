# Jeffrey D. Ullman: Key Collaborators

## Alfred V. Aho (1941-)

### Relationship
Co-recipient of the 2020 Turing Award. Met in 1963 at Princeton registration line.

### Collaboration Span
Over 50 years (1963-present), producing:
- Nine textbooks (including multiple editions)
- Foundational compiler theory
- Algorithms research
- Lossless joins paper

### Collaboration Pattern
Despite working at different institutions from 1969 onward:
- Regular meetings to discuss research
- Coordinated book writing schedules
- Shared research interests across decades
- Mutual respect and complementary skills

## John Hopcroft (1939-)

### *The Design and Analysis of Computer Algorithms* (1974)
Co-author with Aho and Ullman on this seminal algorithms textbook.

### Turing Award
Hopcroft won the Turing Award independently in 1986 (with Robert Tarjan).

## Héctor García-Molina (1954-)

### InfoLab Collaboration
Co-director of Stanford InfoLab database research group.

### Joint Work
- Data integration systems
- Distributed database research
- Semistructured data management

## Jennifer Widom

### InfoLab Collaboration
Co-director of Stanford InfoLab.

### Joint Work
- Database theory applications
- Data stream management
- Data cleaning and integration

## Catriel Beeri (1949-)

### Lossless Joins Paper (1979)
Co-author of "The Theory of Joins in Relational Databases" with Aho and Ullman.

### Impact
This paper established the theoretical foundation for database normalization.

## Sergey Brin

### PhD Student
Ullman was Brin's PhD advisor at Stanford.

### Famous Outcome
Brin left the PhD program to co-found Google, becoming one of the most famous "dropouts" from Ullman's group.

## Jure Leskovec

### *Mining of Massive Datasets*
Co-author with Ullman and Anand Rajaraman.

### Research
Stanford professor working on network analysis and large-scale data mining.

## Anand Rajaraman

### *Mining of Massive Datasets*
Co-author with Ullman and Leskovec.

### Background
Entrepreneur and academic, co-founder of Junglee and Kosmix.

## Ravi Sethi

### Dragon Book
Co-author of Dragon Book 2nd edition with Aho and Ullman.

## Monica Lam

### Dragon Book
Co-author of Dragon Book 3rd edition with Aho, Ullman, and Sethi.

### Stanford Connection
Professor at Stanford, independent of Ullman's group but collaborator on textbook.

## Notable Students

Ullman has mentored numerous PhD students who became leaders in:
- Database systems
- Distributed computing
- Data mining
- Search engines (Google)

## Collaboration Philosophy

Ullman's collaborative style:
- Long-term sustained partnerships (especially with Aho)
- Mix of industry and academic collaborators
- Credit-sharing on publications
- Willingness to mentor across career stages

## Pattern

Ullman's most significant collaborations:
1. **Aho**: 50+ years, books and theory
2. **Hopcroft**: Algorithms textbook
3. **InfoLab colleagues**: Database systems
4. **Students**: Many successful careers

## Key Insight

Ullman demonstrates that sustained collaboration multiplies impact. His 50-year partnership with Aho produced more than either could have achieved alone, culminating in the shared Turing Award.
