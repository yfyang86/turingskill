# Herbert A. Simon: Scientific Contributions

## Bounded Rationality and Satisficing
Simon's most influential concept challenged economic orthodoxy:

### The Core Argument
Human decision-makers lack:
- Complete information about alternatives
- Unlimited computational capacity
- Consistent preference orderings
- Ability to optimize across all possibilities

Instead, they "satisfice" - seek solutions that are "good enough" rather than optimal.

### Organizational Implications
- Organizations simplify decisions through standard operating procedures
- Search processes are problem-driven rather than comprehensive
- Goals are often discovered through action, not pre-specified
- Rationality is bounded by both individual and organizational constraints

### Impact on Economics
Simon's work anticipated and influenced:
- Behavioral economics (Kahneman, Tversky, Thaler)
- Transaction cost economics (Williamson)
- Evolutionary economics (Nelson, Winter)
- Contract theory and mechanism design

## Artificial Intelligence Foundations

### The Logic Theorist and GPS
With Newell, Simon developed:
- **Heuristic search**: Using problem-specific knowledge
- **Means-ends analysis**: Reducing differences between current and goal states
- **Protocol analysis**: Studying human thinking through verbal reports
- **Cognitive simulation**: Building programs that think like humans

### The Physical Symbol Systems Hypothesis
Newell and Simon's central claim:
"A physical symbol system has the necessary and sufficient means for general intelligent action."

This provided theoretical foundation for symbolic AI and cognitive science.

## Problem-Solving Research

### "Human Problem Solving" (1972)
The comprehensive study with Newell:
- Protocols of humans solving problems
- GPS as simulation model
- Discovery of general problem-solving strategies
- Understanding of expertise development

### Key Findings
- Experts organize knowledge differently than novices
- Problem representation is crucial
- Search is guided by heuristics, not brute force
- Learning occurs through chunking and pattern recognition

## Administrative Behavior and Organization Theory

### "Administrative Behavior" (1947)
Simon's first major work introduced:
- **Fact-value distinction**: Separating factual and ethical judgments
- **Bounded rationality**: Cognitive limits on decision-making
- **Satisficing**: Seeking adequate rather than optimal solutions
- **Attention focus**: Organizations as systems of attention allocation

### Influence on Management Science
- Challenge to classical management theory
- Foundation of behavioral theory of the firm
- Influence on strategic management
- Organizational decision-making research

## Scientific Discovery

### BACON and Scientific Reasoning
Simon developed programs that:
- Rediscovered scientific laws (Bacon, Dalton, etc.)
- Simulated theory formation
- Demonstrated discovery as heuristic search
- Showed machine discovery was possible

### Philosophy of Science Implications
- Discovery has logic, not just psychology
- Heuristics guide scientific inference
- Computational models of scientific reasoning
- AI as tool for studying scientific creativity

## Cognitive Science

### Psychology of Thinking
Simon's research established:
- Protocol analysis as rigorous method
- Computational models of cognition
- Expertise studies across domains
- Memory and learning mechanisms

### The Sciences of the Artificial (1969)
His book argued for:
- Design as central to engineering and social sciences
- Artificial systems as proper subject of science
- Decomposition and near-decomposability
- Complexity and hierarchical organization

## Social Science Methodology

### Interdisciplinary Approach
Simon advocated:
- Methodological pluralism
- Formal modeling when appropriate
- Empirical testing of models
- Cross-disciplinary problem focus

### Influence on Political Science
- Behavioral revolution foundations
- Rational choice theory (though he critiqued strong versions)
- Organization theory
- Policy analysis methods

## Overall Assessment
Simon's scientific contribution was unique in:
- Spanning natural and social sciences
- Integrating theory, experiment, and computation
- Creating new fields (cognitive science, AI)
- Transforming established disciplines (economics, psychology)

His work demonstrated that rigorous scientific method could address complex human phenomena previously considered beyond formal analysis.
