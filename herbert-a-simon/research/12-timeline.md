# Herbert A. Simon: Timeline

## 1916-1940: Education and Early Career
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1916 | Born June 15, Milwaukee, Wisconsin | Midwest upbringing; father's engineering influence |
| 1933 | Entered University of Chicago | Exposure to interdisciplinary environment |
| 1936 | BA Political Science, Chicago | Political science rather than economics major |
| 1938 | Married Dorothea Pye | Lifelong partnership |
| 1943 | PhD Political Science, Chicago | Dissertation became "Administrative Behavior" |

## 1940-1949: Early Academic Career
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1939-1942 | Research position, UC Berkeley | Early research experience |
| 1942-1949 | Illinois Institute of Technology | Faculty position; continued research |
| 1947 | "Administrative Behavior" published | Foundation of organization theory |

## 1949-1960: Move to Carnegie and AI Founding
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1949 | Joined Carnegie Tech GSIA | Environment for interdisciplinary work |
| 1952 | "Models of Man" published | Collected essays on bounded rationality |
| 1954 | Met Allen Newell at RAND | Began legendary collaboration |
| 1955-1956 | Logic Theorist developed | First AI program |
| 1956 | Dartmouth Conference | AI founded as discipline |
| 1957 | Nobel prediction: Chess in 10 years | Bold forecast (actual: 40 years) |
| 1957-1972 | GPS development with Newell | General Problem Solver |
| 1958 | "Theories of Decision-Making" | AER survey; behavioral economics manifesto |

## 1960-1978: Cognitive Science and Economics
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1962 | "A Programming Language" with Iverson | APL book (pre-implementation) |
| 1969 | "The Sciences of the Artificial" | Design science; near-decomposability |
| 1972 | "Human Problem Solving" with Newell | Magnum opus; cognitive simulation |
| 1975 | ACM Turing Award with Newell | AI recognized by computer science |
| 1975 | "The Psychology of Thinking" | Cognitive psychology contributions |
| 1978 | Nobel Prize in Economics | Recognition for bounded rationality |

## 1978-1992: Nobel Laureate and Continued Work
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1978 | Nobel Lecture: "Rational Decision-Making" | Synthesis of economic contributions |
| 1980s | BACON and discovery programs | Machine discovery research |
| 1982 | "Models of Bounded Rationality" Vol. 1 | Collected economic papers |
| 1984 | "Protocol Analysis" with Ericsson | Methodology book |
| 1986 | National Medal of Science | US highest scientific honor |
| 1987 | "Scientific Discovery" book | Computational philosophy of science |
| 1991 | "Models of My Life" | Autobiography |
| 1992 | Death of Allen Newell | End of 38-year collaboration |

## 1992-2001: Final Years
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1993 | APA Lifetime Achievement Award | Psychology recognition |
| 1994 | Chinese Academy of Sciences | International recognition |
| 1996 | "The Sciences of the Artificial" 3rd ed | Updated classic |
| 1997 | "Models of Bounded Rationality" Vol. 3 | Final collection |
| 2001 | Died February 9, Pittsburgh | End of era; legacy assessment begins |

## Research Phase Summary
| Period | Focus | Major Output |
|--------|-------|--------------|
| 1940s | Organization theory | "Administrative Behavior" |
| 1950s-60s | Behavioral economics | Satisficing, bounded rationality |
| 1955-1972 | AI and cognitive simulation | Logic Theorist, GPS |
| 1970s-1990s | Scientific discovery | BACON, philosophy of science |
| 1980s-2001 | Synthesis and reflection | Autobiography, collections |
