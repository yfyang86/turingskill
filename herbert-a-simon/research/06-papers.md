# Herbert A. Simon: Key Publications

## Foundational Books

### "Administrative Behavior" (1947)
**Publisher**: Macmillan (4th edition 1997)
**Significance**: Simon's first masterpiece, introducing bounded rationality and satisficing. Revolutionized organization theory and laid groundwork for behavioral economics. Based on his Chicago PhD dissertation.

### "Human Problem Solving" (1972)
**Authors**: Newell and Simon
**Publisher**: Prentice-Hall
**Significance**: 920-page comprehensive treatment of GPS and information processing psychology. Detailed theory plus extensive experimental results. The definitive statement of their cognitive simulation approach.

### "The Sciences of the Artificial" (1969)
**Publisher**: MIT Press (3rd edition 1996)
**Significance**: Argues for design as a scientific discipline. Introduces near-decomposability, hierarchical organization, and the artifact as subject of science. Influential across engineering, design research, and organization theory.

### "Models of My Life" (1991)
**Publisher**: Basic Books
**Significance**: Autobiography reflecting on interdisciplinary career. Provides insight into his thinking across economics, AI, psychology, and management. Shows development of his scientific philosophy.

## Economic Theory

### "A Behavioral Model of Rational Choice" (1955)
**Publication**: Quarterly Journal of Economics
**Significance**: Formal statement of satisficing and bounded rationality. Challenged expected utility maximization as descriptive theory.

### "Theories of Decision-Making in Economics and Behavioral Science" (1959)
**Publication**: American Economic Review
**Significance**: Survey showing economic theory's limitations and psychology's relevance. Widely cited as behavioral economics manifesto.

### "Rational Choice and the Structure of the Environment" (1956)
**Publication**: Psychological Review
**Significance**: Bounded rationality applied to psychology. Introduced aspiration levels and adaptive search.

## Artificial Intelligence

### "The Logic Theory Machine" (1956)
**Authors**: Newell and Simon
**Publication**: IRE Transactions on Information Theory
**Significance**: First AI program description. Introduced heuristic search and list processing.

### "GPS: A Program That Simulates Human Thought" (1959)
**Authors**: Newell and Simon
**Significance**: Early comprehensive description of General Problem Solver and cognitive simulation methodology.

### "Computer Science as Empirical Inquiry" (1976)
**Authors**: Newell and Simon
**Publication**: Communications of the ACM (Turing Award lecture)
**Significance**: Physical Symbol Systems Hypothesis. Articulated symbolic AI research program.

### "The Empirical Study of Human Problem Solving" (1970)
**Significance**: Methodological statement on protocol analysis and computational modeling.

## Cognitive Psychology

### "How Big Is a Chunk?" (1974)
**Publication**: Science
**Significance**: Analysis of chunking in expert memory. Quantified expertise and knowledge organization.

### "Expert and Novice Problem Solving" (various papers with Larkin, McDermott, others)
**Significance**: Established expertise research methodology. Found expertise based on pattern recognition and knowledge, not general reasoning ability.

### "Protocol Analysis" (1984, with K.A. Ericsson)
**Publisher**: MIT Press
**Significance**: Definitive methodology for verbal reports as data. Established rigorous standards for think-aloud studies.

## Scientific Discovery

### "Scientific Discovery as Problem Solving" (various papers with Langley, Bradshaw, Zytkow)
**Significance**: BACON program and others rediscovering scientific laws. Computational approach to philosophy of science.

### "Scientific Discovery: Computational Explorations of the Creative Processes" (1987)
**Authors**: Langley, Simon, Bradshaw, Zytkow
**Publisher**: MIT Press
**Significance**: Comprehensive treatment of machine discovery programs.

## Essays and Collected Works

### "Models of Man" (1957)
**Publisher**: Wiley
**Significance**: Collected essays on behavioral economics and rational choice.

### "The Shape of Automation for Men and Management" (1965)
**Significance**: Essays on technology and employment, automation's social implications.

### "Models of Bounded Rationality" (3 volumes, 1982-1997)
**Publisher**: MIT Press
**Significance**: Collected economic papers spanning his career on bounded rationality.

## Nobel Prize Lecture (1978)
**Title**: "Rational Decision-Making in Business Organizations"
**Significance**: Synthesis of his organizational and economic contributions. Accessible statement of bounded rationality.
