# John L. Hennessy: Ethics and Professional Responsibility

## Academic Entrepreneurship
Hennessy's founding of MIPS raised ethical considerations:

### University-Industry Boundaries
- Managing conflicts between academic and commercial interests
- Ensuring research independence from commercial pressures
- Balancing IP ownership between university and company
- Maintaining student mentorship while running a company

### MIPS handled these by:
- Hennessy taking leave of absence during company formation
- Clear separation of university and company research
- Fair treatment of student contributions
- Eventual full return to academic role

## Leadership Ethics as Stanford President

### Conflicts of Interest
Managing Stanford's relationships with Silicon Valley:
- Google founders were Stanford students
- Many tech executives serve on Stanford boards
- University investments in technology companies
- Hennessy's own industry connections

### Balancing Acts Required
- Supporting entrepreneurship without favoritism
- Accepting donations without compromising academic integrity
- Encouraging industry partnerships while protecting research openness
- Managing personal relationships with industry leaders
