# John L. Hennessy: Key Collaborators

## David Patterson (UC Berkeley, 1980s-Present)
Hennessy's most significant collaboration has been with David Patterson of UC Berkeley, resulting in the RISC revolution and the influential architecture textbook.

### The RISC Collaboration
Though working at competing universities (Stanford and Berkeley), Hennessy and Patterson:
- Independently pursued RISC architectures (MIPS and RISC-I/RISC-II)
- Cross-fertilized ideas through publications and conferences
- Co-authored the definitive computer architecture textbook
- Jointly received the 2017 Turing Award

### The Textbook Partnership
"Computer Architecture: A Quantitative Approach" (1990):
- Combined Hennessy's quantitative rigor with Patterson's teaching expertise
- Created a unified approach to architecture education
- Regularly updated through multiple editions
- Became the standard reference in the field

## MIPS Computer Systems Team (1984-1992)
Hennessy worked with co-founders and early employees:
- **Bob Miller**: Co-founder of MIPS Computer Systems
- **Skip Stritter**: Chief Architect

- **Various engineers and business leaders**
