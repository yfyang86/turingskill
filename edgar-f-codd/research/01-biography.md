# Edgar F. Codd - Biography

## Early Life (1923-1940)

**Birth and Education**
- Born August 19, 1923, in Fortuneswell, Isle of Portland, England
- Grew up in a working-class family on the Dorset coast
- Attended Poole Grammar School on scholarship
- Excelled in mathematics and chemistry

**World War II Service**
- Served as a pilot in the Royal Air Force (1942-1945)
- Flew missions over the Atlantic and Mediterranean
- Commissioned as a lieutenant
- Experience with operational research and logistics planning

## Academic Formation

**Oxford University (1945-1948)**
- Read Mathematics at Exeter College, Oxford
- B.A. in Mathematics and Chemistry (1948)
- Continued interest in logic and mathematical structures

**Move to United States (1948)**
- Emigrated to New York after graduation
- Joined IBM as a programming mathematician
- Initial work on SSEC (Selective Sequence Electronic Calculator)
- Developed expertise in early computer systems

## IBM Career (1948-1984)

**Early IBM Years (1948-1953)**
- Worked on SSEC vacuum tube computer
- Programmed magnetic drum storage systems
- Collaborated with Wallace Eckert at Watson Laboratory
- Developed early database concepts for Census Bureau

**Fortran Development (1957-1960)**
- Contributed to early FORTRAN compiler development
- Worked on optimization techniques
- Began questioning limitations of hierarchical data models

**Doctoral Studies (1961-1965)**
- PhD in Computer Science from University of Michigan (1965)
- Dissertation: "Cellular Automata" - self-replicating structures
- Thesis advisor: John Holland (genetic algorithms pioneer)

**San Jose Research Lab (1968-1984)**
- Moved to IBM Research Laboratory in San Jose, California
- Led database research group
- Published seminal 1970 paper on relational model
- Battled internal IBM politics to promote relational databases

## The Relational Revolution

**The 1970 ACM Paper**
- "A Relational Model of Data for Large Shared Data Banks"
- Published in Communications of the ACM, June 1970
- Introduced relations, tuples, and relational algebra
- Revolutionary concept: data independence from physical storage

**System R Project (1974-1979)**
- IBM's prototype relational database system
- Proved practical feasibility of relational model
- Led by Donald Chamberlin and Raymond Boyce
- Direct ancestor of IBM DB2 and SQL

## Post-IBM Career (1984-2003)

**The Codd & Date Consulting Group**
- Founded consulting firm with Chris Date (1984)
- Advocated for relational database standards
- Consulted for major database vendors
- Published "12 Rules" for relational databases (1985)

**Criticism of Commercial Products**
- Spoke out against "pseudo-relational" databases
- Criticized Oracle and early SQL implementations
- Advocated for true relational purity
- Debated with Larry Ellison publicly

## Personal Life

**Family**
- Married to Sharon Weinberg (second marriage)
- Four children from first marriage
- Maintained English reserve and understated manner

**Character**
- Known for precision in thinking and communication
- Independent thinker, willing to challenge orthodoxy
- Patient but persistent in promoting ideas
- Distrusted marketing claims over technical merit

## Final Years

**Retirement**
- Retired from active consulting in late 1990s
- Continued writing and reviewing database research
- Lived in San Jose area until his death

**Death**
- Died April 18, 2003, in Williams Island, Florida
- Age 79
- Cause: heart failure
- Survived by wife and children

## Legacy Recognition

- ACM Turing Award (1981)
- Inducted into Computing History Museum Hall of Fellows
- IEEE Centennial Medal (1984)
- Numerous patents on database technology
- Foundation of modern data management industry
