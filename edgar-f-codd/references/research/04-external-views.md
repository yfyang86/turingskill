# Edgar F. Codd - External Views and Assessments

## Contemporary Assessments (1970s-1980s)

**IBM Management Perspective**
- Initially skeptical of relational approach
- Preferred IMS hierarchical system
- Gradual acceptance after System R success
- Recognition of strategic importance

**Academic Reception (1970-1975)**
- Charles Bachman: "Too theoretical, impractical"
- Michael Stonebraker: "Revolutionary but needs implementation"
- Jim Gray: "Elegant theory, challenging engineering"
- General consensus: Important but risky

**Industry Response (1975-1985)**
- Oracle: Early commercialization success
- IBM: Reluctant DB2 development
- Ingres: Academic implementation
- Codasyl vendors: Defensive positioning

## Post-Turing Award Perspectives (1981+)

**ACM Recognition**
- "Fundamental contribution to database systems"
- Established database theory as discipline
- Validated academic research direction
- Inspired new generation of researchers

**Database Community Consensus**
- Credited as "Father of Relational Databases"
- Acknowledged 1970 paper as seminal
- Recognized battle against internal IBM resistance
- Appreciated persistence and integrity

**Critical Assessments**
- Some: "Too purist, hindered adoption"
- Others: "SQL deviations necessary pragmatism"
- Debate: "Should have patented core ideas"
- Retrospective: "Correct on fundamentals"

## Modern Evaluations (2000s-Present)

**Historical Significance**

**Technical Assessment**

**Impact Measurement**
- 15,000+ citations of 1970 paper
- Oracle: $300B market cap

- SQL: Most-used programming language

## Comparative Analysis

**Vs. Charles Bachman**
- Bachman: Practical, engineering-focused
- Codd: Theoretical, mathematically-grounded
- Bachman: 1973 Turing Award (network model)
- Outcome: Relational model prevailed

**Vs. Michael Stonebraker**
- Stonebraker: System builder, pragmatic
- Codd: Theorist, purist
- Both: Advocated for relational approach
- Difference: Evolution vs. purity

**Vs. Jim Gray**
- Gray: Transaction processing expert
- Codd: Data model theorist
- Collaboration: Transaction support in relational
- Mutual respect: Both Turing Award winners
