# Edsger W. Dijkstra - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Edsger Wybe Dijkstra
- **Born:** May 11, 1930, Rotterdam, Netherlands
- **Died:** August 6, 2002, Nuenen, Netherlands
- **Nationality:** Dutch
- **Turing Award Year:** 1972

## Education

- **Gymnasium Erasmianum, Rotterdam:** Secondary education
- **Leiden University:**
  - B.S. and M.S. in Mathematics and Physics, 1956
  - Ph.D. in Mathematics and Computer Science, 1959
  - Thesis: "Communication with an Automatic Computer"
  - Worked on Electrologica X1 computer

## Career Timeline

- **1952-1962:** Mathematician, Mathematisch Centrum, Amsterdam
  - 1956: Developed Dijkstra's algorithm
  - 1957: Designed Electrologica X1
  - 1960: ALGOL 60 implementation
- **1962-1984:** Professor of Mathematics, Eindhoven University of Technology
- **1963-1973:** Research Fellow, Burroughs Corporation
  - Part-time consulting role
- **1984-1999:** Schlumberger Centennial Chair, University of Texas at Austin
- **1999-2002:** Professor Emeritus, Austin

## Major Achievements

- **Dijkstra's Algorithm (1956):** Shortest path algorithm
- **Semaphore:** Synchronization primitive
- **Structured Programming:** Pioneer and advocate
- **GOTO Statement Considered Harmful (1968):** Famous letter
- **THE Operating System:** Seminal OS design
- **Weakest Precondition Calculus:** Formal verification

## Personal Characteristics

- Brilliant and uncompromising thinker
- Elegant handwriting (famous for manuscripts)
- Clear and precise writing style
- Controversial and influential opinions
- Passionate about programming elegance
- Strong advocate for formal methods

## Writing Style

- Handwritten manuscripts (EWD series)
- Thousands of technical notes
- Precise and philosophical
- Often polemical
