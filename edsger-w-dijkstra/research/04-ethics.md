# Edsger W. Dijkstra - Ethics and Philosophy

## Programming Philosophy

### Programming as Mathematical Activity
- "Programming is one of the most difficult branches of applied mathematics"
- Mathematical rigor essential
- Elegant solutions preferred
- Rejected ad-hoc approaches

### Elegance and Beauty
- "Elegance is not a dispensable luxury but a matter of life and death"
- Beauty in algorithms and programs
- Simplicity over complexity
- Clear thinking produces clear code

## Views on Computer Science Education

### Teaching Programming
- Separate from teaching language
- Focus on thinking, not syntax
- Mathematical foundations
- Rigorous from the beginning

### Dijkstra's Critique of CS Education
- Criticized "programming as craft" approach
- Wanted CS as mathematical discipline
- Opposed "software engineering" as term
- Believed in formal foundations

## Professional Ethics

### Research Integrity
- Absolute precision in reasoning
- Acknowledgment of errors
- Honest about limitations
- Mathematical honesty

### Writing and Communication
- Handwritten notes (EWD series)
- Clear expression essential
- Polemical when necessary
- Influenced through writing

## Technology and Society

### Computing Responsibility
- Programmers must be responsible
- Correctness matters
- Formal verification important
- Quality over speed

### Views on Computing Profession
- Disliked "computer science" term
- Preferred "computing science"
- Wanted professional standards
- Mathematical foundations required

## Controversial Positions

### GOTO Statement
- "GOTO Statement Considered Harmful" (1968)
- Sparked major controversy
- Eventually widely accepted
- Changed programming practice

### Formal Methods Advocacy
- Strong advocate for formal verification
- Criticized "testing can prove correctness"
- "Testing shows presence, not absence of bugs"
- Industry adoption debated

### Opposition to Certain Trends
- Criticized OOP hype
- Disliked some language features
- Skeptical of AI claims
- Maintained independent thinking

## Personal Values

### Intellectual Independence
- Thought independently

- Maintained principles

### Excellence

- Beauty in work
- Perfectionist tendencies
- Quality over quantity
