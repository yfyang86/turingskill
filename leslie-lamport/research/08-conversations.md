# Leslie Lamport: Conversations and Interviews

## Turing Award Interview (2014)

**On logical clocks**:
> "The insight was simple: we don't need physical time, just a way to establish ordering of events. But this simple idea turned out to be fundamental."

**On Paxos**:
> "I presented it as the Part-Time Parliament because the algorithm seemed obvious to me, and I wanted people to read the paper. The Greek allegory made it more fun."

**On TLA+**:
> "Programmers specify with code, but code is too detailed. TLA+ lets you specify what the system should do, independent of how it does it."

**On LaTeX**:
> "I built LaTeX because I needed it. That it became widely used was gratifying. The key was standing on Knuth's shoulders—TeX made LaTeX possible."

## CACM Interview (2013)

**On formal methods**:
> "People say formal methods are too expensive. But how expensive are the bugs they prevent? Amazon found TLA+ worth the investment."

**On writing**:
> "Clear writing reflects clear thinking. If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough."

## Notable Quotes

On distributed systems:
> "A distributed system is one in which the failure of a computer you didn't even know existed can render your own computer unusable."

On specification:
> "Code is not a specification. Code describes how; specification describes what."

On research:
> "The best research solves real problems elegantly."
