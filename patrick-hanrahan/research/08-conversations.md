# Patrick Hanrahan: Interviews and Dialogues

## On Discovering Computer Graphics

> "I was there right at the beginning, and that turned out to be so very lucky. My eclectic background was the perfect preparation for the field."

Hanrahan describes his journey from nuclear engineering to chemistry, biology, and biophysics before discovering computer graphics at UW-Madison.

## On the Power of Visualization

> "I could have all these abstract thoughts, physics or math or biology, but now I could make them concrete. I could see it."

This revelation about the power of computer graphics to visualize abstract concepts became the driving force of his career.

## On Working at Pixar

> "We were creating tools that enabled artists to do things they could never do before. The shading language was about giving creative control to people who weren't programmers."

Hanrahan emphasizes that RenderMan was designed as an artistic tool, not just a technical achievement.

## On Education and Grades

> "I loved everything I was doing. It was life-changing."

Despite not excelling in high school, Hanrahan found his passion at university. He credits the honors program's flexibility for allowing him to explore diverse interests.

## On Teaching

> "I'm drawn to introductory classes because of their potential to change students' views of the world."

Hanrahan values teaching introductory courses where he can inspire new students, just as he was inspired.

## On Mentorship

> "Every one of them has been amazingly successful, and that's definitely the thing I'm most proud of."

Of mentoring over 50 PhD students, Hanrahan expresses the most pride in their collective success.

## On Curiosity

> "Curiosity and passion determine success."

Hanrahan's mantra emphasizes intrinsic motivation over external validation.

## On Interdisciplinary Work

> "The best representations are easy to understand and easy to manipulate. Expert problem solvers know how to pick the best representations for solving the problem at hand."

From his keynote "On Notation," Hanrahan discusses the importance of representation across disciplines.

## On the Evolution of Graphics

In his Dertouzos Distinguished Lecture at MIT:
> "Shading languages were subsequently extended to run in real-time on graphics processing units, and now shading languages are widely used in game engines. The final step was the realization that the GPU is a data-parallel computer."

Hanrahan traces the path from RenderMan to modern GPU computing.

## On Receiving the Turing Award

> "It was exciting, surprising, and a great honor to be recognized alongside Ed Catmull for our work in computer graphics."

Hanrahan expressed gratitude for the recognition of graphics as a fundamental area of computer science.

## Advice to Students

> "Follow your curiosity. Don't be afraid to explore different fields. The intersections are where the most interesting problems live."

Hanrahan encourages students to embrace interdisciplinary thinking, drawing from his own experience.

## On Tableau's Success

> "We wanted to make data analysis accessible to everyone, not just experts. The visual interface democratizes access to insights."

Hanrahan explains the motivation behind founding Tableau Software.
