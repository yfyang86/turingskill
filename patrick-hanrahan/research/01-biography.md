# Patrick Hanrahan: Biography

## Early Life and Education

Patrick M. Hanrahan was born in 1955 in Green Bay, Wisconsin. Growing up in a working-class family, Hanrahan did not initially excel in the classroom during high school. However, his academic trajectory changed dramatically when he arrived at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

An engineering dean encouraged him to enroll in the honors program based on his entrance exam scores. This flexibility allowed Hanrahan to follow his interests across multiple disciplines:
- Started in nuclear engineering
- Moved to chemistry and biology
- Ultimately discovered computer graphics

He earned his Bachelor's degree in Engineering Physics in 1977 and his Ph.D. in Biophysics in 1985, both from the University of Wisconsin-Madison. His eclectic background proved to be perfect preparation for the emerging field of computer graphics.

## The Graphics Revelation

During his graduate studies, Hanrahan was struck by the revelation that he could feed equations into a computer and it would draw objects like spheres. As he later recalled: "I could have all these abstract thoughts, physics or math or biology, but now I could make them concrete. I could see it."

This insight prompted him to leave his graduate program temporarily to join the computer graphics lab at the New York Institute of Technology (NYIT), one of the pioneering centers for computer graphics research at the time.

## Pixar Years (1986-1989)

In 1986, Hanrahan joined Pixar Animation Studios as a founding employee. At Pixar, he became the driving force behind 3D rendering software:

- **RenderMan Interface**: Chief architect of the specification
- **RenderMan Shading Language**: Designed the procedural shading system
- Contributed to early Pixar productions including *The Magic Egg* (1984), *Tin Toy* (1988), and *Toy Story* (1995)

His shading language enabled the depiction of light and shadow at a level never before achieved in computer graphics, creating photorealistic rendering that transformed filmmaking.

## Academic Career

**Princeton University (1989-1995)**
- Joined the computer science faculty
- Continued research in rendering and visualization

**Stanford University (1995-present)**
- Canon Professor of Computer Science and Electrical Engineering
- Computer Graphics Laboratory
- Mentored over 50 PhD students
- Co-founded the Center for Visualization and Analytics (2005)

## Industry Impact: Tableau Software

In 2003, Hanrahan co-founded Tableau Software, serving as CTO. The company brought data visualization to a broad audience through an easy-to-use interactive visual interface. Salesforce acquired Tableau in 2019 for $15.7 billion.

## Awards and Recognition

- **Three Academy Awards** (1993, 2004, 2014) for technical achievements in computer graphics
- **SIGGRAPH Computer Graphics Achievement Award** (1993)
- **SIGGRAPH Stephen A. Coons Award** (2003)
- **IEEE Visualization Career Award** (2006)
- **Turing Award** (2019, shared with Edwin Catmull)

## Philosophy

Hanrahan believes that "curiosity and passion determine success." He is particularly drawn to introductory classes because of their potential to change students' views of the world. Of his many accomplishments, he says he is most proud of his students: "Every one of them has been amazingly successful."

## Retirement

Hanrahan is now retired from full-time teaching, though he remains active in public speaking, research, and running the Maxwell/Hanrahan Foundation with his wife Delle Maxwell, making unrestricted grants to talented people working in underappreciated but important areas.
