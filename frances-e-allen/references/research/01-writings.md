# Frances E. Allen - Writings and Publications Archive

## Primary Technical Papers

### Foundational Works (1966-1971)

| Year | Title | Publication | Significance |
|------|-------|-------------|--------------|
| 1966 | Program Optimization | Annual Review in Automatic Programming | Most cited work; established data flow analysis |
| 1970 | A Basis for Program Optimization | IFIP Congress (with John Cocke) | Formalized interval analysis |
| 1970 | Control Flow Analysis | ACM SIGPLAN Notices | Systematic CFG analysis methods |
| 1971 | A Catalogue of Optimizing Transformations | IBM Research (with Cocke) | Taxonomy of optimization techniques |

### Parallelization Era (1984-1994)

| Year | Title | Publication | Project |
|------|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1984 | Automatic Program Parallelization | IBM Research Report | PTRAN founding |
| 1986 | An Overview of the PTRAN Analysis System | ICPP Conference | Parallel analysis |
| 1988 | The PTRAN Parallel Compiler System | IBM Research (with Cytron et al.) | PDG introduction |
| 1994 | Compiler Parallelization | Various IBM reports | PTRAN culmination |

### System-Specific Reports

**Stretch/Harvest (1960-1963):**
- Internal IBM reports on multi-language compiler framework
- Documentation of Alpha language compiler
- NSA liaison reports (classified, now presumably declassified)

**ACS Project (1963-1968):**
- Compiling for the ACS-1 (1968)
- Interval analysis algorithms documentation
- Superscalar optimization techniques

**ECS Project (1970s-1980s):**
- PL/I optimization strategies
- Interprocedural analysis frameworks
- Mixed-mode compilation

## Interviews and Oral Histories

### Published Interviews

1. **ACM Video Interview (2006)** - Comprehensive career retrospective
2. **Computer History Museum Interview** - Focus on early career and IBM
3. **Various print interviews (2006-2007)** - Post-Turing Award media coverage

### Characteristics of Available Material

Allen was known for being:
- **Technically precise:** Her writings are clear and rigorous
- **Modest:** Rarely emphasized her own contributions over collaborators'
- **Focused:** Interviews consistently returned to technical content
- **Private:** Limited discussion of personal life or career obstacles

**Source Quality Note:** The written record of Allen's work is primarily technical. Her personal voice and perspectives are relatively rare, appearing mainly in interviews rather than written form. Researchers must infer her views and values from her actions and limited statements.
