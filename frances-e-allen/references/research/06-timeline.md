# Frances E. Allen - Timeline

## Early Life and Education

| Year | Age | Event |
|------|-----|-------|
| 1932 | 0 | Born August 4 in Peru, New York |
| 1940s | 8-16 | Attends local school; inspired by math teacher |
| 1950-1954 | 18-22 | Attends New York State College for Teachers (now SUNY Albany) |
| 1954 | 22 | Earns BA in Mathematics with minor in Physics |
| 1954-1956 | 22-24 | Teaches mathematics at Peru High School |
| 1956 | 24 | Summer courses at Columbia University |
| 1956-1957 | 24-25 | University of Michigan; earns MA in Mathematics |
| 1957 | 25 | Learns programming on IBM 650 from Bernard Galler |

## IBM Career: Early Years (1957-1970)

| Year | Age | Event |
|------|-----|-------|
| 1957 | 25 | Joins IBM Research (July 15) to teach FORTRAN |
| 1957 | 25 | Reads FORTRAN compiler source code by John Backus |
| 1957-1959 | 25-27 | Works on IBM 704 MAD operating system |
| 1959 | 27 | Assigned to Stretch/Harvest compiler project |
| 1962 | 30 | Spends year at NSA installing Harvest system |
| 1962 | 30 | Declines System/360 coordination; returns to IBM Research Yorktown |
| 1963 | 31 | Joins ACS (Advanced Computing Systems) Project with John Cocke |
| 1966 | 34 | Publishes "Program Optimization" - seminal paper |
| 1968 | 36 | ACS project ends; IBM Corporate Award for Algorithms |
| 1970 | 38 | Publishes "A Basis for Program Optimization" with Cocke |

## Middle Career and Sabbatical (1970-1984)

| Year | Age | Event |
|------|-----|-------|
| 1970-1973 | 38-41 | Sabbatical at NYU Courant Institute; meets Jack Schwartz |
| 1971 | 39 | Publishes "A Catalogue of Optimizing Transformations" with Cocke |
| 1972-1978 | 40-46 | Serves on NSF Computer and Information Science Advisory Board |
| 1973-1980 | 41-48 | Leads Experimental Compiler Systems (ECS) project at IBM |
| 1977 | 45 | Sabbatical at Stanford University as Consulting Professor |
| 1980-1985 | 48-53 | Leads development of product compilers at IBM |

| 1960s | Supercomputers; time-sharing emerges | Stretch/Harvest compilers; optimization theory |
| 1970s | Minicomputers; early microprocessors | NYU sabbatical; ECS project; PL/I optimization |
| 1980s | Personal computers; parallel computing emergence | PTRAN project; automatic parallelization |
| 1990s | Internet emergence; commodity computing | BlueGene advisory; continued IBM research |
| 2000s | Multicore processors; mobile computing | Retirement; advocacy; Turing Award |

| 2020s | AI/ML dominance; quantum computing emergence | Posthumous legacy; continued influence |
