# Richard S. Sutton: Biography

## Early Life and Education

Richard Stuart Sutton was born in 1958. He developed interests in psychology, artificial intelligence, and learning systems.

Sutton earned his Bachelor of Arts degree in Psychology from Stanford University in 1978, graduating with honors and distinction. His senior thesis on classical and instrumental conditioning foreshadowed his future work.

He then joined the University of Massachusetts at Amherst for graduate studies in Computer and Information Science, earning his MS in 1980 and PhD in 1984. His doctoral thesis on "Temporal credit assignment in reinforcement learning" established core concepts in the field.

## Early Career (1984-1998)

After his PhD, Sutton held various research positions:
- Postdoctoral research
- Industry research labs
- Continued collaboration with Barto

## AT&T Shannon Laboratory (1998-2002)

Sutton served as Principal Technical Staff Member in the Artificial Intelligence Department at AT&T Shannon Laboratory in Florham Park, New Jersey.

This industry experience informed his understanding of practical AI applications.

## University of Alberta (2002-present)

Sutton joined the University of Alberta as a Professor in Computing Science. He has been instrumental in establishing Alberta as a global center for reinforcement learning research.

### Roles at Alberta
- Professor of Computing Science
- Founder and former director of the Reinforcement Learning and Artificial Intelligence (RLAI) Lab
- Quarter-time Professor (current)

## DeepMind (2017-2023)

Sutton was a Distinguished Research Scientist at DeepMind from 2017 to 2023, bridging academia and industry while continuing his Alberta affiliation.

At DeepMind:
- Applied RL to challenging problems
- Developed new algorithms
- Mentored researchers

## Keen Technologies and Amii (2023-present)

Currently:
- Research Scientist at Keen Technologies (AGI company)
- Chief Scientific Advisor of the Alberta Machine Intelligence Institute (Amii)
- Quarter-time Professor at University of Alberta

## Founding Openmind Research Institute (2023)

Sutton founded the Openmind Research Institute to advance reinforcement learning research and applications.

## Awards and Recognition

- **IJCAI Research Excellence Award** (2021)
- **Lifetime Achievement Award**, Canadian Artificial Intelligence Association (2018)
- **Outstanding Achievement in Research Award**, UMass Amherst (2013)
- **Fellow of the Royal Society** (2021)
- **Turing Award** (2024, shared with Andrew Barto)

## Honors

- Fellow of the Association for the Advancement of Artificial Intelligence (AAAI)
- Fellow of the Royal Society of Canada
- Fellow of the Royal Society of London

## Research Focus

Sutton's work centers on:
- Temporal difference learning
- Reinforcement learning algorithms
- Planning and learning integration
- Psychology of learning
- Artificial general intelligence

## Personal Style

Sutton is known for:
- Algorithmic innovation
- Clear conceptual thinking
- Interdisciplinary approach
- Long-term perspective on AI
- Active industry engagement

## Philosophy

Sutton views reinforcement learning as:
> "The computational theory of intelligence—understanding how agents can learn to behave optimally through interaction with their environment."

He advocates for continued focus on fundamental RL research as the path to AGI.
