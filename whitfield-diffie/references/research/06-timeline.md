# Whitfield Diffie - Timeline

## 1940s-1960s: Early Life and Education

### 1944
- **June 5:** Born in Washington, D.C.
- **Father:** History professor
- **Environment:** Academic upbringing

### 1960s
- **High school:** Developed interest in mathematics
- **Self-study:** Cryptography exploration

### 1965
- **Started at MIT:** Mathematics undergraduate
- **Interests:** Computer science, cryptography

### 1960s (late)
- **Left MIT:** Pursue independent study
- **Context:** Dissatisfied with curriculum
- **Decision:** Self-directed learning

## 1970s: The Breakthrough Decade

### 1972
- **Began serious cryptography study**
- **Method:** Library research, correspondence

### 1973
- **Moved to Stanford:** AI Lab research
- **Fellowship:** Research position
- **Access:** Computing resources

### 1974
- **Met Martin Hellman:** Stanford faculty
- **Context:** First meeting, shared interests
- **Outcome:** Began collaboration

### 1976
- **May:** "New Directions in Cryptography" submitted
- **Publication:** IEEE Transactions on Information Theory
- **Significance:** Public-key cryptography introduced

### 1976
- **Diffie-Hellman key exchange published**
- **Impact:** Foundation of secure communications

### 1977
- **DES analysis paper:** With Hellman
- **Content:** Cryptanalysis critique
- **Impact:** Policy debate

### 1978
- **Joined Bell Northern Research**
- **Location:** Mountain View, California
- **Role:** Security research

## 1980s-1990s: Industry and Policy

### 1980
- **Continued at Bell Northern Research**
- **Work:** Security system design

### 1990
- **Left Bell Northern Research**
- **Next:** Independent consulting

### 1991
- **Joined Sun Microsystems**
- **Title:** Distinguished Engineer
- **Role:** Security strategy

### 1993
- **Clipper Chip opposition:** Public advocacy
- **Coalition:** Built civil liberties alliance
- **Outcome:** Defeated proposal

### 1994
- **Wired "Crypto Rebels" article**
- **Coverage:** Public profile increased

### 1996
- **Co-authored book:** With Susan Landau
- **Title:** "Privacy on the Line"
- **Content:** Wiretapping and encryption policy

### 1998
- **Privacy on the Line published**
- **Publisher:** MIT Press

## 2000s: Continued Influence

### 2008

### 2009
