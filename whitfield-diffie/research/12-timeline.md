# Whitfield Diffie: Timeline

## Early Life and Education (1944-1971)
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1944 | Born June 5, Washington, D.C. | Birth to academic family |
| 1960s | Grows up in intellectual household | Father's historical scholarship influences his interests |
| 1965 | B.S. Mathematics, MIT | Formal education in mathematics |
| 1965 | Decision not to pursue Ph.D. | Sets independent career path |
| Late 1960s | Reads "The Codebreakers" by David Kahn | Develops passion for cryptography |

## Early Career and Cryptography Study (1971-1974)
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| Early 1970s | Works at MITRE Corporation | Applies mathematical skills to computing problems |
| Early 1970s | Stanford AI Laboratory (SAIL) | Exposure to cutting-edge computer science |
| 1972-1974 | Independent study of cryptography | Builds expertise that will lead to breakthrough |
| 1974 | Joins Stanford as research programmer | Position that enables Hellman collaboration |

## The Public-Key Breakthrough (1974-1976)
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1974 | Begins collaboration with Martin Hellman | Partnership that changes cryptography |
| 1975 | Development of key concepts | Working through public-key mathematics |
| 1976 | "New Directions in Cryptography" published | Foundational paper introduces public-key concepts |
| 1976 | Diffie-Hellman key exchange formalized | Practical protocol for secure key establishment |
| 1976 | Digital signature concept articulated | Authentication without shared secrets |

## Early Industry Career (1976-1991)
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1977 | DES critique with Hellman | Questioning government cryptographic standards |
| Late 1970s | Bell Northern Research | Industry position applying cryptographic knowledge |
| 1980s | Various industry positions | Building practical experience |
| 1980s | Continued cryptographic research | Developing expertise in applied cryptography |
| 1990s | Crypto Wars engagement | Advocacy against government restrictions |
| 1991 | Joins Sun Microsystems | Major industry position |

## Sun Microsystems Era (1991-2009)
| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1991 | Becomes Chief Security Officer at Sun | Corporate leadership in security |
| 1990s | Testimony on Clipper Chip | Opposing government backdoor proposals |
| 1990s | Export control opposition | Advocating for cryptographic freedom |
| 1998 | "Privacy on the Line" published | Major book on cryptography policy |
| 2000s | Java security work | Securing widely-used platform |
| 2000s | Continued standards work | Influencing internet security standards |
| 2009 | Leaves Sun Microsystems | End of major corporate chapter |

## Era Summary
| Period | Focus | Key Output |
|--------|-------|------------|

## Concurrent Context
| Year | Diffie | Computing/Security Context |
|------|--------|---------------------------|
| 1976 | "New Directions" published | Early ARPANET; computer security nascent |
| 1977 | RSA developed | Rivest, Shamir, Adleman implement public-key encryption |
| 1991 | Joins Sun | Web beginning; security becoming critical |
| 1994 | SSL deployed | Netscape implements web security |
| 1999 | TLS standardized | Internet security infrastructure matures |
| 2009 | Joins ICANN | DNS security (DNSSEC) development |
| 2013 | Snowden revelations | Mass surveillance revealed; cryptography debates intensify |
| 2015 | Turing Award | Recognition of foundational contributions |

This timeline shows Diffie's progression from independent student to recognized pioneer, with his key breakthrough coming relatively early in his career (age 32), followed by decades of application, advocacy, and policy engagement that extended his influence throughout the development of the internet and modern digital security.
