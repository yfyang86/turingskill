# Leonard M. Adleman - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Leonard Max Adleman
- **Born:** December 31, 1945, San Francisco, California, USA
- **Nationality:** American
- **Turing Award Year:** 2002 (with Rivest and Shamir)
- **Award Citation:** For seminal contributions to cryptography

## Education

- **University of California, Berkeley:** B.A. in Mathematics, 1968
- **University of California, Berkeley:** Ph.D. in Computer Science, 1976
- **Doctoral Advisor:** Manuel Blum

## Career Timeline

- **1976-1980:** Assistant Professor, MIT
- **1980-present:** University of Southern California (USC)
- **1980-1983:** Assistant Professor
- **1983-1985:** Associate Professor
- **1985-present:** Professor
- **Current:** Henry Salvatori Professor of Computer Science and Professor of Molecular Biology

## Major Achievements

- **1977:** Co-invented RSA algorithm with Rivest and Shamir
- **1980s:** Primality testing algorithms
- **1994:** DNA computing pioneer
- **2002:** ACM Turing Award

## Research Focus Areas

- Cryptography and number theory
- Computational complexity
- DNA computing
- Molecular biology
- Algorithms

### Role in Development

### Breakthrough (1994)
- First molecular computation
- Solved Hamiltonian path problem
- Seven-day DNA experiment
- New paradigm proposed

### Significance
- Bridge between CS and biology
- Alternative computation model
- Massive parallelism potential
- Unconventional approach
