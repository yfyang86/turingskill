# Ronald L. Rivest - Interviews and Dialogues

## Historical Accounts

### On RSA Development
"We were trying to solve the key distribution problem. The insight about using one-way functions came gradually, then the mathematics fell into place."

### Collaboration with Shamir and Adleman
"We were all at MIT, bouncing ideas around. Each of us contributed different perspectives—I focused on making it practical."

### On Public-Key Cryptography
"The beauty of public-key is that you can communicate securely with someone you've never met. That changes everything about secure communication."

## Conference Presentations

### CRYPTO Keynotes
On RSA: "The security of RSA relies on the difficulty of factoring. As computers get faster, we need larger keys. The quantum threat is real."

On hash functions: "MD5 was designed for efficiency. We later learned that security requires more than just collision resistance."

### Turing Award Lecture (2002)
"Cryptography is about mathematics, yes, but also about policy, about human rights, about the kind of society we want to live in."

## On Voting Systems

### Election Integrity
"Democracy depends on trust in elections. Cryptography can provide mathematical guarantees that votes are counted correctly."

### ThreeBallot
"Paper ballots with cryptographic verification gives you the best of both worlds—understandable to voters, verifiable mathematically."

## Media Interviews

### On Encryption Policy
"Backdoors in encryption don't just help law enforcement—they help criminals and foreign adversaries too. There's no such thing as a backdoor only good guys can use."

### On Privacy
"Privacy isn't about having something to hide. It's about having control over your personal information."

### On Education
"The CLRS textbook came from our teaching at MIT. We wanted to capture what we had learned about presenting algorithms."

## Personal Reflections

### On Career
"I've been fortunate—great collaborators, great students, important problems to work on."

### On Recognition
"The Turing Award is wonderful, but what matters is that the work has been useful. RSA is used everywhere—that's the real reward."

### On Future
"Post-quantum cryptography is urgent. We need to prepare before quantum computers arrive."

## Late Career Comments

### On Modern Cryptography
"The field has exploded—homomorphic encryption, zero-knowledge proofs, amazing developments."

### On Machine Learning
"I'm exploring how crypto and ML intersect. Privacy-preserving machine learning is fascinating."

### On Voting
"Election security is more important than ever. We need verifiable, trustworthy systems."
