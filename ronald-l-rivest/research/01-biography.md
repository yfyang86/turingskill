# Ronald L. Rivest - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Ronald Linn Rivest
- **Born:** May 6, 1947, Schenectady, New York, USA
- **Nationality:** American
- **Turing Award Year:** 2002 (with Adi Shamir and Leonard Adleman)
- **Award Citation:** For seminal contributions to the theory and applications of cryptography

## Education

- **Yale University:** B.A. in Mathematics, 1969
- **Stanford University:** Ph.D. in Computer Science, 1974
- **Thesis:** "Analysis of Associative Retrieval Algorithms"
- **Advisor:** Robert W. Floyd

## Career Timeline

- **1974-present:** MIT faculty member
- **1974-1980:** Assistant Professor
- **1980-1984:** Associate Professor
- **1984-present:** Professor of Computer Science
- **Current:** Andrew and Erna Viterbi Professor of Computer Science, MIT

## Major Achievements

- **1977:** Co-invented RSA algorithm with Shamir and Adleman
- **1980s:** Developed MD5 hash function
- **1990s:** RSA Data Security co-founder
- **2000s:** Voting system research
- **2002:** ACM Turing Award

## Research Focus Areas

- Cryptography and security
- Algorithm design
- Machine learning
- Voting systems
- Data privacy

## RSA Algorithm

### Development (1977)
- Collaboration with Adi Shamir and Leonard Adleman at MIT
- First practical public-key cryptosystem
- Based on difficulty of factoring large integers
- Published in Communications of the ACM

### Impact
- Foundation of Internet security
- SSL/TLS protocols
- Digital signatures
- Secure communications worldwide

## Honors and Awards

- **2002:** ACM Turing Award (with Shamir and Adleman)
- Fellow of ACM, IEEE, AAAS
- Member, National Academy of Engineering
- Member, National Academy of Sciences
- Multiple honorary degrees
