# Kenneth E. Iverson: Interviews and Dialogues

## On APL's Origins
"I was teaching at Harvard and became frustrated with traditional notation. It was inconsistent and didn't handle arrays well. I started developing my own notation for teaching."

## On Notation
"Notation is a tool of thought. Good notation helps you think; bad notation gets in the way. I wanted notation that was consistent and powerful."

## On APL Characters
"The special characters were a barrier, but they made the notation elegant. With J, we showed the same ideas work in ASCII."

## On Turing Award
"It recognized that notation matters, that programming languages can be mathematical tools. I was honored, especially since APL was initially considered just a notation, not a real language."

## On Teaching
"APL was always meant for teaching as much as for computing. The goal was to make mathematical concepts clear through executable notation."

## On J Language
"J continues the ideas of APL without the special characters. Roger Hui did brilliant work implementing it. I'm pleased it continues as open source."

## Advice
"Follow the notation. If it's clear and consistent, it will lead to good ideas. Don't be afraid to reinvent notation when the old doesn't serve."

His conversations reflected teaching dedication and notational clarity.
