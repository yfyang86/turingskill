# Kenneth E. Iverson: Ethics and Professional Responsibility

## Notation for All
Iverson believed:
- Clear notation serves everyone
- Mathematical tools should be accessible
- Teaching is essential purpose
- Complexity should be in the ideas, not the syntax

## IBM Employment
While at IBM:
- Created APL as practical tool
- Also described competitor architectures (System/360)
- Open publication of notation
- Willingness to leave for IP Sharp (independence)

## Credit and Collaboration
- Shared APL credit with Falkoff
- Acknowledged influences
- Named J after original APL (not himself)
- Generous with ideas

## Open Source Spirit
Though from proprietary era:
- J language freely distributed
- Open to community contributions
- Educational focus
- Accessibility priority

## Overall Assessment
Iverson's ethics:
- Notation for human benefit
- Teaching commitment
- Collaborative credit
- Long-term persistence despite lack of recognition

No ethical controversies; model of principled language design.
