# Kenneth E. Iverson: Biography

## Early Life and Education
Kenneth Eugene Iverson was born on December 17, 1920, on a farm in Camrose, Alberta, Canada. His Norwegian heritage gave him the middle name "Eugene" (originally Eugen). He left school after ninth grade to work on the family farm.

## Military Service and Education
During World War II:
- 1942: Drafted into Canadian Army
- 1943: Transferred to Royal Canadian Air Force
- Served as flight engineer specializing in reconnaissance
- Completed high school via correspondence courses while serving
- Promised to pursue mathematics after the war

## University Education
- 1950: BA in Mathematics and Physics, Queen's University
- 1951: MA in Mathematics, Harvard University
- 1954: PhD in Applied Mathematics, Harvard
- Dissertation supervised by Howard Aiken and Wassily Leontief

## Harvard Years (1950-1960)
At Harvard, Iverson:
- Helped establish first graduate computing course
- Developed notation for teaching (precursor to APL)
- Taught "Automatic Data Processing" with Fred Brooks
- Became frustrated with traditional mathematical notation

## IBM Years (1960-1980)
Joined IBM Research in 1960:
- 1962: Published "A Programming Language" (APL book)
- 1960s: Developed APL with Adin Falkoff
- 1970: Named IBM Fellow
- Used notation to describe IBM System/360 architecture

## APL Development
- 1966: First interactive APL implementation
- Pioneered timesharing and interactive computing
- Special Selectric typeball for APL characters
- APL became popular for mathematical and financial applications

## I.P. Sharp Associates (1980-1987)
Left IBM to join Canadian company:
- Continued APL development
- Created Sharp APL
- Focused on timesharing services

## J Language (1990s-2000s)
With Roger Hui and son Eric Iverson:
- 1990: First J interpreter
- ASCII-based successor to APL
- Evolved Iverson notation
- Open source distribution

## Honors
- 1970: IBM Fellow
- 1975: Harry Goode Award (AFIPS)
- 1979: ACM Turing Award
- 1982: IEEE Computer Pioneer Award
- 1991: National Medal of Technology

## Personal Life
Married Jean Nicholson for 58 years; three children. Died October 19, 2004, in Toronto at age 83.

## Character
Known for:
- Notation as thought tool
- Teaching and clarity
- Mathematical elegance
- Persistence despite initial lack of recognition

## Quote
"A programming language is a tool for expressing human thought, and only secondarily for instructing a computer."
