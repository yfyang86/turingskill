# Kenneth E. Iverson: Key Collaborators

## Fred Brooks (Harvard, 1950s)
- Co-taught computing at Harvard
- Wrote "Automatic Data Processing" chapter
- Brooks later famous for "Mythical Man-Month"
- Both went to IBM

## Adin Falkoff (IBM, 1960-1980)
Primary APL collaborator:
- Co-developed APL at IBM
- Joint papers on APL design
- Shared vision for array programming
- Long-term partnership

## Howard Aiken (Harvard)
PhD supervisor:
- Harvard Computation Laboratory
- Mark I computer
- Influence on Iverson's computing perspective

## Wassily Leontief (Harvard)
PhD supervisor:
- Nobel laureate economist
- Input-output analysis
- Matrix methods influencing Iverson's array thinking

## Roger Hui (1990s-2000s)
J language collaborator:
- Implemented first J interpreter
- Co-designed J language
- Continued Iverson's work after his death

## Eric Iverson (1990s-2000s)
Kenneth's son:
- Business lead for J Software Inc.
- Promoted J language
- Continued family involvement

## I.P. Sharp Associates Colleagues
- Canadian APL community
- Sharp APL development
- Timesharing services team

## IBM Research Colleagues
- Various APL implementers
- System/360 documentation team
- Fellow researchers in Yorktown Heights

## Students and Users
APL's influence through:
- Students taught at Harvard
- APL users in finance and science
- Array programming community

## Collaboration Style
Iverson was known for:
- Partnership on notation/language
- Generous credit sharing
- Long-term relationships (Falkoff, decades)
- Family collaboration (son Eric)

His major works were collaborative, showing how language creation requires partnership.
