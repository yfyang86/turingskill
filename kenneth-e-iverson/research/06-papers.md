# Kenneth E. Iverson: Key Publications

## Foundational Works

### "A Programming Language" (1962)
**Publisher**: Wiley
**Significance**: The APL book; introduced Iverson notation before computer implementation.

### "The Description of Finite Sequential Processes" (1964)
**With Adin Falkoff**
Early formalization of APL concepts.

### "APL\360 User's Manual" (1968)
**With Falkoff**
Documentation of first implementation.

## Turing Award Paper

### "Notation as a Tool of Thought" (1980)
**Publication**: Communications of the ACM (Turing Award lecture)
**Significance**: Articulated philosophy of notation and programming.

## APL Papers

### "APL Language" (1970s-1980s)
Numerous papers with Falkoff on:
- Language design
- Array operations
- Implementation techniques
- Applications

### "The Evolution of APL" (1978)
**With Falkoff**
History and development of the language.

## J Language

### "J Introduction and Dictionary" (1990s)
Documentation of J language.

### "J Phrases" and Educational Materials
Teaching materials for J.

## Archival Sources
- IBM Research archives
- J Software archives
- APL community collections
- ACM Digital Library

Iverson's publications were fewer than some contemporaries but highly influential - quality over quantity.
