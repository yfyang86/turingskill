# John Backus: Interviews and Dialogues

## On FORTRAN Development
"I didn't know what I was doing when I started. I just knew programming was too hard, and we needed to make it easier. The team worked incredibly hard for four years."

## On Being "Lazy"
"Much of my work has come from being lazy. I didn't like writing programs in assembly, so I wanted a better way. Laziness can be a great motivator."

## On BNF
"I developed the notation for describing ALGOL. Peter Naur refined it. It's satisfying that something so simple became so universal."

## On Failure
"I myself have had many failures and I've learned that if you are not failing a lot, you are probably not being as creative as you could be."

## On the Turing Award Lecture
"I wanted to challenge the way we program. The von Neumann style has limitations. Functional programming offers alternatives."

## On FP's Limited Adoption
"FP didn't become mainstream, but it influenced languages that did. Ideas have their own life."

## Advice to Programmers
"Keep trying new things. Don't accept that the way things are is the way they must be. Programming can always be better."

His conversations reflected humility, humor, and persistent innovation.
