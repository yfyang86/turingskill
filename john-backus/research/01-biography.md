# John Backus: Biography

## Early Life and Education Struggles
John Warner Backus was born on December 3, 1924, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. His early education was marked by rebellion and change:
- Expelled from University of Virginia for poor attendance (chemistry)
- Dropped out of medical school after 9 months
- Disliked memorization; wanted to think
- Eventually found his path in mathematics

He served in the US Army during World War II, then completed his education:
- 1949: BS Mathematics, Columbia University
- 1950: MA Mathematics, Columbia University

## Joining IBM (1950)
Backus joined IBM purely by chance. While visiting IBM's headquarters on Madison Avenue, he mentioned his interest in mathematics to a tour guide. After passing an informal math test, he was hired as a programmer despite having no programming experience.

## Speedcoding and the IBM 704 (1950-1953)
Backus worked on:
- Selective Sequence Electronic Calculator (SSEC)
- Speedcoding: First high-level language for IBM computers
- Advocated for floating-point hardware on IBM 704

## FORTRAN Development (1953-1957)
Backus led the team that created FORTRAN:
- Assembled talented team of programmers
- Skepticism about high-level languages competing with assembly
- First compiler shipped in 1957
- Proved high-level languages could be efficient

## ALGOL and BNF (1958-1963)
Backus contributed to international programming language standards:
- Member of ALGOL 58 and ALGOL 60 committees
- Developed Backus-Naur Form (BNF) with Peter Naur
- Formal syntax description method still used today

## IBM Fellow and Research (1963-1991)
In 1963, Backus became an IBM Fellow:
- Freedom to pursue own research
- Worked on mathematical properties of families of sets
- Began functional programming research (1970s)
- Developed FP and FL languages

## Functional Programming and Turing Award (1977)
His Turing Award lecture "Can Programming Be Liberated from the von Neumann Style?" introduced:
- Critique of conventional programming
- FP (Functional Programming) language
- Algebra of programs
- Influenced functional programming movement

## Later Career and Retirement
- 1991: Retired from IBM
- Continued interest in functional programming
- Advocated for programming language innovation

## Honors
- 1963: IBM Fellow
- 1967: W.W. McDowell Award (IEEE)
- 1975: National Medal of Science
- 1977: ACM Turing Award
- 1985: Fellow, American Academy of Arts and Sciences
- 1993: Draper Prize
- 1997: Computer History Museum Fellow
- 2007: Asteroid 6830 Johnbackus named in his honor

## Personal Characteristics
Backus was known for:
- Self-deprecating humor: "Much of my work has come from being lazy"
- Willingness to fail: "You have to generate many ideas only to discover they don't work"
- Innovation: Willingness to challenge established practices
- Persistence: Four years to develop FORTRAN

## Death
Backus died on March 17, 2007, in Ashland, Oregon, at age 82. His legacy lives on in every high-level programming language and in the functional programming paradigm he championed.

## Quote
"I myself have had many failures and I've learned that if you are not failing a lot, you are probably not being as creative as you could be."
