# John Backus: Key Decisions

## 1. Joining IBM (1950)
Chance visit led to career in computing.

## 2. Developing FORTRAN (1953-1957)
Committed to high-level languages despite skepticism.

## 3. Creating BNF (1959-1960)
Formal notation for language syntax.

## 4. IBM Fellowship (1963)
Freedom for long-term research.

## 5. Functional Programming (1970s)
New paradigm at age 50+.

## 6. Turing Award Lecture (1977)
Challenging von Neumann style.

## Pattern
Backus consistently:
- Identified real problems
- Sought better solutions
- Persisted through skepticism
- Accepted failures
- Maintained humility
