# Tony Hoare: Interviews and Dialogues

## On Quicksort
"I developed it in Moscow while working on machine translation. I bet my manager sixpence it would be faster. It was, and I collected."

## On Hoare Logic
"Programs are mathematical objects. They should be subject to mathematical reasoning. {P}C{Q} gives us that rigor."

## On CSP
"Shared memory concurrency is error-prone. CSP's message passing makes synchronization explicit and verifiable."

## On the Billion Dollar Mistake
"I call it my billion-dollar mistake. It seemed easy to implement in 1965. I couldn't have imagined the cost in bugs and crashes over the decades."

## On Simplicity
"There are two ways of constructing a software design: One way is to make it so simple that there are obviously no deficiencies, and the other way is to make it so complicated that there are no obvious deficiencies. The first method is far more difficult."

## On Formal Methods
"Formal verification is not optional for critical systems. We have the mathematics; we should use it."

## On Go Language
"I'm pleased to see CSP ideas in Go's goroutines. Message passing concurrency is finally going mainstream."

## On Career
"I've been fortunate to work on fundamental problems with brilliant colleagues. Oxford and Microsoft Research provided ideal environments."

His conversations reflected rigor, humility, and commitment to correctness.
