# C.A.R. (Tony) Hoare: Biography

## Early Life and Education
Sir Charles Antony Richard Hoare was born January 11, 1934, in Colombo, Ceylon (now Sri Lanka). His father was a colonial civil servant. He was educated in England:
- 1956: BA in Classics and Philosophy ("Greats"), Merton College, Oxford
- 1956: Postgraduate certificate in Statistics, Oxford
- National Service: Royal Navy, learned Russian

## Moscow State University (1958-1960)
Exchange student studying:
- Machine translation of languages
- Worked with Andrey Kolmogorov
- Developed Quicksort algorithm (1959-1960)
- Won 6 pence bet with manager at Elliotts that it was faster

## Elliott Brothers (1960-1968)
Computer manufacturer:
- Led team building ALGOL 60 compiler
- Implemented Quicksort
- Developed early programming expertise

## Queen's University Belfast (1968-1977)
Professor of Computing Science:
- Developed Hoare Logic (1969)
- Axiomatic basis for program correctness
- Seminal work on formal verification

## Oxford University (1977-1999)
Led Programming Research Group:
- Christopher Strachey Chair
- Built Oxford as formal methods center
- Developed CSP (Communicating Sequential Processes, 1978)
- Work on concurrent programming

## Quicksort (1959-1960)
While in Moscow:
- Divide-and-conquer sorting algorithm
- Still widely used today
- Taught in every CS curriculum
- Won bet with manager: 6 pence

## Hoare Logic (1969)
Axiomatic semantics:
- {P} C {Q} notation
- Precondition, command, postcondition
- Foundation for program verification
- Influenced all subsequent verification work

## Communicating Sequential Processes (1978)
Formal language for concurrency:
- Process algebra
- Synchronized communication
- Influenced Occam, Go, Erlang
- Foundation for concurrent programming theory

## Z Notation and Formal Methods
Contributions to:
- Z specification language
- Formal software engineering
- Industrial verification tools
- Unifying Theories of Programming (with He Jifeng)

## Microsoft Research (1999-2026)
Principal Researcher at Cambridge:
- Continued research on concurrency
- Programming language design
- Grand Challenges in Computing
- Mentor to younger researchers

## Honors and Awards
- 1980: ACM Turing Award
- 1982: Fellow of the Royal Society
- 1985: IEEE Emanuel Piore Award
- 1990: Kyoto Prize
- 2000: Knighted for services to computer science
- 2006: Computer History Museum Fellow
- 2011: IEEE John von Neumann Medal

## The Billion Dollar Mistake (2009)
At QCon conference, Hoare apologized for inventing null references:
- "I call it my billion-dollar mistake"
- Null pointer exceptions cost industry dearly
- Honest acknowledgment of design error
- Led to null safety in modern languages

## Death
Sir Tony Hoare died on March 5, 2026, at age 92.

## Character
Known for:
- Intellectual rigor and elegance
- Self-critical honesty
- Commitment to program correctness
- Generosity to colleagues and students
- British understatement and humor

## Quote
"There are two ways of constructing a software design: One way is to make it so simple that there are obviously no deficiencies, and the other way is to make it so complicated that there are no obvious deficiencies."
