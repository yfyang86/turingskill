# Peter Naur - uexternal views

## Overview
Peter Naur was a Danish computer science pioneer and 2005 Turing Award winner. Known for BNF notation and contributions to ALGOL 60.

## Turing Award 2005
- For fundamental contributions to programming language design and the definition of Algol 60

## Key Information
- Born: October 25, 1928, Frederiksberg, Denmark
- Died: January 3, 2016
- PhD: Astronomy, then computing pioneer
- Worked at Regnecentralen (Danish computing center)

## Major Contributions
- BNF (Backus-Naur Form) notation
- ALGOL 60 Report (editor)
- Computing as human activity
- Programming methodology
- Psychology of programming

## Research Focus
- Programming languages
- Software engineering
- Computing philosophy
- Human aspects of computing
