# John Hopcroft - Biography

## Early Life (1939-1961)

**Birth and Education**
- Born October 7, 1939, in Seattle, Washington
- Grew up in Washington state
- Excellent public school education
- Strong early interest in mathematics

**University of Washington**
- B.S. in Electrical Engineering (1961)
- Interest in switching theory
- Introduction to computer science
- Distinguished undergraduate record

**Stanford Graduate Studies**
- M.S. in Electrical Engineering (1962)
- PhD in Electrical Engineering (1964)
- Advisor: Richard Mattson
- Dissertation on finite automata

## Academic Career

**Princeton University (1964-1967)**
- Assistant Professor of Electrical Engineering
- Started research in automata theory
- Colleague: Jeffrey Ullman
- Early graph algorithm work

**Cornell University (1967-present)**
- Joined as Associate Professor (1967)
- Professor of Computer Science (1970)
- Joseph Silbert Dean of Engineering (1994-2001)
- Professor Emeritus, active research

**Administrative Leadership**
- Cornell Engineering Dean (1994-2001)
- NSF Science and Technology Center Director
- International partnerships building
- Fundraising for research

## The Hopcroft-Tarjan Collaboration

**Meeting Tarjan (1960s-1970s)**
- Met at Stanford (Tarjan undergraduate)
- Reconnected at Cornell
- Intense collaboration period (1971-1974)
- Complementary strengths

**Planarity Testing (1974)**
- Linear-time algorithm
- Previous best: O(n³) or worse
- Revolutionary graph algorithm
- Published jointly

**Other Joint Work**
- Biconnected components algorithm
- Strongly connected components
- Depth-first search applications
- Graph algorithm textbook

## Major Research Contributions

**Graph Algorithms**
- Planarity testing (with Tarjan)

**Automata Theory**

**Data Structures**

## Textbook Legacy

**Formal Languages and Their Relation to Automata (1969)**
- Co-author: Jeffrey Ullman
- First comprehensive automata theory textbook
- Multiple editions
- Standard graduate reference

**Introduction to Automata Theory, Languages, and Computation (1979)**
- Co-authors: Rajeev Motwani, Jeffrey Ullman
- Undergraduate standard
- Multiple editions (current: 3rd)
- Millions of copies sold

**Data Structures and Algorithms (1983)**
- Co-authors: Alfred Aho, Jeffrey Ullman
- Comprehensive algorithms text
- Widely used internationally
- Influenced curriculum design
