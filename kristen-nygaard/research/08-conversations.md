# Kristen Nygaard - Interviews and Dialogues

## Historical Accounts

### On Simula Origins
"We wanted to describe systems of interacting processes. The real world is full of objects that maintain their identity over time. Why shouldn't programs model this?"

### Collaboration with Dahl
"Ole-Johan was the technical genius. I brought the application perspective and the vision. Together we created something neither could have done alone."

### On Object-Oriented Programming
"The beauty of objects is that they mirror how we think about the world. This isn't just technical—it's cognitive, it's human."

## Conference Presentations

### HOPL Conference (1978)
On Simula development: "The process concept came from watching real systems—factories, organizations, natural systems. We needed to capture that in programs."

On design philosophy: "Technical elegance matters, but so does human utility. A language must serve its users."

### OOPSLA Keynotes
On OOP evolution: "I'm proud of what OOP has become, but concerned it's sometimes used without understanding the human dimension."

On future directions: "The next challenge is making technology serve democracy, not undermine it."

## Political Speeches

### Union Addresses
On researcher rights: "Knowledge workers must organize. Our expertise gives us responsibility—and power to effect change."

On technology policy: "Scientists cannot hide in labs. We must engage with how our work affects society."

### Campaign Events
On Liberal Party platform: "Technology should serve human flourishing, not just efficiency and profit."

On democratic participation: "Every citizen deserves voice in technological decisions that affect their lives."

## Participatory Design Sessions

### With Workers
"You know your work better than any designer. Our job is to give you tools to express that knowledge."

### On User Involvement
"Users aren't sources of requirements—they're partners in design. Their knowledge is essential."

## Media Interviews

### On Norwegian Computing
"Norway can be a model for humane technology. Small countries can lead in values, not just scale."

### On International Recognition
"Awards are nice, but what matters is whether technology serves people. That's the real measure."

## Personal Reflections

### On Career
"I've been fortunate—technical work with Dahl, social engagement with unions and politics, teaching wonderful students."

### On Politics
"Some say scientists shouldn't be political. I say we have a duty to be political—with integrity."

### On Dahl Partnership
"Ole-Johan and I were different—he quiet and precise, me outgoing and visionary. Perfect complement."

## Late Career Comments

### On Modern OOP
"The concepts spread further than imagined. But I worry about complexity—Simula was meant to simplify."

### On Technology Today
"We have more computing power than ever, but have we made society better? That's the question."

### On Legacy
"If I've contributed to both good technical ideas and social responsibility in computing, I'm satisfied."
