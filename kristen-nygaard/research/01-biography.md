# Kristen Nygaard - Biography

## Personal Information

- **Full Name:** Kristen Nygaard
- **Born:** August 27, 1926, Oslo, Norway
- **Died:** August 10, 2002, Oslo, Norway
- **Nationality:** Norwegian
- **Turing Award Year:** 2001 (with Ole-Johan Dahl)
- **Award Citation:** For ideas fundamental to the emergence of object-oriented programming

## Education

- **University of Oslo:** M.S. in Mathematics, 1956
- Focus on numerical analysis and statistics
- Early interest in operations research

## Career Timeline

- **1948-1957:** Junior Researcher, Norwegian Defense Research Establishment (NDRE)
- **1957-1960:** Operations Research, Norwegian Operational Research Society
- **1960-1963:** Head of Research, NDRE
- **1963-1984:** Director, Norwegian Computing Center (NCC)
- **1975-1976:** Visiting Professor, University of Aarhus, Denmark
- **1977-1984:** Professor, University of Oslo (part-time)
- **1984-1996:** Professor of Computer Science, University of Oslo

## Major Achievements

- **1961-1967:** Co-developed Simula I and Simula 67 with Ole-Johan Dahl
- **1960s:** Pioneered object-oriented programming concepts
- **1970s-1980s:** Political activism for social responsibility in computing
- **1980s-1990s:** Research on system development and participatory design

## Research Focus Areas

- Object-oriented programming
- Discrete event simulation
- Systems development methodology
- Participatory design
- Technology and society

## Honors and Awards

- **1984:** Doctor Honoris Causa, Lund University, Sweden
- **1986:** Doctor Honoris Causa, Aalborg University, Denmark
- **1990:** Doctor Honoris Causa, Oslo, Norway
- **1999:** Doctor Honoris Causa, Aarhus, Denmark
- **2000:** IEEE John von Neumann Medal (with Dahl)
- **2001:** ACM Turing Award (with Dahl)

## Political and Social Activism

### Norwegian Politics
- Active in Norwegian Liberal Party (Venstre)
- Chair of party's technology policy committee
- Candidate for parliament

### Union Leadership
- Leader of Norwegian Association of Researchers
- Advocate for researcher rights
- Science policy influence

## Personal Characteristics

- Charismatic and outgoing
- Passionate about social issues
- Bridge between technical and social
- Engaging speaker and writer
- Committed to public service

## Later Years

- Continued teaching until retirement in 1996
- Remained politically active
- Died in 2002, shortly before Turing Award ceremony
- Award accepted by family
