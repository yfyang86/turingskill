# Marvin Minsky - Key Decisions

## Career Decisions

### Building SNARC (1951)
- Undergraduate project at Harvard
- Built first neural network learning machine
- Demonstrated practical AI possible
- Launched his AI career

### PhD at Princeton (1954)
- Chose mathematics over physics
- Thesis on neural nets and brain modeling
- Interdisciplinary approach established
- Foundation for future work

### Moving to MIT (1957)
- Left Harvard Society of Fellows
- Joined MIT to build AI program
- Co-founded AI Project with McCarthy
- Established MIT as AI center

### Dartmouth Conference (1956)
- Co-organized founding AI conference
- Committed to establishing AI as field
- Invited key researchers
- Defined research agenda

## Technical Decisions

### Perceptrons Book (1969)
- Analyzed neural network limitations
- Co-authored with Papert
- Shaped AI direction for decades
- Risked alienating connectionists

### Frame Theory (1975)
- Developed knowledge representation
- Influenced object-oriented programming
- Alternative to semantic networks
- Major KR paradigm

### Society of Mind Theory
- Focused on distributed intelligence
- Challenged unified self concept
- Interdisciplinary approach
- Book took years to write

### Logo Development
- Collaborated with Papert on education
- Constructionist learning approach
- Focus on children and learning
- Long-term educational impact
