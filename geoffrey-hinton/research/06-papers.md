# Geoffrey Hinton: Key Publications

## Foundational Works

### Learning representations by back-propagating errors (1986)
**Rumelhart, D.E., Hinton, G.E., & Williams, R.J.**
*Nature*, 323(6088), 533-536

The paper that brought backpropagation to mainstream attention and launched the connectionist revolution.

**Key contributions:**
- Clear derivation of backpropagation algorithm
- Demonstrations across multiple problem domains
- Established neural networks as practical learning systems
- Created the foundation for all subsequent deep learning

**Impact:** Over 40,000 citations; foundational to modern AI

---

### A fast learning algorithm for deep belief nets (2006)
**Hinton, G.E., Osindero, S., & Teh, Y.W.**
*Neural Computation*, 18(7), 1527-1554

The paper that launched the modern deep learning era by solving how to train deep networks.

**Key contributions:**
- Restricted Boltzmann Machine pretraining
- Layer-wise unsupervised learning
- Demonstration of effective deep architectures
- State-of-the-art MNIST results

**Impact:** Revived neural network research; direct precursor to ImageNet breakthrough

---

### Reducing the dimensionality of data with neural networks (2006)
**Hinton, G.E., & Salakhutdinov, R.R.**
*Science*, 313(5786), 504-507

Demonstrated neural networks for dimensionality reduction, showing deep learning's versatility.

**Key contributions:**
- Autoencoder architectures
- Nonlinear dimensionality reduction
- Unsupervised feature learning
- Applications to document retrieval and visualization

---

## Computer Vision Revolution

### ImageNet Classification with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (2012)
**Krizhevsky, A., Sutskever, I., & Hinton, G.E.**
*NIPS 2012*

The AlexNet paper that proved deep learning at scale and transformed computer vision.

**Key contributions:**
- Large-scale convolutional networks
- GPU acceleration for training
- ReLU activation functions
- Dropout regularization
- Data augmentation strategies

**Impact:** Won ImageNet 2012 by 10 percentage points; triggered industry-wide AI investment

---

## Probabilistic Models

### Boltzmann Machines: Constraint Satisfaction Networks that Learn (1985)
**Hinton, G.E., & Sejnowski, T.J.**
*Cognitive Science*, 9(1), 147-169

Introduced energy-based models and unsupervised learning of distributed representations.

**Key contributions:**
- Stochastic neural networks
- Energy-based learning
- Unsupervised structure discovery
- Bridge between statistical physics and AI

---

### Training products of experts by minimizing contrastive divergence (2002)
**Hinton, G.E.**
*Neural Computation*, 14(8), 1771-1800

Made Boltzmann machines practical by introducing contrastive divergence.

**Key contributions:**
- Efficient approximation to maximum likelihood
- Enabled training of large RBMs
- Foundation for deep belief networks
- Biologically plausible learning rule

---

## Capsule Networks

### Dynamic Routing Between Capsules (2017)
**Sabour, S., Frosst, N., & Hinton, G.E.**
*NIPS 2017*

Hinton's attempt to improve upon convolutional networks with explicit pose representation.

**Key contributions:**
- Capsule architectures
- Dynamic routing by agreement
- Equivariance to transformations
- Part-whole relationships

### Matrix Capsules with EM Routing (2018)
**Hinton, G.E., Sabour, S., & Frosst, N.**
*ICLR 2018*

Refined capsule approach using Expectation-Maximization.

---

## Alternative Architectures

### The Forward-Forward Algorithm (2022)
**Hinton, G.E.**
*arXiv:2212.13345*

Proposed replacement for backpropagation using two forward passes.

**Key contributions:**
- Local learning rules
- No need to store activations
- More biologically plausible
- Potential for analog hardware

### How to Represent Part-Whole Hierarchies in a Neural Network (2021)
**Hinton, G.E.**
*arXiv:2102.12627*

GLOM architecture proposal for explicit hierarchical representation.

---

## Edited Volumes

### Parallel Distributed Processing (1986)
**Rumelhart, D.E., McClelland, J.L., & the PDP Research Group**

Two-volume edited work that established connectionism as a paradigm. Hinton contributed multiple chapters and helped shape the volumes' direction.

## Publication Patterns

Hinton's publication strategy reveals his priorities:
1. **Quality over quantity**: Fewer papers than many contemporaries
2. **Impactful venues**: Nature, Science, NIPS/NeurIPS, ICML
3. **Collaborative**: Most papers with students or colleagues
4. **Long-term focus**: Returns to themes over decades
5. **Late-career innovation**: Continues proposing new approaches (Capsules, Forward-Forward, GLOM)
